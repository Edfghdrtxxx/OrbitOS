## Daily Levers — The Quests

> 3 actions the person you're becoming would simply do every day.
> These are not aspirational. They are the minimum viable identity expression.

| # | Quest | What it looks like |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Thesis block** (primary) | 2–4 focused hours: model analysis, writing, or figures — not planning |
| 2 | **One Japan-track task** | Fixed weekly rotation — Mon: Japanese / Tue: Physics problem set / Wed: TOEFL listening / Thu: Japanese / Fri: outreach or admin — 30–60 min. *Japanese slots are low priority; swap for exam prep or TOEFL if behind schedule.* |
| 3 | **One connector** | Update a progress log, send one email, or capture one insight in the daily note |

> If you only do Quest 1 every day, you're still winning.
> Quest 2 rotation is a default, not a rule — swap days freely, but ensure each track gets time across the week.

- **Financial buffer is non-negotiable** — if direct PhD entry succeeds, RIKEN JRA covers living costs from D1. If the Kenkyusei fallback activates, the self-funded year costs ~2.5–2.8M JPY. Savings must clear this threshold before departure in either case. Do not commit to the October 2027 timeline without confirmed liquidity. Money is not the goal; its absence collapses the path.

## The Cybernetics Check (Weekly)

> From Koe Section V: act → sense → compare → iterate.

Every Sunday (or in your weekly daily note), answer:
1. Did I do the thesis block daily? How many days?
2. Did I do one Japan-track task daily?
3. What specific progress happened in the thesis this week?
4. Am I moving toward the anti-vision or the vision?
5. What's the one change to make next week?
