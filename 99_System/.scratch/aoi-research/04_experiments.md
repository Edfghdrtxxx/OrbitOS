## Beamlines and Separators

### BigRIPS Fragment Separator
- Aoi is listed as admin of the BigRIPS web page (ribf.riken.jp/~aoi/RIBF/)
- BigRIPS is the primary in-flight fragment separator at RIBF
- Produces secondary RI beams via projectile fragmentation or in-flight fission of uranium
- Particle identification via TOF, magnetic rigidity (Brho), and energy loss (Delta-E) => tagged RI beams
- Used in most of Aoi's in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy experiments
- Source: [BigRIPS NIM paper (PDF)](https://ribf.riken.jp/~aoi/RIBF/reference/BigRIPS_NIM_published.pdf)

### ZeroDegree Spectrometer
- Downstream of BigRIPS for particle identification of reaction residues
- Used in conjunction with DALI2 for in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy experiments
- Example: First spectroscopy of 40Mg (PRL 122, 052501, 2019)

### OEDO-SHARAQ System (CNS)
- Aoi is a named collaborator on the OEDO-SHARAQ collaboration
- **SHARAQ spectrometer**: High-resolution spectrometer (p/Delta-p = 1/15000), completed 2009, CNS main device
- **OEDO**: Energy-degrading beamline with RF deflector; decelerates RI beams to 10-50 AMeV while maintaining beam quality; completed March 2017
- Combined system enables: missing-mass spectroscopy, low-energy RI production, surrogate reactions
- Other CNS members in collaboration: N. Imai, K. Yako, H. Yamaguchi, N. Kitamura, Y. Yamamoto, S. Bae, J.T. Li
- Sources:
  - [OEDO-SHARAQ collaboration](https://oedo-sharaq.cns.s.u-tokyo.ac.jp/collaboration)
  - [OEDO-SHARAQ homepage](https://oedo-sharaq.cns.s.u-tokyo.ac.jp/)
  - [OEDO-SHARAQ system paper (NIM B, 2023)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0168583X23001477)

### RIPS (older separator)
- Earlier-generation fragment separator at RIKEN (predecessor to BigRIPS)
- Used in Aoi's early experiments on neutron drip-line nuclei (e.g., N=16 magic number discovery, 2000)

## Detector Systems

### DALI2 / DALI2+ (NaI(Tl) Gamma-ray Array)
- Aoi is a co-author on the DALI2 instrument paper
- 186 NaI(Tl) scintillator detectors
- Covers polar angles ~15-160 degrees with ~6 degree angular resolution (FWHM)
- ~10% energy resolution, ~20% full-energy photopeak efficiency for 1 MeV gammas from fast nuclei
- Primary detector for in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy at RIBF with fast beams
- Used in many of Aoi's key experiments (32Ne, 40Mg, 42Si, island of inversion studies)
- Source: [DALI2 paper (NIM A, 2014)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0168900214008419), [arXiv:1403.5349](https://arxiv.org/abs/1403.5349)

### GRAPE (Gamma-Ray detector Array with Position and Energy sensitivity)
- Position-sensitive germanium detector array developed at CNS
- Corrects Doppler shift in measurements from moving beam experiments
- Measures de-excitation gamma rays in MeV range
- Used in combination with SHARAQ spectrometer for high-resolution spectroscopy
- Source: [SHARAQ and GRAPE paper (NIM B)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0168583X08006927)

### Active Targets (CAT, CAT-M, CAT-S)
- Developed at CNS using Gas Electron Multiplier (GEM) technology (developed in collaboration with RIKEN)
- **CNS GEM-TPC**: Thick-GEM technology surpassing original CERN designs; higher gain at lower voltage; adopted in ALICE TPC upgrade at CERN
- **CAT-M**: Active target for measurements with high-intensity beams (>100 kcps); uses low-pressure gaseous TPC for recoil particle detection at forward angles
- Enables 3D imaging of nuclear reactions
- High rate capability (handles ~1 million particles/second)
- Uses low-pressure drift chamber detectors and high-speed timing measurement devices
- Source: [CNS Active Target page](https://www.cns.s.u-tokyo.ac.jp/en/research/equipments/cat/)

### TiNA (Silicon Strip + CsI(Tl) Array)
- Recoil particle detector array at CNS
- Silicon strip detectors + CsI(Tl) scintillators (1000+ channels)
- Energy resolution below 100 keV
- Used for neutron capture reaction studies

### Beamline Detectors (CNS)
- Low-pressure MWDC: fast-response for ion identification
- Diamond detectors: radiation-hard
- SR-PPAC: beam monitoring

### PANDORA (Neutron Counter Array)
- Detects neutrons >100 keV
- Plastic scintillator with pulse-shape discrimination
- Real-time data analysis capability

## Experimental Techniques

### In-beam Gamma-ray Spectroscopy (Primary Technique)
- Core technique of Aoi's research program
- Fast RI beams (typically 200-300 MeV/nucleon) from BigRIPS
- Gamma rays detected by DALI2 (or HiCARI/GRAPE) surrounding secondary target
- Reaction residues identified by ZeroDegree spectrometer
- Used to measure: excitation energies of 2+ states, B(E2) transition strengths, level schemes
- Applied to neutron-rich nuclei in the island of inversion and beyond

### Proton Inelastic Scattering (Inverse Kinematics)
- RI beam on liquid hydrogen target or thick proton-rich target
- Measures deformation parameters via angle-integrated cross sections
- Distorted-wave calculations used to extract deformation lengths
- Applied to nuclei like 28,30Ne, 32,34,36Mg, 16C
- Technique yields information on both proton and neutron contributions to collectivity

### Coulomb-Nuclear Interference Method
- Applied to heavy target (e.g., 208Pb) scattering
- Disentangles proton and neutron transition matrix elements (Mn/Mp ratio)
- Demonstrated in 16C study showing neutron-dominant quadrupole collectivity

### Knockout Reactions
- One-proton and one-neutron removal reactions
- Population of excited states measured via gamma-ray detection
- Momentum distributions analyzed for orbital angular momentum assignment
- Example: 33Mg studied via knockout from 34Mg and 34Al

### Projectile Fragmentation
- Secondary beam production via fragmentation of primary beam on production target
- Also used as reaction mechanism for spectroscopy (e.g., 32Ne from 48Ca fragmentation)

### Surrogate Reactions (via OEDO)
- (d,p) transfer reactions with decelerated RI beams
- Determines neutron capture rates for r-process nucleosynthesis studies
- SSD-CsI array + SHARAQ spectrometer
- Example: (d,p) on 79Se and 130Sn (Imai, spring 2022)

### Missing-Mass Spectroscopy
- High-resolution spectrometry using SHARAQ
- Studies tetra-neutron systems and other exotic states

### Lifetime Measurements
- Recoil shadow method for measuring excited-state lifetimes (used in 16C B(E2) measurement)
- Doppler-shift techniques with HiCARI Ge array

## Key Experiments and Physics Results

### Discovery of N=16 Magic Number (2000)
- **Landmark result**: Established new magic number N=16 near the neutron drip line
- Studied neutron drip line up to fluorine via projectile fragmentation
- 94.1 AMeV 40Ar beam on RIPS separator at RIKEN
- First observation of 31F; evidence for particle instability of 24,25N, 27,28O, 30F
- Published: Phys. Rev. Lett. (2000)
- Source: [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10990977/)

### Anomalously Hindered B(E2) in 16C (2004)
- **Landmark result**: Proton-neutron decoupling in exotic nucleus
- Led by N. Imai with N. Aoi as co-author
- Measured lifetime of 2+_1 state: 77 +/- 14(stat) +/- 19(syst) ps
- B(E2; 2+_1 -> 0+) = 0.63 e^2 fm^4 = 0.26 W.u. (anomalously small)
- Technique: recoil shadow method on inelastically scattered 16C
- Published: Phys. Rev. Lett. 92, 062501 (2004)
- Sources: [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14995232/), [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/8680987_Anomalously_hindered_E2_strength_BE221--0_in_16C)

### Neutron-Dominant Quadrupole Collectivity in 16C
- Follow-up study using Coulomb-nuclear interference method (208Pb+16C)
- Proton transition strength extremely small (0.28 +/- 0.06 W.u.)
- Mn/Mp ratio = 7.6 +/- 1.7 (unexpectedly large)
- 2+_1 state is nearly pure valence neutron excitation
- Source: [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/235445489_Neutron-dominant_quadrupole_collective_motion_in_16C)

### Spectroscopy of 32Ne and the Island of Inversion (2009)
- First spectroscopic study of N=22 nucleus 32Ne at RIBF
- 226 MeV/u 32Ne beam on carbon target (inelastic scattering) + proton removal from 33Na (245 MeV/u)
- Single gamma line at 722(9) keV assigned to 2+_1 -> 0+ transition
- Demonstrates island of inversion extends to at least N=22 for Ne isotopes
- Published: Phys. Rev. Lett. 103, 032501 (2009)
- Source: [APS](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.103.032501), [arXiv:0906.3775](https://arxiv.org/abs/0906.3775)

### In-beam Gamma-ray Spectroscopy of 38,40,42Si (N=28 region)
- Studied shell quenching at N=28 in silicon isotopes
- 42Si: gamma line at 742(8) keV confirms 2+ state; 4+_1 at 2173(14) keV
- E(4+)/E(2+) ratio = 2.93(5) indicates well-developed deformation in 42Si
- Multi-nucleon removal reactions from 40S and 44S beams at RIBF
- Source: [HAL](https://hal.science/in2p3-00989741)

### Island of Inversion: Quadrupole Collectivity in Ne, Na, Mg
- Proton inelastic scattering on 28,30Ne and 32,34,36Mg (liquid hydrogen target)
- In-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy in inverse kinematics
- Deformation lengths extracted via distorted-wave calculations
- In-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy of 34,36,38Mg: merging N=20 and N=28 shell quenching
- Published: Phys. Rev. Lett. (2013, on 34,36,38Mg)
- Source: [PubMed 24313483](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24313483/)

### First Spectroscopy of 40Mg (Near Drip Line, 2019)
- First study of excited states in 40Mg (N=28, near neutron drip line)
- One-proton removal from 41Al
- Two gamma-ray transitions observed
- Unexpected excitation spectrum compared to systematics and theory
- Used DALI2 + ZeroDegree spectrometer at RIBF
- Published: Phys. Rev. Lett. 122, 052501 (2019)
- Source: [APS](https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevLett.122.052501)

### Spectroscopy of 33Mg via Knockout Reactions
- One-neutron removal from 34Mg and one-proton removal from 34Al
- Resolved parity controversy of 33Mg ground state (negative parity)
- Momentum distribution analysis
- Published: Phys. Rev. C 103, 064318 (2021)
- Source: [APS](https://journals.aps.org/prc/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevC.103.064318)

### Large Collectivity of 34Mg (2001)
- Measured large B(E2) value for 34Mg via Coulomb excitation
- Evidence for breakdown of N=20 shell closure
- Source: [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0370269301012448)
