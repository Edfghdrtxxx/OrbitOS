## Topic 1: Island of Inversion (N=20 Region)

### Physical Phenomenon
The "island of inversion" is a region of the nuclear chart around N=20 (neutron number 20) and Z=10-12 (Ne, Na, Mg) where nuclei exhibit unexpected ground-state deformation rather than the spherical shapes predicted by the traditional shell model. In these nuclei, intruder configurations (where neutrons are promoted from the sd-shell across the N=20 gap into the fp-shell) become energetically favored over the normal closed-shell configuration.

### Why It Matters
- The N=20 shell closure is one of the classic magic numbers in nuclear physics. Its breakdown in neutron-rich nuclei fundamentally challenges our understanding of nuclear forces far from stability.
- Understanding shell evolution is critical for predicting properties of nuclei relevant to astrophysical r-process nucleosynthesis.
- The island of inversion serves as a benchmark for nuclear many-body theories (shell model, density functional theory).

### Theoretical Framework
- **Shell model with cross-shell excitations**: The USD (sd-shell) interaction extended to include fp-shell intruder configurations. Calculations by Warburton, Becker, Brown (1990) and Caurier, Nowacki, Poves predicted the inversion of normal and intruder configurations.
- **Key mechanism**: The monopole component of the proton-neutron interaction (tensor force) reduces the N=20 gap as protons are removed from the d5/2 orbital, causing the f7/2 and p3/2 intruder orbitals to drop in energy.
- **Observable signatures**: Low E(2+) excitation energies, large B(E2) values, large E(4+)/E(2+) ratios approaching the rotational limit of 3.33.

### Aoi's Contributions

#### 32Ne: Extension of the Island to N=22
- **Paper**: P. Doornenbal, H. Scheit, **N. Aoi** (3rd author), et al., "Spectroscopy of 32Ne and the Island of Inversion," *Phys. Rev. Lett.* **103**, 032501 (2009). [arXiv:0906.3775](https://arxiv.org/abs/0906.3775)
- **Result**: First spectroscopic study of 32Ne at RIKEN RIBF. A gamma-ray line at **722(9) keV** was observed via both inelastic scattering (226 MeV/u 32Ne on C) and proton removal from 33Na (245 MeV/u).
- **Significance**: The low E(2+) = 722 keV demonstrates the island of inversion extends to at least N=22 for Ne isotopes, confirmed by shell model calculations.

#### 34Mg: First Spectroscopy, Strong Deformation
- **Paper**: N. Aoi et al., "Gamma-ray spectroscopy of 34Mg via RI beam fragmentation," *Nucl. Phys. A* **701**, 133c (2002). [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0375947401016244)
- **Result**: First determination of low-lying states in 34Mg using in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy with two-step fragmentation of a 36Si beam at 38 MeV/u on 9Be target.
- **Key observables**: Two gamma-lines at **660(10) keV** and **1460(20) keV**, tentatively assigned to 2+->0+ and 4+->2+ transitions.
- **E(4+)/E(2+) ratio**: ~3.2, consistent with a well-deformed rotor.

#### Proton Inelastic Scattering on 32Mg
- **Paper**: Research involving N. Aoi on proton inelastic scattering of 32Mg at 56 MeV/nucleon on a liquid hydrogen target, studying higher excited states.
- **Technique**: In inverse kinematics, measuring de-excitation gamma-rays and scattering angles to extract angle-integrated and differential cross sections.
- **Result**: 32Mg exhibits the largest quadrupole collectivity among the neutron-rich N=20 isotones.
- Source: [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/231014450_Proton_inelastic_scattering_on_32Mg)

#### Quadrupole Collectivity in Ne and Mg Isotopes (Island of Inversion)
- Aoi contributed to systematic studies of quadrupole collectivity in 28,30Ne and 32,34,36Mg via proton inelastic scattering on liquid hydrogen targets by in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy in inverse kinematics.
- Angle-integrated cross sections to the first 2+ states were determined; deformation lengths extracted by comparison with distorted-wave (DWBA) calculations.
- Source: [CNS UTokyo draft](https://www.cns.s.u-tokyo.ac.jp/~mitimasa/r361n/draft/revise.1/draft_r361n.pdf)

## Topic 2: Merging of N=20 and N=28 Islands of Inversion

### Physical Phenomenon
In the neutron-rich Mg isotopic chain, the traditional shell closures at N=20 and N=28 both break down, creating a continuous region of deformation that merges the two "islands of inversion" into one large deformed region spanning N=19 to N=30.

### Why It Matters
- This challenges the view that magic numbers provide isolated islands of stability/sphericity.
- It reveals how nuclear forces drive collective behavior across a wide swath of the nuclear chart.
- Important for understanding the evolution of shell structure toward the neutron drip line.

### Aoi's Contributions

#### 34,36,38Mg: In-beam Gamma-ray Spectroscopy
- **Paper**: P. Doornenbal, H. Scheit, **N. Aoi**, et al., "In-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy of 34,36,38Mg: Merging the N=20 and N=28 shell quenching," *Phys. Rev. Lett.* **111**, 212502 (2013). [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24313483/)
- **Experiment**: Neutron-rich N=22, 24, 26 Mg isotopes studied via in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy at RIKEN RIBF following secondary fragmentation reactions on a C target at ~200 MeV/nucleon.
- **Key results**:
  - E(4+)/E(2+) ratios for 34,36,38Mg: **3.14(5), 3.07(5), 3.07(5)** — nearly identical and close to the rigid rotor limit (3.33).
  - These ratios indicate persistent strong deformation from N=22 to N=26.
  - Emergence of a large area of deformation extending from N=20 to N=28 shell quenching — the two islands of inversion **merge** in the Mg chain.
- Source: [Phys. Rev. Lett. 111, 212502](https://journals.aps.org/prl/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevLett.111.212502)

### Theoretical Context
- The rotational E(4+)/E(2+) ratio for a perfect rotor is 10/3 = 3.33; vibrational limit is 2.0.
- Values near 3.0–3.1 indicate well-developed rotational behavior, consistent with large prolate deformation.
- Shell model calculations (SDPF-M, SDPF-U interactions) predict intruder configurations dominate across this region.

## Topic 3: N=28 Shell Quenching — Deformation in 42Si

### Physical Phenomenon
The N=28 magic number, which corresponds to a filled f7/2 neutron orbital, breaks down in neutron-rich nuclei around Z=14 (silicon). The nucleus 42Si (Z=14, N=28) exhibits strong deformation despite having a nominally closed neutron shell.

### Aoi's Contributions

#### Well-Developed Deformation in 42Si
- **Paper**: S. Takeuchi, M. Matsushita, **N. Aoi** (3rd author), et al., "Well-developed deformation in 42Si," *Phys. Rev. Lett.* (2012). [arXiv:1207.6191](https://arxiv.org/abs/1207.6191)
- **Experiment**: In-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy with multi-nucleon removal reactions using intense 40S and 44S beams at RIKEN RIBF. Gamma-gamma coincidence measurements were performed.
- **Key results**:
  - E(2+) for 42Si: **742(8) keV** (confirmed earlier measurement)
  - E(4+) for 42Si: **2173(14) keV** (first identification)
  - **E(4+)/E(2+) = 2.93(5)** — indicates well-developed deformation
  - For 38Si: E(4+)/E(2+) = 2.09(5); for 40Si: 2.56(5)
  - Shows **rapid deformation development** from N=24 to N=28 in Si isotopes.
- **Significance**: Demonstrates the N=28 shell closure completely breaks down at Z=14, consistent with shell quenching driven by proton-neutron interactions.

## Topic 4: New Magic Number N=34 — Evidence from 54Ca

### Physical Phenomenon
The nucleus 54Ca (Z=20, N=34) provides evidence for a new magic number at N=34, predicted theoretically to arise from the tensor force component of the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

### Why It Matters
- Traditional magic numbers (2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126) were thought to be universal. The discovery of N=34 as magic in Ca isotopes shows that new shell closures can **emerge** far from stability.
- This validates the role of three-nucleon forces and tensor forces in driving shell evolution.
- Predicted by Otsuka et al. (UTokyo group, 2001) before experimental confirmation.

### Aoi's Contributions

#### Spectroscopy of 54Ca — Nature Publication
- **Paper**: D. Steppenbeck, S. Takeuchi, **N. Aoi** (3rd author), et al., "Evidence for a new nuclear 'magic number' from the level structure of 54Ca," *Nature* **502**, 207-210 (2013). [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/nature12522), [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24108051/)
- **Experiment**: Proton knockout reactions from 55Sc and 56Ti beams traveling at ~60% speed of light at RIKEN RIBF, using the BigRIPS fragment separator and an array of 186 DALI2 NaI(Tl) detectors on a beryllium target.
- **Key result**: The first excited state (2+) in 54Ca lies at a **high energy**, characteristic of a large shell gap. This highlights the doubly magic nature of 54Ca and provides direct experimental evidence for the onset of a sizable subshell closure at N=34.
- **Authors**: D. Steppenbeck, S. Takeuchi, N. Aoi, P. Doornenbal, M. Matsushita, H. Wang, H. Baba, N. Fukuda, S. Go, M. Honma, J. Lee, K. Matsui, S. Michimasa, T. Motobayashi, D. Nishimura, T. Otsuka, H. Sakurai, Y. Shiga, P.-A. Soderstrom, T. Sumikama, H. Suzuki, R. Taniuchi, Y. Utsuno, J.J. Valiente-Dobon, K. Yoneda.
- **Theoretical context**: N=34 magic number predicted by the UTokyo group in 2001 from the tensor force in the nucleon-nucleon interaction driving the f5/2-p1/2 gap.

## Topic 5: Neutron-Dominant Collectivity — Proton-Neutron Decoupling in 16C

### Physical Phenomenon
In extremely neutron-rich nuclei, the proton and neutron contributions to collective motion can decouple, leading to "neutron-dominant" quadrupole collectivity where the neutron deformation far exceeds the proton deformation.

### Why It Matters
- In normal nuclei, protons and neutrons contribute roughly proportionally (N/Z ratio) to collective excitations.
- Decoupling of proton/neutron modes represents a qualitatively new form of nuclear collectivity unique to exotic nuclei.
- Has implications for the nature of nuclear forces and the interplay between single-particle and collective degrees of freedom.

### Key Theoretical Quantities
- **Mn/Mp ratio**: The ratio of neutron to proton matrix elements for the E2 transition. In normal nuclei, Mn/Mp ~ N/Z.
- **B(E2; 2+ -> 0+)**: The reduced electric quadrupole transition probability, sensitive primarily to proton contributions.
- **Deformation parameters**: beta_n (neutron deformation) and beta_p (proton deformation) can be extracted separately.

### Aoi's Contributions

#### 16C: Neutron-Dominant Quadrupole Collective Motion
- **Paper**: H.J. Ong, N. Imai, **N. Aoi**, et al., "Neutron-dominant quadrupole collective motion in 16C," *Phys. Rev. C* **73**, 024610 (2006). [Phys. Rev. C](https://journals.aps.org/prc/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevC.73.024610)
- **Experiment**: Inelastic proton scattering on 16C in inverse kinematics at 33 MeV/nucleon beam energy.
- **Key results**:
  - Deformation parameter from proton scattering: beta_pp' = 0.50(8) (earlier measurement); revised to 0.47(5) in the Phys. Rev. C paper.
  - **Mn/Mp ratio = 7.6 +/- 1.7** — an extraordinarily large value indicating the 2+ state is nearly a pure valence neutron excitation.
  - The B(E2; 2+ -> 0+) = **0.28 +/- 0.06 Weisskopf units** — extremely small, confirming minimal proton involvement.
  - Conclusion: The quadrupole collectivity in 16C is **neutron-dominant**, with protons barely participating in the collective motion.
- **Theoretical interpretation**: The proton and neutron distributions have very different shapes (radius and deformation); the low-lying collective excitations decouple into proton and neutron modes, with the neutron mode being softer.
- Source: [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/235445489_Neutron-dominant_quadrupole_collective_motion_in_16C)

#### Related: Decoupling of Valence Neutrons in 16C
- Theoretical companion paper in *Phys. Lett. B* (2004) analyzing the proton-neutron deformation decoupling.
- [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0370269304003077)

## Topic 6: Proton Inelastic Scattering as a Nuclear Structure Probe

### Physical Phenomenon
Proton inelastic scattering in inverse kinematics (where a radioactive beam hits a proton target) is a powerful tool to measure nuclear deformation and collectivity of exotic nuclei. The technique excites the nucleus to its first 2+ state, and the de-excitation gamma-rays are measured coincidently with the scattered particles.

### Why It Matters
- Complementary to Coulomb excitation: proton scattering is sensitive to both proton AND neutron deformation (via the nuclear interaction), while Coulomb excitation is primarily sensitive to proton deformation.
- By combining proton scattering with Coulomb excitation data, one can separately extract Mn and Mp matrix elements.
- Applicable to the lightest and most exotic nuclei where other probes are difficult.

### Key Observables
- **Deformation length (delta)**: Extracted from angle-integrated inelastic cross sections via DWBA (distorted-wave Born approximation) calculations.
- **Mn/Mp ratio**: Obtained by comparing proton scattering (sensitive to both) with B(E2) from Coulomb excitation or lifetime measurements (sensitive mainly to protons).
- **Cross sections**: Both angle-integrated and differential cross sections measured.

### Aoi's Systematic Program
Aoi and collaborators conducted an extensive program of proton inelastic scattering studies on neutron-rich nuclei at RIKEN:

| Nucleus | Energy | Key Observable | Source |
|---------|--------|---------------|--------|
| 32Mg | 56 MeV/u | Higher excited states in IoI | [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/231014450_Proton_inelastic_scattering_on_32Mg) |
| 30Ne | intermediate | Deformation length (smaller than 32Mg) | [Phys. Rev. C 93, 044306](https://journals.aps.org/prc/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevC.93.044306) |
| 16C | 33 MeV/u | Mn/Mp = 7.6, neutron-dominant | [Phys. Rev. C 73, 024610](https://journals.aps.org/prc/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevC.73.024610) |
| 11Be, 14Be, 17B, 18C, 19C | various | Deformation of drip-line nuclei | Various |
| 28,30Ne, 32,34,36Mg | intermediate | Systematic collectivity in IoI | CNS draft |

### Technique: Inverse Kinematics with Liquid Hydrogen Target
- Radioactive beam impinges on thick liquid H2 target.
- De-excitation gamma-rays measured with DALI2 array (186 NaI(Tl) detectors).
- Scattered particles identified in downstream spectrometer (ZeroDegree or SAMURAI).
- Cross sections extracted by gamma-ray yield analysis.
- Deformation lengths obtained via comparison with DWBA calculations using optical model potentials.

## Topic 7: Superdeformation and Shape Coexistence in Light Nuclei

### Physical Phenomenon
Superdeformation refers to nuclear shapes with axis ratio approximately 2:1 (beta_2 ~ 0.6). Shape coexistence occurs when a nucleus has multiple 0+ states with very different shapes (spherical, normally deformed, superdeformed) at similar excitation energies. Electric monopole (E0) transitions between 0+ states are the key experimental signature.

### Why It Matters
- Superdeformation in light nuclei (A < 50) probes the interplay of single-particle and cluster degrees of freedom.
- E0 transition strengths (rho^2 values) quantify the overlap between different shape configurations.
- Connects to alpha-cluster structure in sd-shell nuclei.

### Key Physics: E0 Transitions
- The E0 (electric monopole) operator is T(E0) = sum_i e_i r_i^2, which connects 0+ states with different shapes.
- The dimensionless strength rho^2(E0) = |<0+_f|T(E0)|0+_i>|^2 / (e R^2)^2 quantifies the transition.
- Small rho^2 indicates weak mixing between shapes; large rho^2 indicates strong mixing.

### Aoi's Contributions

#### E0 Transition from Superdeformed Band in 40Ca
- **Paper**: E. Ideguchi, T. Kibedi, J.T.H. Dowie, T.H. Hoang, M. Kumar Raju, **N. Aoi**, et al., "Electric Monopole Transition from the Superdeformed Band in 40Ca," *Phys. Rev. Lett.* **128**, 252501 (2022). [PRL](https://link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevLett.128.252501), [arXiv:2205.06516](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.06516)
- **Technique**: e+e- pair-conversion spectroscopy.
- **Key results**:
  - **rho^2(E0; 0+_3 -> 0+_1) = 2.3(5) x 10^-3** — the smallest rho^2(E0; 0+ -> 0+) found in A < 50 nuclei.
  - **rho^2(E0; 0+_2 -> 0+_1) = 25.9(16) x 10^-3** — an order of magnitude larger, indicating significant mixing between the "normally deformed" 0+_2 and the "spherical" ground state.
  - The unusually small E0 strength from the superdeformed 0+_3 arises from **destructive interference** in shape-coexisting structures based on multiparticle-multihole excitations.
  - Large-Scale Shell Model (LSSM) calculations explain the microscopic structure.

#### Shape Coexistence and Superdeformation in 24Mg
- **Paper**: J.T.H. Dowie et al. (N. Aoi co-author), "Evidence for shape coexistence and superdeformation in 24Mg," *Phys. Lett. B* **811**, 135855 (2020). [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0370269320306584)
- **Key results**:
  - First observation of the E0 transition depopulating the first-excited 0+ state in 24Mg.
  - **rho^2 x 10^3 = 380(70)** — a large E0 strength.
  - Two-state mixing model implies deformation of the first-excited 0+ state: **beta_2 ~ 1** (superdeformed!).
  - Change in mean-square charge radius: **Delta<r^2> ~ 1.9 fm^2**.
  - Direct evidence of shape coexistence and superdeformation in 24Mg.
  - Connects to the alpha-cluster nature of 24Mg (6-alpha-particle structure).

#### Search for Hyper-Deformed Nuclei (KAKEN Grant 2018-2020)
- Aoi led a Grant-in-Aid for Scientific Research (A) project at Osaka University: "Search for hyper-deformed nuclei" (2018-2020).
- Hyper-deformation corresponds to axis ratios of ~3:1, even more extreme than superdeformation.
- This remains an active frontier; no definitive observation of hyper-deformed bands has been achieved.
- Source: [KAKEN](https://nrid.nii.ac.jp/en/nrid/1000000311647/)

## Topic 8: Shell Evolution Far from Stability

### Physical Phenomenon
The ordering and spacing of nuclear single-particle levels (the shell structure) is not fixed but evolves as a function of neutron and proton numbers. Far from stability, traditional magic numbers can disappear and new ones can emerge.

### Driving Forces
- **Monopole component of the tensor force**: The proton-neutron tensor interaction shifts single-particle levels depending on the relative orientation of nucleon spins. When a spin-orbit partner orbital is being filled, the tensor force pushes the opposite-spin partner orbital, changing the shell gap.
- **Three-nucleon forces**: Contribute to shell evolution, particularly for the emergence of N=34 in Ca isotopes.
- **Central and spin-orbit forces**: Also evolve with N/Z ratio.

### Key Formula
The monopole interaction V_mono between orbitals j and j' determines the effective single-particle energy shift:

epsilon_j(eff) = epsilon_j(bare) + sum_j' V_mono(j,j') n_j'

where n_j' is the occupation number of orbital j'.

### Aoi's Contributions to Shell Evolution Studies
Aoi's experimental work has provided critical data points for understanding shell evolution:

1. **Disappearance of N=20**: Studies of Ne, Na, Mg isotopes (island of inversion) demonstrating the collapse of the N=20 gap.
2. **Disappearance of N=28**: 42Si work showing complete breakdown of N=28 in neutron-rich Si.
3. **Emergence of N=34**: 54Ca spectroscopy providing evidence for new magic number.
4. **N=40 collectivity**: Contributions to studies of Cr and Fe isotopes near N=40.

### Related KAKEN Projects
- "Evolution of shell Structure and Collectivity in Neutron-Rich Nuclei" (2003-2006, Co-I with Shimoura)
- "Shell structure study in neutron-rich nuclei" (1999-2000, PI)

## Topic 9: Collectivity at N=40 — Neutron-Rich Cr and Fe Isotopes

### Physical Phenomenon
The N=40 harmonic oscillator shell closure (between the pf-shell and the g9/2 orbital) is not a traditional magic number but can produce shell effects in some nuclei. In neutron-rich Cr (Z=24) and Fe (Z=26) isotopes, a dramatic onset of collectivity occurs near N=40, creating what some call a "second island of inversion."

### Key Results (with Aoi as co-author)
- **62,64Cr spectroscopy**: First gamma-ray spectroscopy beyond the 4+ state in 62,64Cr using nucleon removal reactions at RIKEN RIBF.
  - Candidate for 6+ state in 64Cr identified.
  - Possible second excited 0+ state in 62Cr.
  - Results compared with LNPS shell-model calculations allowing neutron excitations across N=40 into g9/2 and d5/2 orbitals.
  - Source: [OSTI](https://www.osti.gov/pages/biblio/1760685)

- In-beam spectroscopy reveals competing nuclear shapes in 62Cr.
  - Source: [Nature Physics (2024)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41567-024-02680-0)

- The textbook shell model predicts spherical shapes for 64Cr (N=40), but experiments reveal strong deformation — analogous to the N=20 and N=28 islands of inversion.

## Topic 10: Neutron-Rich Nuclei Beyond 132Sn

### Physical Phenomenon
132Sn is a doubly magic nucleus (Z=50, N=82) far from stability. Exploring nuclei beyond this shell closure tests the seniority scheme and proton-neutron configuration mixing in heavy neutron-rich nuclei.

### Aoi's Contributions

#### 136Sn and 132Cd Spectroscopy
- **Paper**: N. Aoi (2nd author) et al., "Nuclear structure study for the neutron-rich nuclei beyond 132Sn: In-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy of 136Sn and 132Cd," *EPJ Web Conf.* (2018). [EPJ Conf.](https://www.epj-conferences.org/articles/epjconf/abs/2018/13/epjconf_cgs162018_02019/epjconf_cgs162018_02019.html)
- **Experiment**: In-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy with one- and two-proton removal reactions at RIKEN RIBF.
- **Key results**:
  - 2+_1 -> 0+_gs transitions identified in both nuclei.
  - **136Sn**: E(2+) similar to 134Sn, suggesting the **seniority scheme** holds for heavy tin isotopes beyond N=82.
  - **132Cd**: First spectroscopic information for even-even nuclei in the "southeast" region of 132Sn.
  - Valence neutrons play essential role in low-lying excitations.

## Topic 11: Spin-Orbit Potential Modification in Neutron-Rich Nuclei

### Physical Phenomenon
The spin-orbit potential, which is responsible for the nuclear shell structure, may be modified in neutron-rich nuclei due to the extended neutron distribution (skin/halo). Proton elastic scattering with analyzing power measurements can probe this potential.

### Aoi's Contributions

#### Proton Scattering from 6He and 8He
- **Paper**: S. Sakaguchi, T. Uesaka, **N. Aoi** (co-author), et al., "Shallow and diffuse spin-orbit potential for proton elastic scattering from neutron-rich helium isotopes at 71 MeV/nucleon," *Phys. Rev. C* **87**, 021601(R) (2013). [PRC](https://journals.aps.org/prc/abstract/10.1103/PhysRevC.87.021601), [arXiv:1302.4237](https://arxiv.org/abs/1302.4237)
- **Technique**: Vector analyzing power measurements using a solid polarized proton target at 71 MeV/nucleon.
- **Key result**: The spin-orbit potential for 8He (and 6He) is **significantly shallower and more diffuse** than global systematics of stable nuclei.
- **Physical interpretation**: Close similarity between the matter radius and the root-mean-square radius of the spin-orbit potential, indicating the extended neutron distribution modifies the spin-orbit interaction.
- This has implications for understanding shell structure evolution in neutron-rich nuclei, as the spin-orbit force is directly responsible for magic numbers.

## Topic 12: Halo Nuclei — Breakup Reactions

### Physical Phenomenon
Neutron halo nuclei (like 6He, 11Be, 11Li) have one or two weakly-bound valence neutrons extending far beyond the nuclear core, creating an extended matter distribution.

### Aoi's Contributions
- Coulomb dissociation of 11Be at 72 MeV/nucleon at RIKEN, extracting spectroscopic factor for halo configuration: **S = 0.72 +/- 0.04**.
- Kinematically complete measurement of 6He breakup (alpha + 2n) on Pb and C targets at 70 MeV/nucleon.
- Strong low-energy E1 excitation in 11Li Coulomb dissociation at 70 MeV/nucleon, peaked at Ex ~ 0.6 MeV with **B(E1) = 1.42(18) e^2 fm^2**.
- Sources: [INSPIRE](https://inspirehep.net/files/975faf8360ddefd392a1f397e24890d0), [PRL 96, 252502](https://link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevLett.96.252502)

## Topic 14: Experimental Techniques and Detector Development

### DALI2 / DALI2+ Gamma-Ray Array
- NaI(Tl) detector array with typically 186 scintillators.
- Covers polar angles ~15 deg to ~160 deg with ~6 deg angular resolution (FWHM).
- Designed for in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy with fast RI beams.
- Source: [SciSpace](https://scispace.com/papers/dali2-a-nai-tl-detector-array-for-measurements-of-g-rays-1kodfhxnwc)

### CAGRA (Clover Array Gamma-ray spectrometer at RCNP for Advanced research)
- 16-element Compton-suppressed Ge Clover array, constructed as a U.S.-Japan collaboration.
- Used for high-resolution nuclear structure studies at RCNP and RIBF.
- Aoi involved in development at RCNP.
- Source: [RCNP](https://www.rcnp.osaka-u.ac.jp/Divisions/np1-a/CAGRA/index.html)

- Aoi's KAKEN keywords include "gamma-ray tracking detectors" — next-generation Ge detector technology for position-sensitive gamma-ray detection.

## Cross-Cutting Themes

1. **Shell evolution**: How magic numbers change far from stability (N=20 breakdown, N=28 breakdown, N=34 emergence).
2. **Deformation and collectivity**: Measuring how nuclei deform in exotic regions; quantifying collectivity through E(2+), B(E2), E(4+)/E(2+) ratios.
3. **Proton-neutron decomposition**: Using proton scattering + Coulomb excitation to separately measure proton and neutron contributions.
4. **Shape coexistence**: Multiple nuclear shapes coexisting at similar energies; E0 transitions as the diagnostic.
5. **Technique development**: Advancing gamma-ray spectroscopy capabilities for use with fast RI beams.
