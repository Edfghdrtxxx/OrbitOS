## 3. Research Program

### Core Mission
The NUSPEQ group investigates **properties of nuclei in extreme states** (極限状態の原子核の性質) and their origins. The two target domains are:
1. **Unstable nuclei** not found in nature (far from the valley of stability)
2. **Extremely deformed nuclei** — superdeformed and hyperdeformed states

### Research Pillars

#### Pillar 1: In-beam Gamma-Ray Spectroscopy of Exotic Nuclei
- Shell evolution and collectivity in neutron-rich nuclei (e.g., Cr, Pd, Mg, Si isotopes)
- Deformation coexistence — studying nuclei that exhibit multiple shapes at similar energies
- Superdeformation — nuclei with axis ratio ~2:1
- **Hyperdeformation** — nuclei with axis ratio ~3:1 (Aoi's signature research topic; KAKEN grant "Search for hyper-deformed nuclei" 2018-2020)
- Uses GRAPE (Gamma-Ray detector Array with Position and Energy sensitivity) and DALI/DALI2 arrays at RIBF

#### Pillar 2: Active-Target TPC Experiments (Low-Energy Nuclear Reactions)
- Development and operation of **CAT-M** (CNS Active Target — Medium)
  - GEM-TPC with active area ~280 x 310 mm^2
  - Uses multi-layered thick GEM (1.2 mm) for electron multiplication
  - Dual-gain design: low gain for beam region, high gain for recoil region
  - Handles beam rates >10^5 Hz
  - Physics: missing-mass spectroscopy, nuclear incompressibility (ISGMR), deuteron inelastic scattering
- Also uses **CAT-S** (CNS Active Target — Small, ~100 x 100 mm^2)
- Experiments conducted at RIBF and HIMAC

#### Two Sub-programs (from NUSPEQ group page):
1. **Intermediate Energy Nuclear Spectroscopy** — using fast RI beams at ~100-300 MeV/u
2. **Low Energy Nuclear Spectroscopy** — using decelerated beams (via OEDO or other methods)

### Key Publications Involving Aoi
- "Evidence for shape coexistence and superdeformation in 24Mg" — PLB 2020 (Aoi as co-author)
- "Well-developed deformation in 42Si" — earlier work on neutron-rich Si
- Collectivity in neutron-rich Pd isotopes (in-beam gamma at RIBF)
- Collectivity in neutron-rich Cr isotopes
- Competing nuclear shapes in 62Cr — Nature Physics 2024 (UNVERIFIED whether Aoi is co-author)

## 6. Connection to Other Groups

### DONUTS Group (Imai Lab, CNS)
- **Shared infrastructure:** Both groups use OEDO-SHARAQ beamline system at RIBF
- The Imai research file states: "Prof. Nori Aoi leads the NUSPEQ group, which operates the CAT-M active-target TPC. Both groups share the OEDO-SHARAQ infrastructure and collaborate closely."
- Overlapping research interests in nuclear structure, deformation, and charge-exchange reactions
- Akane Sakaue's DGT research connects to Imai group's DGT program (Sasano, Yako, Imai et al.)

### SUNFLOWER Collaboration
- **Full name:** Spectroscopy of Unstable Nuclei with Fast and sLOW beam Experiments at RIBF
- Aoi is a central member/collaborator
- SUNFLOWER coordinates in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy experiments at RIBF
- Uses detector systems common to NUSPEQ work: GRAPE, DALI2, HiCARI

### RCNP, Osaka University
- Aoi spent 14 years at RCNP (2011-2025) before returning to UTokyo
- Shinsuke Ota (CAT-M developer) is now at RCNP
- Eiji Ideguchi, Kenichi Yoshida, and others at RCNP are collaborators
- RCNP co-hosts joint symposia with CNS (e.g., PCM2025)

### RIKEN Nishina Center
- NUSPEQ experiments are conducted at RIBF
- Aoi was a RIKEN researcher (2003-2011) before going to RCNP
- Historical co-investigators include H. Sakurai, H. Iwasaki, T. Motobayashi (all RIKEN)
