### Key Results Summary (Most-Cited Papers)

1. **54Ca magic number (Nature 2013, 347 cit.)** — Demonstrated N=34 as a new magic number from the level structure of 54Ca, measured at RIBF/RIKEN. The high 2+ excitation energy confirmed strong sub-shell closure.

2. **11Li E1 strength (PRL 2006, 254 cit.)** — Observed strong low-lying E1 strength in the two-neutron halo nucleus 11Li via Coulomb dissociation, providing key evidence for the soft dipole mode.

3. **11Be Coulomb dissociation (PLB 1994, 236 cit.)** — One of the pioneering halo nucleus breakup experiments, measuring Coulomb dissociation of 11Be at intermediate energy. Confirmed 11Be halo structure.

4. **19C halo (PRL 1999, 235 cit.)** — Established the one-neutron halo character of 19C through Coulomb dissociation measurements.

5. **11Be breakup (PRC 2004, 220 cit.)** — Detailed study of Coulomb and nuclear breakup mechanisms for the halo nucleus 11Be.

6. **31Ne halo in island of inversion (PRL 2009, 211 cit.)** — Discovered halo structure in 31Ne, a nucleus within the "island of inversion" at N=20, demonstrating the interplay between deformation and halo formation.

7. **22C drip-line (PRL 2010, 210 cit.)** — Observed a remarkably large reaction cross-section for the drip-line nucleus 22C, suggesting an extended matter distribution possibly indicating a two-neutron halo.

8. **31F stability (PLB 1999, 171 cit.)** — First evidence for particle stability of 31F and particle instability of 25N and 28O, mapping the neutron drip line.

9. **DALI2 detector (NIM-A 2014, 155 cit.)** — Technical paper describing the DALI2 NaI(Tl) detector array, a workhorse instrument for in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy at RIKEN RIBF.

### Highlights from Recent Work

- **Superdeformation in 40Ca (PRL 2022)** — First observation of the E0 transition from the superdeformed band in 40Ca, providing direct evidence for superdeformation in a doubly-magic nucleus.
- **Shape coexistence in 24Mg (PLB 2020)** — First observation of the E0 transition depopulating the first excited 0+ state in 24Mg; extracted deformation beta2 ~ 1 provides direct evidence for superdeformation and shape coexistence.
- **Triaxial Zr nuclei (PLB 2024)** — High-resolution in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy of neutron-rich Zr isotopes using the HiCARI array at RIBF, exploring triaxial deformation.
- **Mo isotope spectroscopy (PLB 2025)** — First high-resolution in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy of neutron-rich Mo isotopes at relativistic energy.
- **6He three-body breakup (PLB 2021, 26 cit.)** — Three-body breakup study of 6He revealing details of its halo structure.

## Research Themes Summary

Aoi's publication record spans several interconnected themes in nuclear structure physics:

1. **Halo Nuclei** — Core contributor to breakup/Coulomb dissociation studies of 11Be, 11Li, 19C, 22C, 31Ne, 6He
2. **Island of Inversion (N=20)** — Extensive work mapping deformation, collectivity, and shell evolution around N=20 (Ne, Na, Mg isotopes)
3. **Shell Evolution and Magic Numbers** — Key contributor to discovery of N=34 magic number (54Ca), N=16 closure (24O), and studies of Z=28 evolution (74Ni)
4. **Nuclear Deformation** — First-author work on large deformation in 62Cr (N=38), superdeformation in 24Mg and 40Ca
5. **Neutron Drip Line** — Studies of particle stability/instability at the neutron-rich boundary
6. **Instrumentation** — Co-developer of DALI2 detector array, spin-controlled RI beam production technique
7. **Neutron-Dominant Collectivity** — Discovery of anomalous neutron-proton decoupling in 16C quadrupole motion
