## Research Fields and Interests

**Primary field:** Experimental nuclear physics (素粒子・原子核・宇宙線・宇宙物理)

**Key research topics:**
- **Nuclear structure of unstable/exotic nuclei** — studying nuclei far from stability using radioactive isotope (RI) beams
- **In-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy** — using de-excitation gamma rays to probe single-particle and collective nuclear properties
- **Shell evolution and magic numbers** — investigating how nuclear shell structure changes in neutron-rich regions (e.g., island of inversion, new magic numbers like N=16)
- **Nuclear deformation** — superdeformation and hyperdeformation in light nuclei (e.g., 24Mg, 40Ca, 40Ar)
- **Direct reactions with fast RI beams** — knockout reactions, inelastic scattering at intermediate energies
- **Gamma-ray tracking detectors** — development of detection technology

**Keywords (from KAKEN/researchmap):** 不安定核ビーム (unstable nuclei beams), 中性子過剰核 (neutron-rich nuclei), ガンマ線核分光 (gamma-ray nuclear spectroscopy), 殻構造 (shell structure), 核変形 (nuclear deformation), hyperdeformation

## Summary Profile

Nori Aoi is an experimental nuclear physicist who has spent his career studying the structure of exotic nuclei using radioactive isotope beams. After completing his PhD at the University of Tokyo (~1998), he held positions at UTokyo (assistant, 1998–2002), RIKEN Nishina Center (researcher, 2003–2011), and Osaka University RCNP (professor, 2011–2025). As of March 2025, he returned to UTokyo as Professor at the Center for Nuclear Study (CNS), where he is now a colleague of Prof. Nobuaki Imai. His research focuses on in-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy, shell evolution in neutron-rich nuclei, and nuclear deformation phenomena. He has approximately 464 publications and over 10,000 citations (per ResearchGate).
