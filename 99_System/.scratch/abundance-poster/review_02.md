## 2. Flagged Error: Frebel & Norris Misattribution

The report's central finding is that the draft cites "Frebel & Norris, ARAA 50 (2012) 165--210" but that ARAA 50, pp. 165--210 is actually **Wiescher, Kappeler & Langanke (2012)**, "Critical Reactions in Contemporary Nuclear Astrophysics."

**Assessment: The flag appears correct and well-handled.**

- ARAA volumes are annual; Vol. 50 corresponds to 2012. The report identifies a specific different paper occupying those pages. The DOI given (`10.1146/annurev-astro-081811-125543`) follows ARAA's DOI pattern and is internally consistent.
- The report correctly identifies the actual Frebel & Norris paper as being in ARAA **53** (2015), pp. 631--688, with DOI `10.1146/annurev-astro-082214-122423`. This is the well-known review "Near-Field Cosmology with Extremely Metal-Poor Stars."
- The report provides three alternative resolutions (use Wiescher+, use Frebel & Norris 2015, or consider Sneden, Cowan & Gallino 2008). This is thorough.

**One weakness:** The report does not speculate on HOW the error arose. Given the poster is from a 2017 lecture, and Frebel & Norris (2015) was only two years old, the most likely scenario is that a draft citation was written from memory with the wrong volume number. The report should have noted this explicitly to help the user decide which paper was actually intended. The poster's content (stellar populations, metallicity, extremely metal-poor stars) strongly suggests Frebel & Norris (2015) was the intended reference -- the report should have stated this more decisively rather than presenting three equal alternatives.

## 3. Confidence Ratings

All references are rated HIGH confidence. **This is mostly justified but slightly overconfident in one case:**

- **Ref 2 (Argast et al.):** Rated HIGH despite the DOI being unconfirmed. If the report cannot confirm a DOI, it should be rated **HIGH for the bibliographic identification** but should note the DOI gap explicitly rather than lumping it all under a single HIGH rating.
- **Ref 3 (Wiescher+ identification):** The report claims HIGH confidence that ARAA 50 pp. 165--210 is Wiescher, Kappeler & Langanke. This is a strong claim. If correct, it is an important catch. However, the report provides no source for this identification beyond its own assertion. For a skeptical review: this specific claim is the linchpin of the report's value. A wrong identification here would be catastrophic. The confidence rating should acknowledge that this was identified from reference databases (ADS, DOI resolver, etc.) rather than just stated as fact.

**Verdict: Confidence ratings are reasonable but could benefit from brief justification of the method used for verification (e.g., "confirmed via ADS abstract service" or "confirmed via DOI resolution").**

## 5. Completeness

The report recommends three additional references:

### A. B2FH (Burbidge et al. 1957)
- Rev. Mod. Phys. 29, 547--650 (1957). DOI `10.1103/RevModPhys.29.547`.
- **Correct and important suggestion.** The foundational paper of stellar nucleosynthesis. However, for a lecture poster (not a review article), citing B2FH is conventional but not strictly necessary. The report's "arguably should cite it" phrasing is appropriate.

### B. Kobayashi, Karakas & Lugaro (2020)
- ApJ 900, 179. DOI `10.3847/1538-4357/abae65`.
- **Good suggestion.** This is indeed the most comprehensive modern GCE model. However, the poster is from a **2017 lecture** (see the file path: `lecture02_03_21_2017/`), so a 2020 reference could not have been included originally. The report fails to note this temporal impossibility. This recommendation should be flagged as "for an updated version only."

### C. Sneden, Cowan & Gallino (2008)
- ARAA 46, 241--288. DOI `10.1146/annurev.astro.46.060407.145207`.
- **Good suggestion** and already mentioned in the Frebel & Norris error discussion. Relevant to the poster's Stage 3 (r-process enrichment). Was available in 2017.

**Missing from the completeness check:**
- **Cameron (1957)**, "Stellar Evolution, Nuclear Astrophysics, and Nucleogenesis" (CRL-41) -- the independent counterpart to B2FH. If recommending B2FH, Cameron should at least be mentioned.
- **Wallerstein et al. (1997)**, "Synthesis of the elements in stars: forty years of progress," Rev. Mod. Phys. 69, 995--1084. This is the major 40-year update to B2FH and is a standard reference in any chemical evolution context.
- **Matteucci (2012)** or **Matteucci (2003)** -- textbook/review on galactic chemical evolution. For a poster specifically on "Chemical Abundance Evolution," a Matteucci reference would be natural.
- **Asplund et al. (2009)**, ARAA 47, 481--522 -- the updated solar abundance standard that has largely superseded Grevesse & Sauval (1998). The report does not flag that the poster uses an outdated solar reference baseline.

**Verdict: The completeness section is adequate but incomplete. The Kobayashi+ (2020) anachronism is an error in the report. Several standard references are missing from the suggestions.**
