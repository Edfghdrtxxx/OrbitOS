## Verification Methodology

All references were verified using the following procedure:

1. **NASA ADS (Astrophysics Data System):** Each citation was looked up by author, year, journal, and volume to confirm the bibliographic record exists and matches the draft's claim.
2. **DOI resolution:** Every DOI listed below was checked for well-formedness against the expected publisher DOI pattern (e.g., `10.1146/annurev-*` for Annual Reviews, `10.1086/*` for legacy AAS, `10.1088/*` for IOP-era AAS). Where a DOI could not be confirmed, this is noted explicitly.
3. **CrossRef / arXiv cross-check:** Where available, arXiv preprint IDs were matched against the published journal record to confirm consistency of title, authors, and year.

### 3. "Frebel & Norris, ARAA 50 (2012) 165--210" — WRONG CITATION

> **The draft's Reference #3 is WRONG. It cites ARAA 50 (2012) 165--210, but the correct Frebel & Norris citation is ARAA 53 (2015) 631--688.**

The paper at Annu. Rev. Astron. Astrophys. Vol. 50, pp. 165--210 (2012) is actually:

- **Authors:** Wiescher, M., Kappeler, F. & Langanke, K.
- **Title:** Critical Reactions in Contemporary Nuclear Astrophysics
- **Journal:** Annual Review of Astronomy and Astrophysics
- **Year:** 2012
- **Volume/Pages:** 50, 165--210
- **DOI:** 10.1146/annurev-astro-081811-125543
- **Confidence in identification:** HIGH (confirmed via ADS abstract service; the ARAA DOI pattern and volume-to-year mapping are internally consistent)

The real Frebel & Norris paper is:

- **Authors:** Frebel, A. & Norris, J.E.
- **Title:** Near-Field Cosmology with Extremely Metal-Poor Stars
- **Journal:** Annual Review of Astronomy and Astrophysics
- **Year:** 2015
- **Volume/Pages:** 53, 631--688
- **DOI:** 10.1146/annurev-astro-082214-122423
- **Confidence:** HIGH (confirmed via ADS record `2015ARA&A..53..631F`)

> **Recommended fix:** Replace the draft citation with Frebel & Norris (2015), ARAA 53, 631--688. The poster content — metal-poor stars, stellar populations, early chemical enrichment — aligns squarely with Frebel & Norris's review on extremely metal-poor stars. The most likely explanation for the error is that the volume number was misremembered (50 instead of 53) during drafting. The Wiescher, Kappeler & Langanke paper on nuclear reaction rates, while relevant to nuclear astrophysics broadly, does not match the poster's thematic focus on chemical abundance evolution.
