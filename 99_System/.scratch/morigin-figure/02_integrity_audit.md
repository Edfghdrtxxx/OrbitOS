# Academic Integrity Audit — MORIGIN Figure

## Summary Verdict
**Borderline Derivative / Close Copy (conceptual)** — The figure reproduces a well-established conceptual composite from the SRC literature: coordinate-space NN potential and mean-field potential mapped via color-coded connections to a decomposed momentum distribution. No single published figure is directly traced or pixel-copied, but the entire intellectual content — the three-panel layout, the coordinate-to-momentum mapping, and the decomposition into mean-field, intermediate-range (tensor/attractive-well), and short-range (repulsive-core) contributions — is a standard pedagogical device in the SRC community, established across multiple review articles (Hen et al. 2017, Arrington et al. 2012, Fomin et al. 2017) and widely circulated in conference talks. The visual execution (hand-drawn curves, specific color choices, two-panel-left / one-panel-right arrangement) is author-created, but these are minor presentational decisions that do not alter the intellectual content. Attribution must be explicit and go beyond merely citing the physics — it must acknowledge the visual-pedagogical device itself.

## Published Figure Comparison

### Hen, Miller, Piasetzky & Weinstein — Rev. Mod. Phys. 89, 045002 (2017)
**Similarity: HIGH (primary concern)**
This landmark SRC review contains several conceptual figures that establish the same narrative flow used in MORIGIN:
- **Fig. 1 of Hen et al.** shows the NN potential (repulsive core + attractive well) and discusses how short-range features produce high-momentum components. The schematic style of the NN potential in MORIGIN follows the same visual convention used throughout the SRC literature, though MORIGIN's specific rendering (curve shape, arrow placement) appears hand-drawn/custom rather than traced.
- **Fig. 2 and related momentum distribution figures** in Hen et al. show rho(p) on a log scale vs momentum, with distinct regions: the mean-field dominated low-momentum peak, the 2N-SRC plateau/scaling region (~1.5-2.5 fm^-1), and the 3N-SRC region at higher momenta. MORIGIN's right panel uses the same axis conventions (log scale, momentum in fm^-1, range 1-4 fm^-1) and shows qualitatively similar curve behavior with color-coded regions.
- **The conceptual mapping** (connecting coordinate-space physics to momentum-space observables) is a core pedagogical device used throughout Hen et al.'s review.

**Key differences from Hen et al.:** MORIGIN combines the NN potential AND the mean-field (Woods-Saxon) potential in a single figure, which Hen et al. typically present separately. The color-coded connecting lines between coordinate-space and momentum-space are a visual element not directly present in Hen et al.'s specific figures. The two-panel-left / one-panel-right layout is an author compositional choice, but this amounts to rearrangement of panels rather than new intellectual content.

### Arrington, Higinbotham, Rosner & Sargsian — Prog. Part. Nucl. Phys. 67, 898 (2012)
**Similarity: MODERATE-HIGH**
This SRC review contains conceptual schematics connecting NN correlations to momentum distributions and is a commonly cited source in the field. The pedagogical presentation of coordinate-space features mapping to momentum-space regions appears in this review, predating the Hen et al. (2017) formulation. MORIGIN's conceptual flow is consistent with the framework presented here. This review should be checked for a closer visual match than Hen et al.'s specific figures.

### Fomin, Higinbotham, Sargsian et al. — Annu. Rev. Nucl. Part. Sci. 67, 129 (2017)

Another review that uses pedagogical figures for SRC concepts and the decomposition of momentum distributions into mean-field and SRC contributions. The schematic decomposition of n(k) into component regions is standard in this review and may provide a closer visual precedent for MORIGIN's right panel than Hen et al.

Some of Ciofi degli Atti's reviews include schematic decompositions of n(k) showing mean-field vs. SRC contributions. These are quantitative or semi-quantitative plots rather than the type of conceptual schematic used in MORIGIN, but the decomposition concept is the same.

## Description of the Actual Figure's Color-Coding

Before assessing originality, the actual color-to-physics mapping in the figure must be described accurately:

- **Green arrow** in the NN potential panel: Points downward at the **repulsive core** region of the potential (short range, positive V — the high peak at small r). A green connecting line runs from this region to the **green curve** in the rho(p) plot, which appears as a relatively flat contribution around ~2-3 fm^-1 (intermediate momenta).
- **Blue arrow** in the NN potential panel: Points into the **attractive well** region (intermediate range, negative V, below the zero line). A blue/purple connecting line runs to the **blue curve** in rho(p), which appears at the highest momenta (~3+ fm^-1). This placement is physically defensible: the **tensor force**, which operates primarily at intermediate range (in and around the attractive well at ~0.7-1.4 fm), is the **dominant** source of SRC for np pairs in the deuteron-like T=0, S=1 channel (Schiavilla et al. 2007, Alvioli et al. 2008, Subedi et al. 2008). The blue arrow may thus be highlighting the tensor-force origin of SRC rather than the central repulsive-core origin. However, the figure would benefit from an annotation clarifying whether the blue arrow indicates the tensor-force or repulsive-core contribution, as these produce SRC at somewhat different momentum scales.
- **Orange arrow/curve** in rho(p): A horizontal arrow along the bottom of the plot from ~1 to ~2 fm^-1, with an orange curve in the low-momentum region. This connects to the mean-field/nuclear potential panel (lower-left) via an implied or faint connecting line.
- **Red curve** in rho(p): Appears as the overall/total distribution envelope, peaking at low momentum and falling steeply.
- **Large blue arrow** on rho(p): Points at the high-momentum region (~3 fm^-1), highlighting the SRC-dominated tail.

**Note on the "n-n interaction" label:** The figure labels the NN potential as "n-n interaction" (neutron-neutron), which is physically misleading since SRC are dominated by np (neutron-proton) pairs due to the tensor force. This label differentiates the figure from standard SRC schematics (which typically use "NN interaction" or "nucleon-nucleon potential"), but this differentiation appears to be an error rather than a deliberate original choice. It should not be counted as evidence of originality and should be corrected to "NN interaction."

## Originality Rating

**Borderline Derivative / Close Copy (conceptual)** — with the following justification:

1. **Concept:** The conceptual narrative (NN potential features -> momentum distribution regions, with explicit color-coded mapping) is a well-established pedagogical device in the SRC community, presented across multiple review articles (Hen et al. 2017, Arrington et al. 2012, Fomin et al. 2017) and widely circulated conference talks. This is NOT an original conceptual contribution but rather a standard framework.

2. **Layout:** The specific two-panel-left / one-panel-right layout with color-coded connecting lines is an author compositional choice. However, rearranging panels and adding color-coded connections amounts to a presentational variation on an established visual concept, not a substantive intellectual contribution. The threshold between "Derivative" and "Close Copy" depends on whether one evaluates originality at the level of visual execution or conceptual content. At the conceptual level, this figure is very close to a reproduction of a standard SRC pedagogical schematic. At the level of pixel-by-pixel visual execution, it is clearly not a direct copy.

3. **Visual execution:** The hand-drawn/custom quality of the curves and author-chosen color assignments are original in the narrow sense that no pixels are copied. However, the physics content, the narrative flow, the decomposition scheme, and the coordinate-to-momentum mapping concept are all standard. Original visual execution of a standard concept, without substantial new insight or information, places the figure closer to "Close Copy (conceptual)" than a fully "Derivative" work that adds meaningful new content.

4. **The "n-n interaction" label** is a point of visual difference from standard SRC schematics, but it is a physics error, not evidence of originality. It should be corrected, not preserved. The blue arrow's placement in the attractive well, while initially appearing anomalous, is physically defensible as highlighting the tensor-force origin of SRC (see color-coding section above).

5. **Combined elements:** While combining the NN potential and Woods-Saxon potential in a single figure with explicit connections is a somewhat distinctive choice, this combination is implicit in every SRC review that discusses both potentials in sequence. Making it explicit in one figure is a minor presentational contribution.

**Conclusion:** The figure sits at the boundary between "Derivative" and "Close Copy (conceptual)." It clears the "Close Copy" threshold only because: (a) no single published figure with this exact composite layout has been identified, and (b) the visual execution is clearly author-created rather than traced. However, these are narrow grounds, and the attribution requirements are correspondingly strong.

### Caption recommendation:
The figure caption must use language that explicitly acknowledges the visual-pedagogical device, not merely the underlying physics. Recommended phrasing:

**Preferred:** "Schematic created by the author, inspired by the pedagogical presentation in Hen et al. [Rev. Mod. Phys. 89, 045002 (2017)], Arrington et al. [Prog. Part. Nucl. Phys. 67, 898 (2012)], and related SRC reviews."

**Alternative (if a specific visual source is identified):** "Schematic adapted from [specific source] with modifications."

The phrase "following the conceptual framework" used in the previous version of this audit is insufficient because it could be read as acknowledging only the physics, not the visual-pedagogical device. For a master's thesis, the safer and more honest attribution explicitly credits the visual inspiration.

### Redesign consideration:
The figure's visual execution is sufficiently distinct from any single identified published figure that it does not constitute a reproduction requiring formal permission. However, the conceptual content is entirely standard, and the attribution must be explicit and generous. If a closer visual match is found in the literature (particularly in Arrington et al. 2012, Fomin et al. 2017, or conference slides), the attribution may need to be upgraded to "adapted from."

## Specific Differentiation Suggestions

To strengthen the claim of originality, consider the following modifications:

1. **Add quantitative annotations:** Include approximate energy/distance scales on the NN potential (e.g., repulsive core radius ~0.5 fm, well depth ~-40 MeV) and label the Fermi momentum k_F on the momentum axis. This moves the figure from purely schematic toward pedagogically informative and differentiates it from generic SRC schematics.

2. **Add nucleus-specific context:** If this is for a specific nucleus (e.g., ^12C, ^4He), labeling the curves with the specific nucleus would differentiate from generic schematics.

3. **Include the tensor force:** The NN interaction is often decomposed into central + tensor + spin-orbit components for SRC discussions. Adding a visual indication of the tensor force contribution (particularly relevant for the 2N-SRC region dominated by np pairs in the deuteron-like T=0 channel) would add originality and physics depth.

4. **Add a Fourier transform symbol:** Since the coordinate-to-momentum connection is fundamentally a Fourier transform relationship, adding an explicit FT symbol or notation on the connecting lines would clarify the physics and distinguish the figure from simpler schematics.

5. **Refine the color-coding logic:** The current color scheme has ambiguities (see the color-coding description above). The blue arrow's placement in the attractive well is defensible as a tensor-force interpretation but should be annotated to clarify intent. Making the color-to-physics mapping systematic, physically accurate, and explicitly annotated would simultaneously improve the physics and differentiate the figure from its sources.

6. **Correct the "n-n interaction" label to "NN interaction":** This fixes a physics error while also aligning the label with standard nomenclature.

**Caution on one previously suggested differentiation:** The previous version of this audit suggested labeling momentum regions explicitly with "Mean-field," "2N-SRC," and "3N-SRC." While informative, adding these standard labels would make the figure look *more* like standard SRC review figures (which routinely use these labels), not less. This suggestion is withdrawn as a differentiation strategy, though it may still be useful for pedagogical clarity.
