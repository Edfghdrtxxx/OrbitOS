### 2. Baseline & Rate-Dependence Analysis — Zhang Weidong

- Analyzed pulse-height spectra at three beam rates:
    - 10³ rate → mean ≈ 924
    - 10⁴ rate → mean ≈ 890
    - 10⁵ rate → baseline ~30% lower than the other two
- Beam rate increased by ~3%, yet the mean is declining — the non-linear trend may indicate a threshold effect.
- Used upstream and downstream measurements from the MUSIC detector; beam rate cut from 10 to 3 (units TBC).
- Baseline at 10³ and 10⁴ are similar; the significant drop at 10⁵ needs investigation.
- Time-slice analysis (2.5 μs windows): at 10⁴ each slice captures one pulse; at 10⁵ ten slices are needed — data-acquisition density is becoming a key concern.
- Baseline definition: asymmetry can be set to zero; event-by-event processing with time-window segmentation is the proposed approach.
- The decline observed in natural-state values must be reflected in simulations.
