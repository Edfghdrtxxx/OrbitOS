# Tran 2018 — Z=6 Magic Number in Carbon Isotopes

## Paper Summary

Tran et al. present experimental evidence that **Z = 6 is a magic number (subshell closure)** in neutron-rich carbon isotopes ¹³⁻²⁰C. This is the smallest [[Nuclear Magic Numbers|magic number]] originating from [[Spin-Orbit Coupling in Nuclei|spin-orbit coupling]], corresponding to the filled 1p₃/₂ proton subshell. Ab initio calculations with chiral NN+3N forces reproduce the observations.

## Motivation

- The [[Nuclear Shell Model]] and its magic numbers are a cornerstone of nuclear structure
- While the phenomenological spin-orbit force (1949) explains the classical magic numbers, its microscopic origin remains an open question
- Studying the *smallest* SO-originated closure (Z = 6) provides a clean test of modern nuclear theory

## Experimental Approach

### Facility & Setup
- Experiment performed at **RIBF (RIKEN)**, using the BigRIPS fragment separator
- Secondary beams of ¹⁵⁻²⁰C produced via fragmentation of ²²Ne and ⁴⁸Ca primary beams on a Be target
- Interaction cross sections measured on a carbon target at ~900 MeV/nucleon

### Three Lines of Evidence

**1. Point-proton distribution radii (Rp)**
- Extracted from interaction cross sections using Glauber model analysis
- Carbon Rp values are **systematically smaller** than those of neighboring boron (Z = 5) and nitrogen (Z = 7) isotopes
- This "kink" at Z = 6 signals a tightly bound, closed proton configuration

**2. B(E2; 0⁺ → 2⁺) transition rates**
- Compiled from literature for even-even C, O isotopes
- Carbon isotopes (¹⁶C, ¹⁸C, ²⁰C) show **anomalously low B(E2) values** — indicating proton rigidity (the protons don't easily excite because the shell is closed)
- Contrasts with neighboring isotopes where protons are more deformable

**3. Proton separation energies (Sp)**
- From AME2016 atomic mass evaluation
- Carbon isotopes have **enhanced Sp** relative to B and N neighbors across the full neutron-rich range
- Confirms extra proton binding at Z = 6

### Key Result
The subshell closure at Z = 6 is **prevalent** — it persists robustly from ¹³C (N = 7) through ²⁰C (N = 14), surviving the addition of many neutrons.

## Theoretical Validation

- **Ab initio coupled-cluster (CC) calculations** performed for ¹⁴C and ¹⁵C
- Used chiral effective field theory interactions: NNLOsat (NN + 3N forces)
- Calculations reproduce the measured proton radii and confirm the subshell closure
- Demonstrates that the spin-orbit force and three-nucleon interactions rooted in QCD are essential ingredients

## Significance

- Establishes Z = 6 as the smallest SO-originated magic number
- Provides a benchmark for ab initio nuclear theory in the light-nucleus regime
- Opens questions about the evolution of the 1p₃/₂ subshell closure toward the neutron drip line
