### Terminology
- **特定技能 (Specified Skilled Worker Visa)**: A Japanese visa category introduced in 2019 targeting foreign workers in labor-shortage industries. Category 1 requires N3–N4 Japanese and allows a 5-year stay; Category 2 offers a pathway toward long-term residency, though its full implementation remains uncertain.
- **双元制 (Dual Education System)**: A German vocational training model combining on-the-job apprenticeship at a company with part-time study at a vocational school. It provides a legally recognized qualification and is a legitimate pathway to German residency for non-EU nationals.
- **H-1B Lottery**: A US visa program that allows American employers to hire foreign workers in specialty occupations. Because demand far exceeds the annual cap of 85,000 visas, applicants must win a random lottery; the 3-year cumulative approval rate for Chinese nationals is currently below 40%.
- **NIW (National Interest Waiver)**: A US immigration benefit under the EB-2 green card category that waives the normal requirement for a job offer and labor certification, allowing individuals whose work is deemed to be in the national interest of the United States to self-petition for permanent residency.
- **枫叶卡 (Maple Leaf Card)**: The informal name for Canada's Permanent Resident (PR) card. After holding PR status for 5 years, individuals can apply for Canadian citizenship and subsequently use a Canadian passport to obtain a TN visa for employment in the United States under the USMCA agreement.
