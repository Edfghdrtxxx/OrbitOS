# How to Think in English

The video addresses the common challenge of thinking directly in English, even after learning vocabulary and grammar. It explains that many learners' brains are still **translating every word** from their native language into English. The **real secret to fluency** is to **train your mind to think in English directly**.

## Strategies

### 1. Train Your Inner Voice (0:42–1:26)
- **Start small:** Begin by consciously using English for your inner thoughts.
- **Morning thoughts:** When you wake up, think "I need to brush my teeth" in English instead of your native language.
- **Describe your surroundings:** As you walk to school or work, mentally describe what you see: "The sun is bright today" or "That car is moving fast."
- **Practice makes it automatic:** These sentences might feel slow or unnatural at first, but consistent practice will make them automatic, leading your brain to think in English without translation.

### 2. Immerse Yourself in English Sounds (1:28–2:02)
- **Brain as a sponge:** Your brain absorbs English sounds easily, making it easier to think in the language.
- **Integrate into lifestyle:** Don't just study English in lessons; make it part of your daily life.
- **Passive listening:** Watch TV shows and listen to podcasts. It's okay if you don't understand everything initially.
- **Get used to the flow:** The goal is for your ears and brain to become accustomed to the **rhythm, tone, and natural flow** of English.

### 3. Adopt English Thought Patterns (2:04–2:44)
- **Culture and habits of expression:** Thinking in English is also about understanding its cultural and expressive habits.
- **Short and direct sentences:** English speakers often prefer **short and direct sentences**. Avoid trying to translate long, complicated sentences from your native language.
- **Example:** Instead of "I feel very exhausted because I had a long day full of responsibilities," think "I'm so tired. Today was a long day."

### 4. Lower Your Fear of Mistakes (2:48–3:18)
- **Thinking is private:** No one can hear your thoughts — it's a **safe space** to practice without worrying about grammar.
- **Don't strive for perfection:** Even if your inner sentences aren't perfect, the important thing is to keep moving in English without stopping.
- **Natural improvement:** With consistent practice, your brain will naturally get better at choosing the right words and grammar patterns.

### 5. Speak to Yourself in English (3:20–3:48)
- **Describe daily actions:** If you're cooking, say: "I'm cutting the vegetables now. I'm adding salt. The food smells good."
- **Narrate your surroundings:** If driving or walking, say: "I see children playing. The weather feels cold today."
- **Connect English to daily life:** These simple sentences help connect your everyday experiences with English thought.

### 6. Visualize New Words (3:51–4:18)
- **Direct connection to meaning:** When learning new words, connect them directly to an **image in your mind** rather than translating to your native language.
- **Examples:** For "apple," imagine a shiny red apple; for "happiness," imagine a big smile.
- **Faster thinking:** This prevents your brain from wasting time on translation.

### 7. Create Immersion Situations (4:20–4:40)
- **Make English the only option:** Change your phone's language, write your shopping list in English, or send yourself short messages in English.
- **Get comfortable:** Even if it feels strange initially, these actions push your brain to get comfortable with English in daily life.

## Key Takeaway
Thinking in English is a **skill that grows with practice**. You will **speak faster**, **understand more clearly**, and feel **more confident**. Start small, be patient, and keep practicing until it becomes natural.
