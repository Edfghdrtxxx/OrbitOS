# Building Visibility in the European Workplace

## Summary
Building visibility in the European workplace does not require becoming highly social or attending endless coffee chats. Instead, practical, scalable ways to increase exposure at work — especially for introverts — include making expertise visible through public help, internal workshops, and documentation that naturally reaches more people.

## Key Highlights
- Exposure in the workplace is often discussed, but people rarely explain how to build it in a structured and viable way
- Increasing visibility does **not** require attending more coffee chats, networking events, or other social gatherings
- **Introverted professionals** can still become highly visible by making their work easier for others to find and benefit from
- One effective method is **answering technical questions in public team channels** instead of private messages, so more people see your expertise — they begin to associate your name with that topic
- **Knowledge sharing** creates a compounding effect because one answer can help multiple coworkers while also improving communication efficiency
- Another strong visibility strategy is **initiating small internal workshops** on technical topics — even if you are not part of a formal training program
