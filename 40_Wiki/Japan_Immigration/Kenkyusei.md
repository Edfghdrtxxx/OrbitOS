# Kenkyusei

## Definition

Kenkyusei (研究生) is a non-degree "Research Student" status at Japanese universities, allowing foreign students to study under a professor for 6–12 months before formally enrolling in a degree program. It serves as a probationary bridge year, common for prospective PhD students who need to secure a supervisor and demonstrate research fit.

## Key Points

- Not a degree program — no credits earned, no diploma awarded; the purpose is lab integration and supervisor matching
- Typically self-funded (~2.5–2.8M JPY/year for living expenses), though some students receive [[Japan_Graduate_Scholarships_MEXT_and_JASSO|MEXT scholarships]]
- Requires [[Naidaku]] (informal acceptance) from a professor before the university processes the formal application
- Enrollment usually begins in April or October; a [[Student_Visa_Japan|Student Visa]] is issued for the Kenkyusei period
- Successful Kenkyusei often transition to formal Master's or PhD enrollment at the same institution

## Examples

- A Chinese physics graduate applies as Kenkyusei at [[UTokyo CNS]] for October 2027, spends one year in the lab, then enrolls as a PhD student from October 2028.
- A MEXT scholarship recipient may skip the Kenkyusei phase if directly admitted to a degree program.
