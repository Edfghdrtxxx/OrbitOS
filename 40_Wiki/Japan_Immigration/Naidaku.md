# Naidaku

## Definition

Naidaku (内諾) means "informal acceptance" or "internal consent" in Japanese. In the context of graduate admissions, it refers to a professor's verbal or written agreement to accept a student into their lab before the formal university application process begins. Without naidaku, most Japanese universities will not process a [[Kenkyusei]] or PhD application.

## Key Points

- Obtained through direct email outreach to the target professor, typically including a research plan, CV, and academic transcripts
- Not legally binding but culturally strong — a professor who grants naidaku is expected to follow through during the formal review
- The naidaku process is the practical bottleneck for international applicants; the formal application is largely procedural afterward
- Deadlines vary by university; for [[UTokyo CNS]], naidaku should be secured months before the enrollment period (e.g., by June 2026 for October 2027 [[Kenkyusei]] enrollment)

## Examples

- A student emails a [[UTokyo CNS]] professor with a research proposal. After several exchanges and a Zoom meeting, the professor replies: "I am happy to accept you as a research student." This constitutes naidaku.
- Without naidaku, submitting a formal [[Kenkyusei]] application to the graduate school is effectively futile.
