# Gamow-Teller Transition
## Definition

A Gamow-Teller (GT) transition is a nuclear transition mediated by the axial-vector part of the weak interaction, in which both spin and isospin of a nucleon are flipped. It is one of the two allowed beta-decay modes, the other being the [[Fermi Transition|Fermi transition]] (isospin flip only, no spin change).

## Transition Operator

The GT operator acts on the nucleus as:

$$\hat{O}_{GT} = g_A \sum_k \boldsymbol{\sigma}_k \boldsymbol{\tau}_k$$

where $\boldsymbol{\sigma}$ is the Pauli spin operator and $\boldsymbol{\tau}$ is the isospin raising/lowering operator. The axial-vector coupling constant $g_A \approx 1.27$ (free nucleon value), though in-medium quenching reduces the effective value to $g_A^{\text{eff}} \approx 0.7$--$1.0$ in nuclear structure calculations.

## Selection Rules

| Quantity | Allowed change |
|----------|---------------|
| $\Delta J$ | $0, \pm 1$ (but $0^+ \to 0^+$ forbidden) |
| $\Delta \pi$ | No parity change |
| $\Delta T_z$ | $\pm 1$ |

## GT Giant Resonance (GTGR)

The coherent superposition of $1^+$ particle-hole GT excitations forms the **Gamow-Teller Giant Resonance** -- a collective spin-isospin oscillation. It was first observed in $(p,n)$ reactions in the 1980s. The GTGR lies a few MeV above the Isobaric Analog State and exhausts a large fraction of the Ikeda sum rule $S_- - S_+ = 3(N - Z)$.

## Experimental Probes

GT strengths $B(GT)$ are extracted from [[Charge-Exchange Reaction|charge-exchange reactions]] at intermediate energies ($> 100$ MeV/u), where the forward-angle cross section is proportional to $B(GT)$:

$$\left(\frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega}\right)_{0^\circ} \propto B(GT)$$

Common probes include $(p,n)$, $(n,p)$, and $({}^3\text{He}, t)$ reactions.
