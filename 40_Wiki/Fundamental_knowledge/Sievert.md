# Sievert

## Definition

The sievert (Sv) is the SI unit of equivalent and effective radiation dose, representing the biological effect of [[Ionizing Radiation]] on the human body. 1 Sv = 1 J/kg weighted by radiation type and tissue sensitivity.

## Key Points

- **Equivalent dose** = absorbed dose (Gray) × radiation weighting factor (wR): gamma/beta wR = 1, alpha wR = 20, neutron wR = 2.5–20 depending on energy
- **Effective dose** further weights by tissue sensitivity — e.g., gonads and bone marrow are more radiosensitive than skin
- Practical scale: occupational limit in Japan is 50 mSv/year; a chest X-ray delivers ~0.02 mSv; acute dose of ~1 Sv causes radiation sickness
- Measured by personal dosimeters as part of [[Dosimetry]] programs in a [[Radiation Controlled Area]]

## Examples

- A radiation worker's monthly glass badge reading of 0.3 mSv — well within the annual 50 mSv limit
- Natural background radiation delivers ~2.4 mSv/year on average worldwide
