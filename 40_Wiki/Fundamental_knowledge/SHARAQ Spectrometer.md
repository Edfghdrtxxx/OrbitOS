# SHARAQ Spectrometer
## Definition

**SHARAQ** is a high-resolution magnetic spectrometer at [[RIKEN Nishina Center for Accelerator-Based Science (RNC)|RIKEN RIBF]], located downstream of the [[OEDO]] beamline at the S0 focal point. It performs missing-mass spectroscopy on reaction products from [[Radioactive Isotope Beam|RI beam]] experiments with exceptionally high momentum resolution.

## Magnetic Configuration

SHARAQ uses a **QQDQD** configuration: two quadrupole magnets, a dipole, another quadrupole, and a final dipole. This ion-optical design achieves a resolving power of:

$$\frac{\Delta p}{p} = \frac{1}{15000}$$

This resolution is sufficient to separate individual nuclear excited states in missing-mass spectra, even for reactions on exotic nuclei with dense level schemes.

## Unique Capability

The combination of OEDO + SHARAQ is currently the **only facility worldwide** that couples achromatic energy degradation of RI beams with high-resolution missing-mass spectroscopy. This enables precision nuclear structure studies at low beam energies (10--50 MeV/u) on short-lived isotopes that cannot be made into fixed targets.

## Physics Program

- **[[Transfer Reactions|Nucleon transfer]]** $(d,p)$, $(p,d)$: extract [[Spectroscopic Factor|spectroscopic factors]] and single-particle structure via [[DWBA]] analysis
- **[[Charge-Exchange Reaction|Double charge-exchange]]**: search for the [[Double Gamow-Teller Giant Resonance]] in reactions like ${}^{48}\text{Ca}({}^{12}\text{C}, {}^{12}\text{Be})$
- **[[Surrogate Reaction|Surrogate reactions]]**: $(d,p)$ as proxy for $(n,\gamma)$ to constrain [[r-process Nucleosynthesis|r-process]] reaction rates
- **Sub-barrier Coulomb excitation**: measure $B(E2)$ values and nuclear deformation

## Experimental Program

As of late 2025, proposals SHARAQ21--25 are approved or completed. SHARAQ19 data formed the basis of a PhD thesis (Jiatai Li, completed January 2026).
