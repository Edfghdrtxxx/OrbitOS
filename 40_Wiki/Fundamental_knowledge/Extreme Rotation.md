# Extreme Rotation

## Schematics

*No suitable open-license schematics found on Wikimedia Commons for this topic.*

## Definition

Extreme rotation refers to the regime where a nucleus is spun to very high angular momentum ($I \gtrsim 30\hbar$), causing dramatic structural changes — superdeformation, hyperdeformation, band termination, and ultimately fission — as centrifugal and Coriolis forces compete with the nuclear binding potential.

## Key Points

- **Superdeformation**: At certain "magic" angular momenta, shell effects stabilize nuclei at axis ratios of $\sim 2{:}1$ (prolate), creating superdeformed bands with characteristically uniform $\gamma$-ray spacings; first observed in ${}^{152}$Dy at $I \sim 60\hbar$
- **Backbending**: A sudden increase in the nuclear [[Moment of Inertia]] at a critical spin, caused by the breaking of a nucleon Cooper pair that re-aligns its angular momentum along the rotation axis (Coriolis anti-pairing effect); visible as a kink in plots of $2\mathcal{J}^{(1)}/\hbar^2$ versus $(\hbar\omega)^2$
- **Band termination**: At sufficiently high spin, all valence nucleons align their angular momenta and the nucleus can no longer generate collective rotation — the band "terminates" at a fully aligned, non-collective state
- **Hyperdeformation**: Predicted but rarely observed $3{:}1$ axis ratio shapes at the highest spins, near the fission limit

## Examples

- **${}^{152}$Dy**: Discovery of superdeformed band (1986, Daresbury) — a long sequence of equally-spaced $\gamma$-ray transitions forming a near-perfect rotational sequence
- **${}^{158}$Er**: Classic backbending nucleus — alignment of an $i_{13/2}$ neutron pair disrupts the ground-state band around $I \sim 14\hbar$
- **${}^{48}$Cr**: Band termination at $I = 16\hbar$ where all $f_{7/2}$ valence nucleons are fully aligned
