# Charge-Exchange Reaction
## Definition

A charge-exchange (CE) reaction is a nuclear reaction in which one or more units of electric charge are transferred between the projectile and target, changing both nuclei's proton/neutron composition while preserving total baryon number. CE reactions are the primary experimental tool for studying spin-isospin excitations in nuclei.

## Single Charge-Exchange (SCE)

SCE reactions probe $\Delta T_z = \pm 1$ excitations:

| Reaction | Direction | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| $(p,n)$ | $T_z - 1$ | Classic GT probe; free proton beam |
| $(n,p)$ | $T_z + 1$ | Neutron beam (quasi-free) |
| $({}^3\text{He}, t)$ | $T_z - 1$ | Light-ion alternative to $(p,n)$ |
| $(t, {}^3\text{He})$ | $T_z + 1$ | Light-ion alternative to $(n,p)$ |

At intermediate energies ($> 100$ MeV/u), the nucleon-nucleon interaction is dominated by one-pion exchange ($\boldsymbol{\sigma} \cdot \boldsymbol{\tau}$ character), making the forward-angle cross section directly proportional to the [[Gamow-Teller Transition|Gamow-Teller]] strength $B(GT)$. This proportionality is the basis of the "$(p,n)$ method."

## Key Excited Modes

- **Isobaric Analog State (IAS)**: Excited by the Fermi operator ($\boldsymbol{\tau}$ only); appears as a sharp, well-defined peak
- **GT Giant Resonance (GTGR)**: Excited by the GT operator ($\boldsymbol{\sigma} \cdot \boldsymbol{\tau}$); appears as a broad resonance above the IAS

## Double Charge-Exchange (DCX)

DCX reactions change nuclear charge by $\Delta Z = \pm 2$, connecting the same initial and final nuclear states as double-beta decay. Major programs include:

- **RIKEN**: $({}^{12}\text{C}, {}^{12}\text{Be})$ at 250 MeV/u -- high energy favors direct mechanism, simpler [[DWBA]] interpretation
- **NUMEN (INFN-LNS, Catania)**: $({}^{18}\text{O}, {}^{18}\text{Ne})$ and $({}^{20}\text{Ne}, {}^{20}\text{O})$ at 15--70 MeV/u -- systematic DCX survey on all $\beta\beta$ candidate nuclei

The fact that DCX and $0\nu\beta\beta$ share the same $(A, Z) \to (A, Z \pm 2)$ transition makes DCX a powerful tool for constraining the nuclear matrix elements needed to extract neutrino masses from $0\nu\beta\beta$ half-life measurements.
