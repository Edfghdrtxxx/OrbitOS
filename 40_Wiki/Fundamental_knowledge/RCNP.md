# RCNP

## Definition

The Research Center for Nuclear Physics (RCNP) is a joint-use research facility at Osaka University, Japan, operating a $K = 400\;\text{MeV}$ ring cyclotron and associated beam lines for nuclear and hadron physics experiments. It is one of the major accelerator facilities in Japan for intermediate-energy nuclear physics.

## Key Points

- The ring cyclotron delivers proton beams up to ~400 MeV and heavy-ion beams, supporting a wide range of nuclear reaction and structure studies
- Hosts the Grand Raiden spectrometer for high-resolution spectroscopy, and multiple experimental halls for detector R&D and nuclear reaction experiments
- Home to Active Target [[Time Projection Chamber|TPC]] (ATPC) experiments that combine beam and target functions inside the detector volume for studying direct reactions with radioactive beams
- Maintains international collaborations with researchers from Europe, Asia, and the Americas

## Examples

- The RCNP ATPC collaboration conducts experiments using active-target techniques to study nuclear reactions such as $(d, p)$ transfer reactions on unstable isotopes
- RCNP beam time is used for detector calibration and commissioning runs, with data transferred to partner institutions (e.g., MSU) for analysis
