# High-spin States

## Schematics

![[High-spin_States_Lu177m_isomer_levels.gif]]

## Definition

High-spin states are nuclear excited states with large angular momentum quantum numbers ($I \gtrsim 10\hbar$), populated by reactions that transfer significant angular momentum â€” typically heavy-ion [[Fusion-Evaporation Reaction|fusion-evaporation]] or multi-nucleon [[Transfer Reactions|transfer]] â€” and studied via in-beam $\gamma$-ray spectroscopy to map rotational bands and single-particle structure.

## Key Points

- **Yrast line**: The locus of lowest-energy states at each spin value $I$; high-spin spectroscopy systematically maps yrast and near-yrast states to reveal structural evolution with angular momentum
- **Population mechanism**: Heavy-ion fusion-evaporation reactions (e.g., ${}^{48}$Ca + ${}^{124}$Sn) populate states at $I \sim 40$â€“$70\hbar$ and high excitation energy; the nucleus then cools by neutron evaporation and $\gamma$-ray emission along or near the yrast line
- **Detection**: Large $\gamma$-ray detector arrays (EUROBALL, Gammasphere, AGATA) with Compton suppression and tracking measure the $\gamma$-ray cascades; coincidence analysis reconstructs level schemes
- **Isomers**: High-spin states can be [[Half-Life|long-lived]] ([[Nuclear Isomer|isomeric]]) when large spin differences to lower states suppress electromagnetic decay â€” "spin traps" with lifetimes from nanoseconds to years (e.g., ${}^{178m2}$Hf at $16^+$, $t_{1/2} = 31$ yr)

## Examples

- **${}^{177m}$Lu** ($23/2^-$, 970 keV): High-spin isomer decaying via a $\gamma$-ray cascade through multiple intermediate states to the ${}^{177}$Hf ground state
- **${}^{178m2}$Hf** ($16^+$, 2.446 MeV): One of the longest-lived high-spin isomers known ($t_{1/2} = 31$ years), trapped by the large spin difference to accessible lower states
- **In-beam spectroscopy at RIKEN**: The [[OEDO]]-[[SHARAQ Spectrometer|SHARAQ]] system and detector arrays like [[GRAPE]] enable high-spin studies of exotic nuclei produced as [[Radioactive Isotope Beam|RI beams]]
