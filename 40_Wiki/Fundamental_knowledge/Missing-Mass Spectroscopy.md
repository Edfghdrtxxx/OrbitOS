# Missing-Mass Spectroscopy

## Definition

Missing-mass spectroscopy is an experimental technique in nuclear and particle physics that determines the excitation energy of an unobserved reaction product by measuring only the outgoing light particle (the ejectile) with high precision, using conservation of four-momentum to reconstruct the "missing mass" — the invariant mass of everything not directly detected.

## Key Points

- For a two-body reaction $A(a, b)B$, the excitation energy $E_x$ of the heavy residue $B$ is obtained from $E_x = \sqrt{s} - m_b - m_B^{(\text{g.s.})} - T_{\text{cm}}$, where all kinematic quantities on the right are determined by measuring the ejectile $b$ in a high-resolution [[Magnetic Spectrometer]]
- The technique requires only the ejectile to reach the detector, making it uniquely suited for studying states in short-lived or particle-unbound residues that decay before reaching any detector
- Momentum resolution of the spectrometer directly limits the excitation-energy resolution; the [[SHARAQ Spectrometer]] at [[RIKEN Nishina Center for Accelerator-Based Science (RNC)|RIKEN RIBF]] achieves $p/\Delta p \sim 15000$, enabling resolution of individual nuclear levels
- In inverse kinematics (heavy beam on light target), the ejectile is a light particle (e.g., proton, deuteron, triton) detected at large lab angles or with a recoil spectrometer, requiring careful [[Track Reconstruction]] and [[Ion Optics]] matching
- Commonly used with [[Direct Reactions]] such as [[Transfer Reactions]] ($(d,p)$, $(p,d)$), inelastic scattering ($(p,p')$), and [[Charge-Exchange Reaction|charge-exchange reactions]] ($(p,n)$, $({}^3\text{He}, t)$)

## Examples

- $(d,p)$ transfer on a ${}^{132}\text{Sn}$ [[Radioactive Isotope Beam|RI beam]] at OEDO+[[SHARAQ Spectrometer|SHARAQ]]: the proton is detected in SHARAQ's focal plane, and the missing mass reveals the single-neutron states of ${}^{133}\text{Sn}$ — including states above the neutron separation energy that cannot be observed in bound-state spectroscopy
- Grand Raiden at [[RCNP]] performs $(p,p')$ missing-mass spectroscopy to map giant resonances with $\Delta E \sim 30$ keV resolution
