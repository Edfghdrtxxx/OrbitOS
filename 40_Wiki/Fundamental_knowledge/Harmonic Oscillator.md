# Harmonic Oscillator

## Definition

The harmonic oscillator is a system subject to a restoring force proportional to displacement, with potential $V(x) = \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2 x^2$. In quantum mechanics, its energy levels are exactly solvable: $E_n = \hbar\omega(n + \frac{1}{2})$, with equally spaced, non-degenerate levels. It serves as a foundational model across physics.

## Key Points

- The quantum harmonic oscillator is solved elegantly via ladder operators $\hat{a}^\dagger$ and $\hat{a}$, yielding the Fock state basis $|n\rangle$
- In 3D (isotropic), the energy levels are $E_N = \hbar\omega(N + \frac{3}{2})$ with degeneracy $(N+1)(N+2)/2$, producing shell closures at 2, 8, 20, 40, 70, ... — the first three match [[Magic Numbers]] but higher ones do not
- Adding [[Spin-Orbit Coupling]] and an $l^2$ correction to the 3D harmonic oscillator potential reproduces the correct nuclear shell structure in the [[Nuclear Shell Model]]
- The coherent state $|\alpha\rangle$ (eigenstate of $\hat{a}$) is the closest quantum analogue to classical oscillation
- Nearly any potential near a stable equilibrium can be approximated as harmonic to leading order (Taylor expansion)

## Examples

- A diatomic molecule's vibrational modes are well-described by a quantum harmonic oscillator, with infrared absorption at frequency $\omega$
- The 3D harmonic oscillator is the starting point for the [[Nuclear Shell Model]] before spin-orbit corrections
