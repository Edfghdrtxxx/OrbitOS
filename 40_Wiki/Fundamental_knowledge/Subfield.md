# Subfield

## Definition

A **subfield** of a [[Field (mathematics)|field]] $F$ is a subset $K \subseteq F$ that is itself a field under the same addition and multiplication operations inherited from $F$.

## Key Points

- $\mathbb{Q}$ is a subfield of $\mathbb{R}$, and $\mathbb{R}$ is a subfield of $\mathbb{C}$, giving the chain $\mathbb{Q} \subset \mathbb{R} \subset \mathbb{C}$
- A subfield must contain the [[Identity Element|additive identity]] $0$ and [[Identity Element|multiplicative identity]] $1$ of the parent field
- A subfield must be closed under addition, multiplication, and taking [[Inverse Element|inverses]]
- The concept parallels [[Subspace]] in [[Vector_Spaces|vector spaces]] — both are "smaller structures that inherit operations from a larger one"

## Examples

- $\mathbb{Q} \subset \mathbb{R}$: every rational number computation stays rational, and $\mathbb{Q}$ satisfies all field axioms
- $\mathbb{Z} \subset \mathbb{Q}$: the integers are **not** a subfield of $\mathbb{Q}$ because $\mathbb{Z}$ is not a field (no multiplicative inverses)
