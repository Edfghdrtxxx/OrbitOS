# Radiation Exit Survey

## Definition

A radiation exit survey is the mandatory set of measurements performed when personnel and objects leave a [[Radiation Controlled Area]], covering three distinct checks: body [[Surface Contamination]], object surface contamination, and object [[Neutron Activation]] status.

## Key Points

- **Body contamination check** — hands, feet, and any body part potentially exposed; uses hand-foot-clothing monitors or portable survey meters
- **Object contamination check** — all items carried out are surveyed for removable and fixed [[Surface Contamination]] via smear tests and direct measurement
- **Object activation check** — separate from contamination; determines if the object itself has become radioactive through [[Neutron Activation]] (especially critical at accelerator facilities)
- The person carrying objects out is also exiting, so both the person and the objects must be checked — they pass through the same checkpoint

## Examples

- A researcher leaving the RCNP cyclotron hall must check their hands/feet at the monitor AND have their tools surveyed for contamination and activation before exiting
- Taking a laptop out of the controlled area at [[Institute of Modern Physics]] — survey for surface contamination and confirm no activation
