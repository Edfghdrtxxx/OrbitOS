# Pi Meson

## Definition

The pi meson (pion, symbol $\pi$) is the lightest [[Hadron|hadron]] and the primary mediator of the long-range [[Nuclear Force|nuclear force]] between nucleons, as predicted by Yukawa's theory (1935). It exists in three charge states: $\pi^+$, $\pi^-$, and $\pi^0$.

## Key Points

- Quark content: $\pi^+ = u\bar{d}$, $\pi^- = d\bar{u}$, $\pi^0 \approx \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(u\bar{u} - d\bar{d})$; all are spin-0 [[Pseudoscalar|pseudoscalar]] mesons ($J^P = 0^-$)
- Masses: $m_{\pi^\pm} \approx 139.6\;\text{MeV}/c^2$, $m_{\pi^0} \approx 135.0\;\text{MeV}/c^2$; the lightest mesons due to their role as approximate [[Goldstone Boson|Goldstone bosons]] of [[Chiral Symmetry Breaking|chiral symmetry breaking]]
- The pion exchange generates the **tensor component** of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, which is the dominant driver of [[Shell Evolution]] — shifting single-particle energy levels and reshuffling [[Magic Numbers]] in exotic nuclei
- Dominant decays: $\pi^\pm \to \mu^\pm + \nu_\mu$ ($\tau \approx 26\;\text{ns}$); $\pi^0 \to 2\gamma$ ($\tau \approx 8.4 \times 10^{-17}\;\text{s}$)

## Examples

- In Yukawa's original theory, the range of the nuclear force $\sim \hbar / m_\pi c \approx 1.4\;\text{fm}$ matches the observed range of the strong nucleon-nucleon interaction
- In [[Shell Evolution]], the $\pi$-exchange tensor force between proton and neutron [[Spin-Orbit Coupling|spin-orbit]] partners drives the migration of shell gaps far from stability
