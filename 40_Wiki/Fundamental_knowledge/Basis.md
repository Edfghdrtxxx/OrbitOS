# Basis

## Definition

A **basis** of a [[Vector_Spaces|vector space]] $V$ is a set of vectors that is both [[Linear Independence|linearly independent]] and [[Span|spans]] $V$. Every vector in $V$ can be written as a unique [[Linear Combination]] of basis vectors.

## Key Points

- A basis is the **minimal spanning set** and the **maximal independent set** simultaneously
- All bases of a finite-dimensional space have the same number of vectors — this number is the [[Dimension]]
- The standard basis of $\mathbb{R}^n$ is $\{\hat{e}_1, \ldots, \hat{e}_n\}$, but infinitely many other bases exist
- In QM, eigenstates $|n\rangle$ of the Hamiltonian form a basis: $|\psi\rangle = \sum_n c_n |n\rangle$

## Examples

- Standard basis of $\mathbb{R}^3$: $\{(1,0,0), (0,1,0), (0,0,1)\}$
- $\{(2,1,0), (-1,0,1)\}$ is a basis for the [[Subspace]] $\{(x,y,z) \mid x - 2y + z = 0\}$
