# Surface Contamination

## Definition

Surface contamination is radioactive material deposited on the surface of objects or body parts, either as **removable** (loose, transferable) or **fixed** (bound to the surface) contamination. It is distinct from [[Neutron Activation]], where the material itself has become radioactive.

## Key Points

- **Removable contamination** can be transferred by touch or airborne dispersal — detected via smear tests (wiping the surface and measuring the wipe)
- **Fixed contamination** is bound to the surface and cannot be easily transferred — detected by direct survey with a radiation detector held close to the surface
- Checked on both personnel (hands, feet, clothing) and objects when leaving a [[Radiation Controlled Area]]
- Common sources: handling unsealed radioisotopes, contact with activated components, airborne deposition

## Examples

- Radioactive dust settling on tools left near a beamline
- Skin contamination on hands after handling activated target materials without gloves
