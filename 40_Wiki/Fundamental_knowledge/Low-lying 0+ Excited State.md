## Definition

A low-lying $0^+$ excited state is a nuclear excited state with spin-parity $J^\pi = 0^+$ that occurs at relatively low excitation energy (typically a few hundred keV to ~2 MeV above the ground state), indicating an alternative nuclear configuration — usually a different shape — that is nearly as energetically favorable as the ground state.

## Key Points

- Every [[Even-Even Nucleus|even-even nucleus]] has a $0^+$ ground state because protons and neutrons pair up, canceling their individual [[Angular Momentum|angular momenta]]; a $0^+$ excited state shares the same [[Quantum Numbers]] but has a distinct internal configuration
- The presence of a low-lying $0^+$ state is the hallmark experimental signature of [[Deformation Coexistence]] (shape coexistence): it signals that two different nuclear shapes coexist at nearly the same energy, arising from competition between [[Shell closure|shell-closure]] stabilization and collective correlations
- Transitions between $0^+$ states occur exclusively via [[E0 Transition|E0 (electric monopole) transitions]], which are uniquely sensitive to changes in the nuclear mean-square charge radius $\langle r^2 \rangle$ — a direct probe of shape change
- These states often originate from [[Intruder States|intruder configurations]], where nucleons are promoted across a [[Magic Numbers|major shell gap]], creating a deformed minimum in the [[Potential Energy Surface]]
- Systematic surveys of $0^+_2$ energies across isotopic chains reveal regions of strong shape coexistence, particularly near $Z \approx 40$, $Z \approx 82$, and in neutron-deficient Cd/Sn isotopes

## Examples

- The Hoyle state in $^{12}$C ($0^+_2$ at 7.65 MeV) is the most famous $0^+$ excited state, critical for stellar carbon synthesis via the triple-alpha process — though at higher energy, it illustrates how a $0^+$ state reflects a fundamentally different structure (three-alpha cluster configuration)
- In $^{186}$Pb, three $0^+$ states (spherical, oblate, prolate) lie within 650 keV, demonstrating triple shape coexistence detected through large $\rho^2(E0)$ values
