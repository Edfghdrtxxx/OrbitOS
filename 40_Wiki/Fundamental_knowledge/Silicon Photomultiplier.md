# Silicon Photomultiplier

## Definition

A Silicon Photomultiplier (SiPM) is a solid-state [[Photodetector]] composed of an array of [[Single-Photon Avalanche Diode]]s (SPADs) operating in [[Geiger Mode]], capable of detecting single photons with high gain. It serves as a compact, magnetic-field-immune replacement for the traditional [[Photomultiplier Tube]].

## Key Points

- Each micro-cell (SPAD) fires independently in [[Geiger Mode]] and the outputs are summed, giving a signal proportional to the number of detected photons
- Compact form factor (mm-scale) and low operating voltage (~30 V) compared to the [[Photomultiplier Tube]] (~1 kV)
- Immune to magnetic fields, making SiPMs ideal for detectors inside solenoid magnets where [[Photomultiplier Tube]]s fail
- Commonly coupled to [[Scintillation Detector]]s for [[Gamma Spectroscopy]], PET medical imaging, and [[Time-of-Flight]] measurements
- [[Dark Count Rate]] and [[Optical Crosstalk]] are the primary noise sources that limit performance

## Examples

- In PET scanners, SiPMs coupled to LYSO crystals ([[Scintillation Detector]]) provide sub-nanosecond timing for [[Time-of-Flight]] PET
- Large-area SiPM arrays read out plastic scintillator tiles in high-energy physics calorimeters operating inside strong magnetic fields
