# Associativity

## Definition

**Associativity** is an algebraic property stating that the grouping of operands does not affect the result: $(a \circ b) \circ c = a \circ (b \circ c)$. An operation satisfying this is called **associative**.

## Key Points

- Both addition and multiplication in a [[Field (mathematics)|field]] must be associative
- Associativity allows writing $a + b + c$ without parentheses because all groupings yield the same result
- [[Matrix Operations|Matrix multiplication]] is associative: $(AB)C = A(BC)$, even though it is not commutative
- Together with [[Commutativity]] and [[Distributivity]], associativity is one of the three core axioms defining a [[Field (mathematics)|field]]

## Examples

- $(2 + 3) + 4 = 2 + (3 + 4) = 9$ — addition on $\mathbb{R}$ is associative
- Vector cross product is **not** associative: $(\vec{a} \times \vec{b}) \times \vec{c} \neq \vec{a} \times (\vec{b} \times \vec{c})$ in general
