# Neutron Activation

## Definition

Neutron activation is the process by which a stable nucleus captures a neutron and becomes a radioactive isotope, making the material itself emit radiation. Unlike [[Surface Contamination]], the radioactivity is intrinsic to the material — it cannot be wiped off.

## Key Points

- Occurs at accelerator facilities (cyclotrons, reactors) where intense neutron fields are present
- The activated isotope decays with its own half-life, which can range from seconds to years depending on the product nucleus
- Detection requires gamma spectroscopy or dose-rate surveys, not smear tests (since there is nothing removable on the surface)
- Must be checked separately from [[Surface Contamination]] during a [[Radiation Exit Survey]] — a clean smear test does NOT mean an object is safe

## Examples

- Steel shielding near a cyclotron beamline: ⁵⁶Fe(n,p)⁵⁶Mn — the iron nucleus captures a neutron, emits a proton, and becomes radioactive manganese-56
- Aluminum beam pipes developing ²⁴Na activity via ²⁷Al(n,α)²⁴Na after prolonged neutron exposure at RCNP (Osaka University)
