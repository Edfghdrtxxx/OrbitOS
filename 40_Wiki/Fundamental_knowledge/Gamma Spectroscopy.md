# Gamma Spectroscopy

## Definition

Gamma spectroscopy is an analytical technique that measures the energy distribution of gamma rays emitted by [[Radioisotope]]s, producing a spectrum of peaks that uniquely identify the isotopes present and their activities (in [[Becquerel]]).

## Key Points

- Uses high-purity germanium (HPGe) detectors for best energy resolution, or NaI(Tl) scintillators for portable/field measurements
- Each [[Radioisotope]] emits gamma rays at characteristic energies — e.g., $^{60}\text{Co}$ at $1173\;\text{keV}$ and $1332\;\text{keV}$, $^{24}\text{Na}$ at $1369\;\text{keV}$ and $2754\;\text{keV}$
- Essential for identifying [[Neutron Activation]] products in objects leaving a [[Radiation Controlled Area]] — determines *which* isotopes are present and *how much*
- Also used in nuclear physics research for studying excited nuclear states, complementing work on [[Binding Energy]] and [[Shell closure]]

## Examples

- Measuring an activated steel sample to identify $^{54}\text{Mn}$, $^{56}\text{Mn}$, $^{58}\text{Co}$, and $^{60}\text{Co}$ peaks — determining which activation products dominate and their activities
- Portable NaI detector used at exit checkpoints to screen objects for activation before release from the controlled area
