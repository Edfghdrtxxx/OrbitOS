# Distributivity

## Definition

**Distributivity** is the algebraic property that connects two operations, typically addition and multiplication: $a \times (b + c) = a \times b + a \times c$. It ensures multiplication "distributes" over addition.

## Key Points

- A [[Field (mathematics)|field]] requires multiplication to distribute over addition from both sides (left and right distributivity)
- Distributivity is essential for expanding and factoring expressions, e.g., $3(x + 2) = 3x + 6$
- In [[Vector_Spaces|vector spaces]], scalar multiplication distributes over vector addition: $\alpha(\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v}) = \alpha\mathbf{u} + \alpha\mathbf{v}$
- Paired with [[Commutativity]] and [[Associativity]], distributivity completes the axiom triad of [[Field (mathematics)|fields]]

## Examples

- $2 \times (3 + 5) = 2 \times 3 + 2 \times 5 = 16$ — standard distributive law on $\mathbb{R}$
- Dot product distributes over vector addition: $\vec{a} \cdot (\vec{b} + \vec{c}) = \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} + \vec{a} \cdot \vec{c}$
