# RIBLL

## Definition

RIBLL (Radioactive Ion Beam Line in Lanzhou) refers to the two in-flight [[Fragment Separator]]s at [[HIRFL]] that produce [[Radioactive Isotope Beam]]s via [[Projectile Fragmentation]]. RIBLL1 is coupled to the SFC-SSC cyclotron chain; RIBLL2 connects the [[CSR|CSRm]] synchrotron to downstream experimental areas and the [[CSR|CSRe]] storage ring.

## Key Points

- **RIBLL1** (operational since 1998): A 35 m antisymmetric double-achromatic beam line with 4 dipoles, 16 quadrupoles, and 5 focal planes (F0--F4). Maximum [[Magnetic Rigidity|$B\rho_{\max}$]] = 4.2 T$\cdot$m, momentum acceptance $\Delta p/p = \pm 5\%$, angular acceptance $\geq 6.5$ msr. Delivers [[Secondary Beam]]s at up to ~100 MeV/u from SSC (Sun et al., 1999; 2003).
- **RIBLL2** (at HIRFL-CSR): A 55 m beam line with 2 superconducting dipoles (among others) and 5 focal planes (F0--F4). $B\rho_{\max}$ = 10.64 T$\cdot$m, $\Delta p/p = \pm 1\%$, angular acceptance $\pm 25$ mrad. Operates at 300--400 MeV/u, one of the few separators worldwide capable of delivering RIBs above 300 MeV/u (Sun et al., 2018; Tang et al., 2025).
- **Separation method:** Both lines use the $B\rho$-$\Delta E$-$B\rho$ technique -- first [[Magnetic Rigidity]] selection, then a wedge-shaped [[Achromatic Degrader]] for $Z$-dependent energy loss via the [[Bethe-Bloch Formula]], followed by a second $B\rho$ selection to achieve both mass and charge selectivity.
- **RIBLL2 focal-plane detectors:** F1 (plastic scintillator timing + strip array position), F2 (MWPC beam monitoring, location of the External Target Facility), F3 (MWPC for $B\rho$ reconstruction), F4 (plastic scintillator timing stop + MWDCs + [[MUSIC Detector|MUSIC]] chambers for $\Delta E$).
- The separated beam is typically a cocktail of isotopes, requiring event-by-event [[Particle Identification]] via the $\Delta E$-$B\rho$-ToF method at the focal planes.

## Examples

- At RIBLL1, a $^{36}$Ar primary beam on a $^{9}$Be target produces neutron-deficient species like $^{32}$Ar for nuclear structure studies at ~50 MeV/u
- At RIBLL2, $^{12}$C fragmentation at ~400 MeV/u produces neutron-rich carbon and boron isotopes for knockout and transfer reaction experiments at the ETF
