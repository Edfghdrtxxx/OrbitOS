# Fermi Transition

## Definition

A Fermi transition is a [[Nuclear Transition]] mediated by the vector part of the weak interaction, in which a nucleon changes its isospin (proton $\leftrightarrow$ neutron) without any change in spin. It is one of the two allowed beta-decay modes, the other being the [[Gamow-Teller Transition]] (which flips both spin and isospin).

## Key Points

- The Fermi operator is the pure isospin operator $\hat{O}_F = g_V \sum_k \boldsymbol{\tau}_k$, where $g_V = 1$ (exactly, by the conserved vector current hypothesis — no in-medium quenching, unlike the [[Gamow-Teller Transition|GT]] case)
- Selection rules: $\Delta J = 0$, $\Delta \pi = 0$ (no parity change), $\Delta T = 0$, $\Delta T_z = \pm 1$ — the nuclear spin and parity are unchanged, only the isospin projection shifts by one unit
- The dominant Fermi excitation is the **Isobaric Analog State (IAS)**: a state in the daughter nucleus with the same spatial and spin wavefunction as the parent, differing only by $T_z \to T_z \pm 1$, appearing as a sharp peak in [[Charge-Exchange Reaction]] spectra
- **Superallowed $0^+ \to 0^+$ Fermi decays** (e.g., ${}^{14}\text{O} \to {}^{14}\text{N}$, ${}^{26m}\text{Al} \to {}^{26}\text{Mg}$) are pure Fermi transitions used to determine $|V_{ud}|$ of the CKM matrix and to test the unitarity of the Standard Model
- The $ft$ values of superallowed Fermi decays, after radiative and isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections, yield the corrected $\mathcal{F}t$ value: $\mathcal{F}t = \frac{K}{2 G_F^2 |V_{ud}|^2 (1 + \Delta_R^V)}$

## Examples

- ${}^{14}\text{O}(0^+) \to {}^{14}\text{N}(0^+, \text{IAS at } 2.31~\text{MeV})$: a textbook superallowed Fermi decay with $ft = 3042$ s, used in the world average for $|V_{ud}|$
- In $(p,n)$ or $({}^3\text{He}, t)$ [[Charge-Exchange Reaction|charge-exchange reactions]], the IAS peak at forward angles is the Fermi-type excitation, clearly separated from the broader [[Gamow-Teller Transition|Gamow-Teller giant resonance]]
