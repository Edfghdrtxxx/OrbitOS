# Collective Motions

## Schematics

![[Collective_Motions_Nilsson_orbitals.svg]]
*Nilsson diagram for light nuclei: single-particle energy levels as a function of quadrupole deformation parameter $\epsilon_2$, showing how shell structure evolves with collective deformation. (CC BY-SA 4.0, Wikimedia)*

## Definition

Collective motions are coherent excitations of the nucleus in which many nucleons move in a correlated manner, producing macroscopic degrees of freedom — [[Deformation Coexistence|shape deformations]], rotations, and vibrations — described by the [[Liquid Drop Model]] extended with surface parameters $\alpha_{\lambda\mu}$.

## Key Points

- **Rotational bands**: Nuclei with stable axial deformation exhibit rotational energy spectra $E(I) = \frac{\hbar^2}{2\mathcal{J}} I(I+1)$, where $\mathcal{J}$ is the moment of inertia and $I$ the angular momentum quantum number; characteristic of prolate or oblate shapes
- **Vibrational modes**: Spherical or near-spherical nuclei oscillate about equilibrium shape; the lowest collective vibration is the quadrupole ($\lambda = 2$) phonon, with a one-phonon $2^+$ state and a two-phonon triplet ($0^+$, $2^+$, $4^+$) at roughly twice the energy
- **[[Giant Resonance|Giant resonances]]**: High-lying collective oscillations (e.g., Giant Dipole Resonance — protons oscillating against neutrons, or [[Gamow-Teller Transition|Gamow-Teller Giant Resonance]] — spin-isospin mode) are among the most studied collective excitations
- **Transition strengths**: Large $B(E2)$ values for low-lying $2^+ \to 0^+$ transitions are the experimental hallmark of collectivity, measured via [[Coulomb Excitation]] or lifetime methods

## Examples

- **Rotational**: The ground-state band of ${}^{166}$Er follows $E \propto I(I+1)$ up to $I \sim 20\hbar$, a textbook rigid rotor
- **Vibrational**: ${}^{110}$Cd exhibits a near-harmonic two-phonon quadrupole triplet ($0^+$, $2^+$, $4^+$) at approximately twice the one-phonon $2^+$ energy
- **Transitional**: Nuclei near shell closures can transition between vibrational and rotational character as neutrons are added (e.g., Sm isotopes from $N = 82$ to $N = 92$)
