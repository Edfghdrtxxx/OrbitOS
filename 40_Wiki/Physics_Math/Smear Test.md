# Smear Test

## Definition

A smear test (also called a wipe test or swipe test) is a method for detecting removable [[Surface Contamination]] by wiping a defined area of a surface with filter paper or a cotton swab, then measuring the radioactivity transferred to the wipe.

## Key Points

- Detects only **removable** (loose) contamination — fixed contamination stays on the surface and requires a direct survey instead
- Standard procedure: wipe a 100 cm² area with moderate pressure, then count the wipe in a detector (e.g., liquid scintillation counter or proportional counter)
- Result is expressed in Bq/cm² (using [[Becquerel]]) and compared against regulatory clearance limits
- Part of the [[Radiation Exit Survey]] — used on objects leaving a [[Radiation Controlled Area]]

## Examples

- Wiping the surface of a wrench used near a beamline, then counting the filter paper to check for transferable contamination
- Routine monthly smear tests on laboratory benchtops to verify no [[Radioisotope]] has spread
