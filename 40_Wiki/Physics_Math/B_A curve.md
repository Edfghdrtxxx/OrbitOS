# B/A curve

## Definition

The B/A curve plots [[Binding Energy]] per nucleon versus mass number A. It summarizes how tightly nuclei are bound and why energy-releasing reactions move toward higher B/A.

## Key Points

- The curve rises steeply for light nuclei and peaks near iron (A ≈ 56)
- Fusion of light nuclei and fission of heavy nuclei release energy because both move nuclei toward higher B/A
- The [[Liquid Drop Model]] captures the smooth trend, while the [[40_Wiki/Physics_Math/Nuclear Shell Model]] explains local deviations at [[Nuclear Magic Numbers]]
- B/A is linked to [[Mass Defect]] through $E = mc^2$

## Examples

- $^4$He has relatively high B/A, reflecting strong stability
- $^{56}$Fe sits near the peak, making it a reference for reaction energetics
