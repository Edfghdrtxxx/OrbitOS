# Direct Reactions

## Definition

Direct reactions are nuclear reactions where the projectile interacts with one or a few nucleons in the target nucleus in a single step, occurring on a fast timescale ($\sim 10^{-22}$ s) comparable to the transit time of the projectile across the nucleus. They contrast with [[Compound Nucleus]] reactions, which proceed through a long-lived intermediate state.

## Key Points

- Peripheral in nature — occur at the nuclear surface, probing the [[Nuclear Structure]] of specific single-particle states
- Include [[Transfer Reactions]] (e.g., $(d,p)$, $(p,d)$), [[Knockout Reactions]] (e.g., $(p,2p)$), inelastic scattering, and charge-exchange reactions
- Angular distributions are forward-peaked and carry signatures of the transferred orbital angular momentum $\ell$, enabling spin-parity assignments
- Analyzed theoretically using the [[DWBA]] framework to extract [[Spectroscopic Factor|spectroscopic factors]]
- Essential tool for studying nuclei far from stability using radioactive ion beams in inverse kinematics

## Examples

- $^{14}\text{C}(d,p)^{15}\text{C}$: a $(d,p)$ [[Transfer Reactions|transfer reaction]] that adds a neutron to $^{14}\text{C}$, populating bound states of $^{15}\text{C}$
- Quasi-free scattering $(p,2p)$ at RIBF/RIKEN to study proton [[Spectroscopic Factor|spectroscopic factors]] of exotic nuclei
