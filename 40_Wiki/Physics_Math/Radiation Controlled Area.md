# Radiation Controlled Area

## Definition

A radiation controlled area is a designated zone where radiation levels or radioactive contamination may exceed normal limits, requiring access controls, dosimetry, and mandatory [[Radiation Exit Survey]] procedures for all personnel and objects leaving the area.

## Key Points

- Access is restricted to trained, registered personnel who have passed radiation safety education (e.g., comprehension tests at [[RCNP]])
- Personnel must wear dosimeters (personal radiation monitors) while inside
- All exits require surveys for [[Surface Contamination]] on bodies and objects, plus [[Neutron Activation]] checks on objects
- Defined by national regulations (in Japan: Act on Prevention of Radiation Hazards)

## Examples

- The cyclotron hall at RCNP (Osaka University) where beam experiments produce neutrons and secondary radiation
- Target rooms and beamline areas at [[Institute of Modern Physics]]
