# Quantum Numbers

## Definition

Quantum numbers are discrete values that specify the quantum state of a particle in a potential. For a nucleon or electron, the principal quantum number $n$, orbital angular momentum $l$, magnetic quantum number $m_l$, spin $s$, and total [[Angular Momentum]] $j$ collectively determine the energy, shape, and orientation of the state.

## Key Points

- $n$ (principal): labels the radial excitation; determines energy in hydrogen-like systems ($E_n \propto -1/n^2$)
- $l$ (orbital): magnitude of orbital [[Angular Momentum]], $l = 0, 1, \dots, n-1$; labeled $s, p, d, f, \dots$
- $m_l$ (magnetic): $z$-projection of $\vec{L}$, ranges from $-l$ to $+l$; determines behavior in magnetic fields
- $j$ (total): from [[Spin-Orbit Coupling]], $j = l \pm 1/2$ for spin-$1/2$ particles; each $j$ level holds $2j+1$ states
- In the [[40_Wiki/Physics_Math/Nuclear Shell Model]], the set $(n, l, j)$ labels each shell; the filling order determines nuclear properties

## Examples

- The $1d_{5/2}$ nuclear level: $n=1$, $l=2$ ($d$-wave), $j=5/2$, holding $2(5/2)+1 = 6$ nucleons
- Hydrogen $2p_{3/2}$: $n=2$, $l=1$, $j=3/2$ — split from $2p_{1/2}$ by [[Fine Structure]]
