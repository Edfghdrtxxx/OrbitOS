# Photodetector

## Definition

A photodetector is a device that converts [[Photon]]s (light) into electrical signals via the [[Photoelectric Effect]], semiconductor absorption, or related phenomena, enabling measurement of light intensity, timing, and spectral content.

## Key Points

- Fundamental building block for optical readout in [[Scintillation Detector]]s, spectroscopy, and imaging systems
- Types span vacuum devices ([[Photomultiplier Tube]]) and solid-state devices ([[Silicon Photomultiplier]], photodiodes)
- Key figures of merit: quantum efficiency, gain, dark current, and timing resolution
- Choice depends on wavelength range, required gain, [[Time-of-Flight]] needs, and magnetic-field environment

## Examples

- A [[Photomultiplier Tube]] coupled to NaI(Tl) forms a classic [[Gamma Spectroscopy]] detector
- [[Silicon Photomultiplier]] arrays replace PMTs in PET scanners where magnetic-field immunity is required
