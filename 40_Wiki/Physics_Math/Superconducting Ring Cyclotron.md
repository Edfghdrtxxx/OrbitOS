# Superconducting Ring Cyclotron

## Definition

The Superconducting Ring Cyclotron (SRC) is the final-stage [[Cyclotron|cyclotron]] in the accelerator cascade of the [[RIKEN Nishina Center for Accelerator-Based Science (RNC)|RIKEN]] RI Beam Factory (RIBF). It accelerates heavy ions from hydrogen to uranium up to ~345 MeV/nucleon, delivering the world's most intense heavy-ion beams to [[BigRIPS]] for radioactive isotope production via [[Projectile Fragmentation|projectile fragmentation]].

## Key Points

- World's first ring cyclotron using superconducting sector magnets — six sectors with a peak field of ~3.8 T, enabling a bending power of $K = 2600\;\text{MeV}$
- Operates as the fourth and final stage of the RIBF cascade: RILAC → fRC → IRC → **SRC**, each stage boosting beam energy
- Achieves extraction energies of ~345 MeV/nucleon for light ions (e.g., ${}^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$) and ~345 MeV/nucleon for ${}^{238}\mathrm{U}$, enabling reactions far above the [[Coulomb Barrier|Coulomb barrier]]
- Uses the [[Isochronous Cyclotron|isochronous]] principle: the radial magnetic field profile $B(r) \propto \gamma(r)$ compensates for relativistic mass increase, maintaining a constant orbit frequency
- Extraction radius ~5 m with an overall machine diameter of ~19 m; operational since 2007

## Examples

- The SRC accelerates ${}^{238}\mathrm{U}$ beams at 345 MeV/nucleon onto a beryllium target; [[BigRIPS]] then separates the fragments, enabling discovery of dozens of new neutron-rich isotopes
- Combined with the [[RIKEN Nishina Center for Accelerator-Based Science (RNC)|RIBF]] injector chain, the SRC delivers beam intensities exceeding $10^{11}$ particles per second for lighter ions like ${}^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$
