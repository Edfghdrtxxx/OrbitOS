# Transfer Reactions

## Definition

Transfer reactions are a class of [[Direct Reactions]] in which one or more nucleons are transferred between the projectile and target nucleus, selectively populating specific single-particle states in the residual nucleus.

## Key Points

- **Stripping reactions** transfer nucleon(s) from projectile to target (e.g., $(d,p)$ strips a neutron); **pickup reactions** remove nucleon(s) from the target (e.g., $(p,d)$ picks up a neutron)
- The angular distribution of the ejectile encodes the transferred orbital angular momentum $\ell$, allowing spin-parity ($J^\pi$) assignments of populated states
- Comparison of measured [[Differential Cross Section|differential cross sections]] with [[DWBA]] calculations yields [[Spectroscopic Factor|spectroscopic factors]] $C^2S$
- In inverse kinematics (beam = heavy ion, target = deuterium), used extensively with radioactive beams at facilities like RIBF and GANIL
- Sensitive to both occupied and unoccupied orbitals depending on reaction direction

## Examples

- $(d,p)$: the workhorse single-neutron stripping reaction — a deuteron gives its neutron to the target
- $({}^3\text{He},d)$: single-proton stripping, probing proton orbitals
- $(p,d)$: single-neutron pickup — a proton picks up a neutron from the target
