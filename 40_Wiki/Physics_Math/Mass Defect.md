# Mass Defect

## Definition

Mass defect is the difference between the sum of the free nucleon masses and the actual nuclear mass. Through $E = mc^2$, it corresponds directly to the [[Binding Energy]] of the nucleus.

## Key Points

- It is calculated as $\Delta m = Z m_p + N m_n - m_{\text{nucleus}}$
- Larger mass defect means a larger [[Binding Energy]] and greater stability
- The [[Liquid Drop Model]] predicts smooth trends in mass defect across nuclei
- Mass defect is often reported indirectly via mass tables and [[Mass Excess]]

## Examples

- The deuteron mass is less than the sum of a free proton and neutron; the difference is its binding energy
- $^4$He has a significant mass defect, reflecting its tightly bound structure
