# Neutrinoless Double-Beta Decay

> [!important] All math expressions, equations, and formulas **must** use LaTeX notation (`$...$` for inline, `$$...$$` for display blocks).

## Definition

Neutrinoless double-beta decay ($0\nu\beta\beta$) is a hypothetical nuclear transition in which two neutrons simultaneously convert into two protons and two electrons with no emitted neutrinos: $(A, Z) \to (A, Z+2) + 2e^-$. Its observation would prove that the neutrino is a Majorana particle (its own antiparticle) and that lepton number is not conserved, constituting physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Key Points

- **Standard vs. neutrinoless:** Ordinary two-neutrino double-beta decay ($2\nu\beta\beta$) emits two electron antineutrinos and has been observed in several isotopes; in $0\nu\beta\beta$ the virtual neutrino is exchanged internally, so the summed electron kinetic energy equals the full $Q$-value of the decay — producing a sharp peak rather than a continuous spectrum.
- **Majorana mass connection:** The $0\nu\beta\beta$ half-life is related to the effective Majorana neutrino mass $\langle m_{\beta\beta} \rangle$ by $\left(T_{1/2}^{0\nu}\right)^{-1} = G^{0\nu} \left| M^{0\nu} \right|^2 \langle m_{\beta\beta} \rangle^2$, where $G^{0\nu}$ is a phase-space factor and $M^{0\nu}$ is the [[Nuclear Matrix Element]].
- **Candidate isotopes:** Experiments focus on isotopes with large $Q$-values such as ${}^{76}\text{Ge}$ (GERDA/LEGEND), ${}^{136}\text{Xe}$ (KamLAND-Zen, nEXO), and ${}^{130}\text{Te}$ (CUORE), because higher $Q$-values increase the phase space and push the signal above most backgrounds.
- **Nuclear matrix element uncertainty:** Theoretical predictions of the [[Nuclear Matrix Element]] $M^{0\nu}$ vary by factors of 2--3 across different nuclear-structure models (shell model, QRPA, IBM), making precise extraction of $\langle m_{\beta\beta} \rangle$ from a measured [[Half-Life]] model-dependent.
- **Experimental signature:** A mono-energetic peak at the $Q$-value in the summed two-electron [[Energy Resolution]] spectrum, requiring ultra-low-background detectors with excellent energy resolution to distinguish from the $2\nu\beta\beta$ continuum.

## Examples

- **KamLAND-Zen (Japan):** Uses $\sim$750 kg of ${}^{136}\text{Xe}$ dissolved in liquid scintillator; currently sets one of the most stringent lower limits on $T_{1/2}^{0\nu} > 2.3 \times 10^{26}$ yr.
- **LEGEND-1000:** A next-generation ${}^{76}\text{Ge}$ experiment aiming for a half-life sensitivity beyond $10^{28}$ yr, deploying 1000 kg of enriched Ge detectors in liquid argon.
