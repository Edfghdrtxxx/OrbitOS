## Definition

The p-process is a collective term for the set of [[Photodisintegration]] reactions — primarily $(\gamma,n)$, $(\gamma,p)$, and $(\gamma,\alpha)$ — that produce the roughly 35 stable proton-rich isotopes heavier than iron known as [[p-nuclei]]. These isotopes lie on the proton-rich side of the [[Valley of Stability]] and cannot be synthesized by the [[s-process]] or [[r-process Nucleosynthesis|r-process]].

## Key Points

- Occurs at temperatures $T \gtrsim 2 \times 10^9$ K in the oxygen/neon-rich layers of core-collapse supernovae, where intense $\gamma$-ray fluxes photodisintegrate pre-existing [[s-process]] and [[r-process Nucleosynthesis|r-process]] seed nuclei
- The dominant channel is $(\gamma,n)$, which strips neutrons and drives nuclei toward the proton-rich side; subsidiary $(\gamma,p)$ and $(\gamma,\alpha)$ reactions create branching paths to lighter species
- Light p-nuclei (${}^{92,94}$Mo, ${}^{96,98}$Ru) are systematically underproduced in current models — the so-called "p-process puzzle" — suggesting additional mechanisms such as the [[rp-process]] or $\nu p$-process may contribute
- [[Coulomb Barrier]] effects strongly suppress proton and $\alpha$ capture at astrophysical energies, making photodisintegration the dominant production route rather than direct proton capture
- Abundance patterns of p-nuclei are sensitive to [[Nuclear Shell Model|nuclear shell structure]] and [[Magic Numbers]], particularly near the $N = 50$ and $Z = 50$ closures

## Examples

- ${}^{196}$Hg is a classic p-nucleus: it is shielded from $\beta$-decay chains of r-process products by stable ${}^{196}$Pt, so it can only be produced via photodisintegration of heavier Hg or Tl isotopes
- The abundance ratio of ${}^{92}$Mo/${}^{94}$Mo provides a sensitive diagnostic for constraining the astrophysical conditions (temperature, density, timescale) of the p-process site
