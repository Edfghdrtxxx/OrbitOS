# Fragment Separator

## Definition

A fragment separator is a magnetic beam line that selects desired exotic nuclei from the broad distribution of reaction products (the "cocktail beam") produced when a high-energy primary beam undergoes [[Projectile Fragmentation]] on a production target. It is the essential device that converts raw fragmentation output into usable [[Radioactive Isotope Beam]]s.

## Key Points

- **The $B\rho$-$\Delta E$-$B\rho$ method** is the standard in-flight separation technique (Geissel et al., 1992). A first set of dipole magnets selects ions by [[Magnetic Rigidity]] ($B\rho = p/Ze$), filtering by $A/Z$. A wedge-shaped [[Achromatic Degrader]] at the dispersive focal plane introduces $Z$-dependent energy loss via the [[Bethe-Bloch Formula]], differentiating isotopes that share similar $A/Z$. A second $B\rho$ selection then separates the now-differentiated ions, providing both mass and charge selectivity.
- **Speed:** Because fragments retain nearly the beam velocity and are forward-focused, in-flight separation occurs in microseconds -- enabling access to isotopes with sub-microsecond half-lives, a key advantage over the [[ISOL]] method.
- **Output:** Even after $B\rho$-$\Delta E$-$B\rho$ purification, the emerging [[Secondary Beam]] is typically a cocktail of several isotopic species, requiring event-by-event [[Particle Identification]] using $\Delta E$-$B\rho$-ToF measurements at the focal planes.
- **Design elements:** Fragment separators consist of superconducting or normal-conducting dipole magnets, quadrupole multiplets for focusing, sextupoles for aberration correction, and degrader stations. Multiple focal planes along the beam line host diagnostic detectors ([[PPAC]]s, plastic scintillators, [[MUSIC Detector|MUSIC]] chambers).

## Examples

- **[[BigRIPS]]** at RIKEN: The world's most powerful fragment separator, producing the most neutron-rich isotopes accessible to date using $^{238}$U fragmentation and fission
- **[[RIBLL]]** at [[HIRFL]]: RIBLL1 ($B\rho_{\max}$ = 4.2 T$\cdot$m, 35 m) and RIBLL2 ($B\rho_{\max}$ = 10.64 T$\cdot$m, 55 m) for RIB production at IMP
- **A1900** at FRIB (formerly NSCL): Large-acceptance separator for the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams
- **FRS** at GSI: The Fragment Separator that pioneered the $B\rho$-$\Delta E$-$B\rho$ method (Geissel et al., 1992)
