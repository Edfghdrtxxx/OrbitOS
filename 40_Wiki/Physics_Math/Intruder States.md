# Intruder States

## Schematics

*No suitable open-license schematics found on Wikimedia Commons for this topic.*

## Definition

Intruder states are nuclear excited states formed by promoting one or more nucleons across a [[Magic Numbers|major shell gap]], creating multi-particle multi-hole (np-nh) configurations that often produce a deformed minimum in the [[Potential Energy Surface|potential energy surface]] at low excitation energy.

## Key Points

- The energy cost of exciting nucleons across a shell gap is partially offset by the pairing and [[Quadrupole Correlation|quadrupole correlation]] energy gained from the additional valence particles and holes, which can drive collective deformation
- In the simplest picture, 2p-2h intruder configurations descend in energy as one moves toward midshell (more valence nucleons available for correlations), sometimes dropping below the normal-order spherical configuration — this produces [[Deformation Coexistence|shape coexistence]]
- Intruder states manifest experimentally as [[Low-lying 0+ Excited State|low-lying $0^+$ excited states]], enhanced $B(E2)$ values, and large [[E0 Transition|$\rho^2(E0)$]] monopole strengths between normal and intruder configurations
- The systematic lowering of intruder states is particularly dramatic near $Z \approx 82$ (Pb–Hg–Po region) and $Z \approx 40$ (Sr–Zr region), where proton excitations across the $Z = 82$ or $Z = 50$ gaps create strongly deformed bands
- [[Shell Evolution|Shell evolution]] driven by the proton-neutron tensor interaction can modify shell gaps, further facilitating intruder-state formation in exotic nuclei

## Examples

- In $^{186}$Pb, proton 2p-2h and 4p-4h excitations across the $Z = 82$ gap produce oblate and prolate intruder $0^+$ states coexisting with the spherical ground state within 650 keV
- The sudden onset of deformation at $^{100}$Zr ($N = 60$) is driven by proton intruder configurations from the $g_{9/2}$ orbital crossing the $Z = 40$ subshell gap
