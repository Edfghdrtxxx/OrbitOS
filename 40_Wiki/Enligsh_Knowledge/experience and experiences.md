# experience and experiences

## Definition

"Experience" distinguishes an uncountable sense (accumulated knowledge/skill) from a countable sense (individual events or episodes), a nuance similar to other word-choice contrasts like [[SourceVSResource]].

## Key Points

- Uncountable "experience" refers to overall skill or familiarity built over time (e.g., "She has experience with detectors").
- Countable "an experience" or plural "experiences" refers to specific events or episodes.
- As a verb, "to experience" means to undergo or feel something directly.
- Usage nuance is common in English distinctions; compare with [[Following_vs_As_Follows_vs_Subsequent]] for contrast-focused phrasing.

## Examples

- "I have five years of experience in data analysis" (uncountable skill).
- "Their travels were unforgettable experiences" (countable events).
