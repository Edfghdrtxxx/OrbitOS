# Source vs Resource

## Definition

In English word choice (see [[Academic_Word_List_AWL]]), a **source** is the origin or provider of something, while a **resource** is a usable asset relative to a goal. The contrast improves precision in academic writing and everyday explanations.

## Key Points

- **Source** answers “Where does this come from?” and does not depend on intention.
- **Resource** answers “What can I use to achieve X?” and is purpose-relative.
- Something can be a source without being a resource, and vice versa.
- Log usage examples in [[Daily Accumulation]] to keep the distinction active.

## Examples

- The sun is a source of energy; solar panels make that energy a resource for electricity.
- A professor can be a source of knowledge; lecture notes are a resource for studying.
