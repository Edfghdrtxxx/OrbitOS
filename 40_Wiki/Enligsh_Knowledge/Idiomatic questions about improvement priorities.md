# Idiomatic Questions About Improvement Priorities

## Definition

Idiomatic English question patterns that ask which area should be improved first or most, such as "the most important thing to improve" or "needs the most improvement". These forms help frame priorities naturally in feedback and planning.

## Key Points

- "What do you think is the most important thing to improve?" is a natural, idiomatic framing.
- "Needs the most improvement" is a compact alternative using the [[Needs improvement phrasing]] pattern.
- Use "important" for priority and "necessary" for requirements (see [[Important vs necessary (English usage)]]).
- Log successful priority questions in [[Daily Accumulation]] to build fluency.

## Examples

- "What do you think we should improve first?"
- "Which area needs the most improvement?"
