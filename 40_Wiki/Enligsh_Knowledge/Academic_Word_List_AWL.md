# Academic Word List (AWL)

## What Is the AWL?

The **Academic Word List** contains **570 word families** (Coxhead, 2000) that appear frequently across all academic disciplines. AWL words make up ~10% of tokens in academic texts — the same texts TOEFL draws from. Mastering them gives you a direct boost in Reading (vocabulary-in-context), Listening (catching key terms), Writing (sounding academic), and Speaking (signaling fluency).

> [!tip] How to Use This Note
> 1. Work through one category per study session (don't cram all at once)
> 2. For each word: read the example, check the word family, then **write your own sentence** using your physics/ML context
> 3. Add mastered words to your [[Daily Accumulation]] log
> 4. Review using spaced repetition (Anki) per [[TOEFL_Study_Plan#Vocabulary (ongoing throughout all phases)|Phase 1 plan]]

## Category A — Research & Analysis

Words you encounter constantly in physics papers, your [[Masters_Thesis]], and academic lectures.

### Core Words

- [x] **analyze** /ˈæn.ə.laɪz/
  - *v.* break down into components — 分析
  - Family: analysis (n.), analytical (adj.), analytically (adv.)
  - Collocations: analyze data, analyze results, statistically analyze
  - *We **analyzed** the TPC detector signals to extract particle identification features.*

- [x] **approach** /əˈproʊtʃ/
  - *n./v.* a way of dealing with something; to come near — 方法；接近
  - Family: approachable (adj.)
  - Collocations: data-driven approach, novel approach, approach the problem
  - *A domain-adaptation **approach** (DANN) proved more robust than direct transfer.*

- [x] **assess** /əˈsɛs/
  - *v.* evaluate or estimate — 评估
  - Family: assessment (n.), assessable (adj.)
  - Collocations: assess performance, assess risk, assess the impact
  - *We **assessed** the ResNet model's accuracy on unseen particle events.*

- [x] **indicate** /ˈɪn.dɪ.keɪt/
  - *v.* point out, show, or suggest — 表明
  - Family: indication (n.), indicative (adj.), indicator (n.)
  - Collocations: results indicate, data indicates, clearly indicate
  - *The chi-squared test **indicates** a statistically significant difference between the two distributions.*

- [x] **significant** /sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kənt/
  - *adj.* large enough to matter; meaningful — 显著的；重要的
  - Family: significance (n.), significantly (adv.), signify (v.)
  - Collocations: statistically significant, significant improvement, significant difference
  - *A **significant** improvement in PID accuracy was observed after retraining with augmented data.*

- [x] **demonstrate** /ˈdɛm.ən.streɪt/
  - *v.* show clearly, prove — 证明；展示
  - Family: demonstration (n.), demonstrable (adj.), demonstrably (adv.)
  - Collocations: demonstrate that, clearly demonstrate, demonstrate the feasibility
  - *Our experiment **demonstrates** that the ViT architecture outperforms ResNet on low-statistics datasets.*

- [x] **evaluate** /ɪˈvæl.ju.eɪt/
  - *v.* judge the quality or value — 评价
  - Family: evaluation (n.), evaluative (adj.)
  - Collocations: evaluate performance, evaluate the model, evaluate against
  - *We **evaluated** each classifier against the same validation set.*

- [x] **establish** /ɪˈstæb.lɪʃ/
  - *v.* set up or prove — 建立；确立
  - Family: establishment (n.), established (adj.)
  - Collocations: establish a baseline, well-established, establish criteria
  - *We **established** a baseline before varying the drift field parameters.*

- [x] **derive** /dɪˈraɪv/
  - *v.* obtain from a source; work out logically — 推导；源自
  - Family: derivation (n.), derivative (n./adj.)
  - Collocations: derive an equation, derive from, derived quantity
  - *The cross-section formula is **derived** from first principles of quantum scattering theory.*

- [x] **consist** /kənˈsɪst/
  - *v.* be composed of — 由……组成
  - Family: consistent (adj.), consistency (n.), consistently (adv.)
  - Collocations: consist of, consistent with, remain consistent
  - *The dataset **consists** of 50,000 simulated particle events.*

## Category B — Argumentation & Reasoning

Essential for TOEFL Writing/Speaking and academic discussion — structuring arguments, making claims, supporting them.

### Core Words

- [x] **substantiate** /səbˈstæn.ʃi.eɪt/
  - *v.* provide evidence to support — 证实
  - Family: substantiation (n.), substantive (adj.), substantially (adv.)
  - Collocations: substantiate a claim, substantiate with data
  - *You need calibration data to **substantiate** claims about detector resolution.*

- [x] **corroborate** /kəˈrɒb.ə.reɪt/
  - *v.* confirm or support with evidence — 佐证
  - Family: corroboration (n.), corroborative (adj.)
  - Collocations: corroborate findings, independently corroborate
  - *Independent Monte Carlo simulations **corroborate** the experimental results.*

- [x] **justify** /ˈdʒʌs.tɪ.faɪ/
  - *v.* show to be right or reasonable — 证明……正当
  - Family: justification (n.), justifiable (adj.), justifiably (adv.)
  - Collocations: justify the choice, justify the approach, hard to justify
  - ***Justify** your choice of ResNet over ViT with benchmark comparisons.*

- [x] **contradict** /ˌkɒn.trəˈdɪkt/
  - *v.* assert the opposite; be inconsistent with — 矛盾；反驳
  - Family: contradiction (n.), contradictory (adj.)
  - Collocations: contradict the hypothesis, contradict previous findings
  - *The new measurement **contradicts** earlier theoretical predictions.*

- [x] **imply** /ɪmˈplaɪ/
  - *v.* suggest indirectly — 暗示；意味着
  - Family: implication (n.), implicit (adj.), implicitly (adv.)
  - Collocations: imply that, the results imply, implication for
  - *A low p-value **implies** the null hypothesis should be rejected.*

- [x] **conclude** /kənˈkluːd/
  - *v.* bring to an end; reach a judgment — 总结；得出结论
  - Family: conclusion (n.), conclusive (adj.), conclusively (adv.)
  - Collocations: conclude that, in conclusion, draw a conclusion, conclusive evidence
  - *We **conclude** that the systematic uncertainty is dominated by the energy calibration.*

- [x] **pragmatic** /præɡˈmæt.ɪk/
  - *adj.* dealing with things realistically — 务实的
  - Family: pragmatism (n.), pragmatist (n.), pragmatically (adv.)
  - Collocations: pragmatic approach, pragmatic solution
  - *A **pragmatic** approach to PhD funding combines RIKEN JRA with part-time TA work.*

- [ ] **ambivalent** /æmˈbɪv.ə.lənt/
  - *adj.* having mixed feelings — 矛盾的
  - Family: ambivalence (n.), ambivalently (adv.)
  - Collocations: feel ambivalent about, ambivalent attitude
  - *Many graduates feel **ambivalent** about choosing between industry and academia.*

## Category C — Change, Growth & Process

Words describing how things increase, decrease, change, or develop — common in both TOEFL passages and scientific writing.

### Core Words

- [ ] **exacerbate** /ɪɡˈzæs.ər.beɪt/
  - *v.* make worse — 加剧
  - Family: exacerbation (n.)
  - Collocations: exacerbate the problem, further exacerbate
  - *Noise in the readout electronics **exacerbates** the particle misidentification rate.*

- [ ] **proliferate** /prəˈlɪf.ə.reɪt/
  - *v.* increase rapidly in number — 激增
  - Family: proliferation (n.), prolific (adj.)
  - Collocations: proliferate rapidly, nuclear proliferation, proliferation of
  - *AI-assisted analysis tools have **proliferated** across nuclear physics labs.*

- [ ] **mitigate** /ˈmɪt.ɪ.ɡeɪt/
  - *v.* make less severe — 缓解
  - Family: mitigation (n.), mitigating (adj.)
  - Collocations: mitigate risk, mitigate the effects, mitigating factors
  - *Data augmentation **mitigates** the impact of limited training samples.*

- [ ] **fluctuate** /ˈflʌk.tʃu.eɪt/
  - *v.* rise and fall irregularly — 波动
  - Family: fluctuation (n.)
  - Collocations: fluctuate between, fluctuate widely, statistical fluctuation
  - *The count rate **fluctuates** due to beam intensity variations.*

- [ ] **diminish** /dɪˈmɪn.ɪʃ/
  - *v.* make or become less — 减少；降低
  - Family: diminished (adj.), diminishing (adj.)
  - Collocations: diminish the significance, diminishing returns, gradually diminish
  - *The signal-to-noise ratio **diminishes** at lower beam energies.*

- [ ] **facilitate** /fəˈsɪl.ɪ.teɪt/
  - *v.* make easier or possible — 促进
  - Family: facilitation (n.), facilitator (n.)
  - Collocations: facilitate communication, facilitate the process
  - *The Kenkyusei year **facilitates** a smooth transition into the PhD program.*

- [ ] **unprecedented** /ʌnˈprɛs.ɪ.dɛn.tɪd/
  - *adj.* never done or known before — 史无前例的
  - Family: precedent (n.), precedented (adj.)
  - Collocations: unprecedented accuracy, unprecedented scale, set a precedent
  - *The upgraded detector achieved **unprecedented** spatial resolution.*

- [ ] **emerge** /ɪˈmɜːrdʒ/
  - *v.* come into existence or become known — 出现；浮现
  - Family: emergence (n.), emerging (adj.), emergent (adj.)
  - Collocations: emerge from, emerging field, emerging technology
  - *A clear peak **emerged** in the invariant mass spectrum after background subtraction.*

## Category D — Comparison & Description

Words for juxtaposing, contrasting, and describing qualities — high-frequency in TOEFL Reading and integrated tasks.

### Core Words

- [ ] **juxtapose** /ˌdʒʌk.stəˈpoʊz/
  - *v.* place side by side for comparison — 并列对比
  - Family: juxtaposition (n.)
  - Collocations: juxtapose A with B, juxtapose two perspectives
  - *The paper **juxtaposes** simulation results with experimental data.*

- [ ] **ubiquitous** /juːˈbɪk.wɪ.təs/
  - *adj.* present everywhere — 无处不在的
  - Family: ubiquity (n.), ubiquitously (adv.)
  - Collocations: ubiquitous in, become ubiquitous
  - *Machine learning has become **ubiquitous** in modern particle physics analysis.*

- [ ] **distinct** /dɪˈstɪŋkt/
  - *adj.* clearly different; easily perceived — 明显不同的
  - Family: distinction (n.), distinctive (adj.), distinctly (adv.)
  - Collocations: distinct from, distinct pattern, make a distinction
  - *Protons and alpha particles leave **distinct** energy-loss patterns in the TPC.*

- [ ] **inherent** /ɪnˈhɪr.ənt/
  - *adj.* existing as a natural or permanent quality — 固有的
  - Family: inherently (adv.)
  - Collocations: inherent limitation, inherent bias, inherent in
  - *There is an **inherent** trade-off between spatial resolution and readout speed.*

- [ ] **analogous** /əˈnæl.ə.ɡəs/
  - *adj.* comparable in certain respects — 类似的
  - Family: analogy (n.), analogously (adv.)
  - Collocations: analogous to, draw an analogy
  - *Domain adaptation is **analogous** to transfer learning but addresses distribution shift explicitly.*

- [ ] **parameter** /pəˈræm.ɪ.tər/
  - *n.* a measurable factor; a variable — 参数
  - Family: parametric (adj.), parameterize (v.)
  - Collocations: adjust parameters, key parameter, model parameters
  - *We optimized the hyperparameters using a grid search over learning rate and batch size.*

- [ ] **approximate** /əˈprɒk.sɪ.mət/ (adj.) /əˈprɒk.sɪ.meɪt/ (v.)
  - *adj.* close but not exact; *v.* come close to — 近似的；近似
  - Family: approximation (n.), approximately (adv.)
  - Collocations: approximate value, approximately equal, first-order approximation
  - *The Born approximation is valid when the projectile energy is sufficiently high.*

## Category E — Structure & Method

Words for describing methodology, framework, and structure — essential for thesis writing and academic presentations.

### Core Words

- [ ] **framework** /ˈfreɪm.wɜːrk/
  - *n.* a basic structure or set of ideas — 框架
  - Collocations: theoretical framework, within the framework of, provide a framework
  - *We developed a **framework** for automated particle classification using deep learning.*

- [ ] **methodology** /ˌmɛθ.əˈdɒl.ə.dʒi/
  - *n.* a system of methods used in a field — 方法论
  - Family: methodological (adj.), methodologically (adv.)
  - Collocations: research methodology, adopt a methodology, sound methodology
  - *The **methodology** follows a three-stage pipeline: preprocessing, feature extraction, and classification.*

- [ ] **criteria** /kraɪˈtɪr.i.ə/ (plural of criterion)
  - *n.* standards by which something is judged — 标准
  - Collocations: selection criteria, meet the criteria, evaluation criteria
  - *Events were selected based on strict energy-loss **criteria**.*

- [ ] **constrain** /kənˈstreɪn/
  - *v.* limit or restrict — 约束；限制
  - Family: constraint (n.), constrained (adj.)
  - Collocations: constrain the model, impose constraints, tightly constrained
  - *The available beam time **constrains** the number of experimental runs.*

- [ ] **hypothesize** /haɪˈpɒθ.ə.saɪz/
  - *v.* put forward as a hypothesis — 假设
  - Family: hypothesis (n.), hypothetical (adj.), hypothetically (adv.)
  - Collocations: hypothesize that, test the hypothesis, null hypothesis
  - *We **hypothesized** that transfer learning would reduce the required training data by 50%.*

- [ ] **simulate** /ˈsɪm.jʊ.leɪt/
  - *v.* imitate the behavior of a system — 模拟
  - Family: simulation (n.), simulated (adj.), simulator (n.)
  - Collocations: simulate conditions, Monte Carlo simulation, simulate the response
  - *We **simulated** 100k particle events using GEANT4 to generate training labels.*

- [ ] **integrate** /ˈɪn.tɪ.ɡreɪt/
  - *v.* combine into a whole — 整合；积分
  - Family: integration (n.), integral (adj./n.), integrated (adj.)
  - Collocations: integrate into, fully integrated, integral part
  - *The readout system **integrates** charge signals over a 10 μs window.*

- [ ] **constitute** /ˈkɒn.stɪ.tjuːt/
  - *v.* be a part of; make up — 构成
  - Family: constitution (n.), constituent (n./adj.)
  - Collocations: constitute a majority, constitute evidence, constituent parts
  - *Background events **constitute** roughly 15% of the raw dataset.*

### Exercise 5 — Error Correction

Each sentence contains one AWL word used **incorrectly** (wrong meaning, wrong form, or wrong collocation). Find and fix the error. This trains you to catch subtle misuse — exactly what TOEFL tests.

1. *The team derived a new experimental setup in the lab last week.*
2. *The data is analogous that noise levels are too high.*
3. *We need to justify more evidence before publishing.*
4. *The parameter between the two detectors makes comparison difficult.*
5. *Background noise constitutes a serious problem for the readout electronics.* *(Tricky — is this one actually correct?)*
6. *The methodology is inherent flawed because it ignores systematic error.*

> [!success]- Answers
> 1. "derived" → **established** (you *establish* a setup; you *derive* equations)
> 2. "analogous" → **indicates** (analogous means "similar to", not "shows that")
> 3. "justify" → **substantiate / corroborate** (justify = show something is right; substantiate = provide evidence)
> 4. "parameter" → **distinction / difference** (parameter = a measurable variable, not a difference)
> 5. This one is **correct** — "constitutes" means "is" or "makes up", so "constitutes a serious problem" works.
> 6. "inherent" → **inherently** (needs adverb form to modify "flawed")

### Exercise 11 — Odd One Out

In each row, one word does **not** belong to the same semantic group as the others. Circle it and explain why.

1. **analyze** / **evaluate** / **fluctuate** / **assess**

### Exercise 13 — Sentence Auction

2. **Spaced repetition**: 10–15 new words per session, review daily per [[TOEFL_Study_Plan#Vocabulary (ongoing throughout all phases)|Phase 1 plan]]
