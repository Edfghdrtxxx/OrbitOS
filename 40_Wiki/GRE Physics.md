# GRE Physics

## Definition

The GRE Physics Subject Test is a standardized exam administered by ETS that assesses undergraduate-level physics knowledge. It is used by graduate programs (primarily in the US and select international universities such as UTokyo) as part of doctoral admissions.

## Format

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Questions | ~70 |
| Type | Single-answer, five-choice (A–E) |
| Duration | 120 minutes (2 hours) |
| Delivery | Computer-based (since Sep 2023) |
| Units | SI |
| Timer | Continuous — breaks/restroom do **not** pause the clock |

## Scoring

- Score range: **200–990** (10-point increments)
- Three subscores reported: (1) Classical Mechanics, (2) Electromagnetism, (3) Quantum Mechanics & Atomic Physics
- No penalty for guessing (rights-only scoring since the computer-based transition) `[UNVERIFIED — based on general ETS policy for computer-delivered tests; confirm on ETS website before test day]`

## Content Breakdown

| Topic | Weight | Key Subtopics |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| Classical Mechanics | 20% | Kinematics, Newton's laws, work/energy, oscillations, rotational motion, Lagrangian/Hamiltonian, central forces, fluid dynamics |
| Electromagnetism | 18% | Electrostatics, DC circuits, magnetic fields, Lorentz force, induction, Maxwell's equations |
| Quantum Mechanics | 13% | Fundamental concepts, Schrodinger equation, angular momentum, hydrogen atom, perturbation theory |
| Thermodynamics & Statistical Mechanics | 10% | Laws of thermo, ensembles, partition functions, ideal gas, blackbody radiation |
| Atomic Physics | 10% | Properties of electrons, Bohr model, fine/hyperfine structure, selection rules, X-rays |
| Specialized Topics | 9% | Nuclear/particle physics, condensed matter, astrophysics, mathematical methods |
| Optics & Wave Phenomena | 8% | Wave properties, superposition, interference, diffraction, polarization, geometrical optics |
| Special Relativity | 6% | Lorentz transformation, time dilation, length contraction, four-vectors, energy-momentum |
| Laboratory Methods | 6% | Data/error analysis, electronics, instrumentation, radiation detection, laser physics |

Required math: calculus, vector algebra, coordinate systems, Fourier series, PDEs, matrices, determinants, complex variables.

## Test Schedule

Each academic year has **three test windows** (~2 weeks each):

| Window | Approximate Timing |
|--------|-------------------|
| Window 1 | September (early–mid) |
| Window 2 | October (mid–late) |
| Window 3 | April (late, following year) |

Retake policy: minimum **14 days** between attempts (effectively one attempt per window).

Score reports arrive **~8–11 days** after a test window closes.

## Registration

- International: [ets.org](https://www.ets.org/gre/test-takers/subject-tests.html)
- China: NEEA portal — `gre-main.neea.cn`
- Cost: **$175** (worldwide)
- ETS score validity: 5 years

## Key Points

- Specialized Topics (nuclear, particle, condensed matter, astrophysics) are a common blind spot — do not skip them
- Reference textbook: *Conquering the GRE Physics*
- In 2025, GRE Physics is considered low-ROI for US PhD applications, but **UTokyo Graduate School of Science still requires it** for both GSGC and Special Selection (Physics department)
