# Stimulus Control Therapy

## Definition

Stimulus control therapy is a behavioral intervention for insomnia that re-associates the bed and bedroom exclusively with sleep. When someone lies awake in bed repeatedly, the brain forms a conditioned association between the bed and wakefulness. This therapy systematically breaks that association by enforcing strict rules about bed use.

## Key Points

- Core rules: (1) go to bed only when sleepy, (2) use the bed only for sleep, (3) if not asleep within ~20 minutes get up and do something boring in dim light, (4) return only when drowsy, (5) maintain a fixed wake time regardless of sleep duration
- The fixed wake time is the most important anchor — it stabilizes [[Circadian Rhythm]] and builds sleep pressure for the following night
- Short-term sleep loss from getting up is expected and therapeutic — it increases homeostatic sleep drive
- One of the most effective single-component treatments for insomnia, often used as part of [[CBT-I]]
- Counterintuitive: spending *less* time in bed initially leads to *better* sleep efficiency

## Examples

- Lying in bed for 25 minutes unable to sleep → get up, read a physical book in dim light for 15 minutes → return to bed when eyelids feel heavy
- Eliminating phone use, planning, and TV watching in bed so the brain re-learns "bed = sleep"
