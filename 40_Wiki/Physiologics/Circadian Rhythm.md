# Circadian Rhythm

## Definition

The body's internal 24-hour biological clock (昼夜节律) that regulates sleep-wake cycles, hormone release, and other physiological processes. Disrupting it — such as by sleeping and waking at irregular hours — negatively affects the dopamine system and overall well-being.

## Key Points

- Governed by the suprachiasmatic nucleus (SCN) in the hypothalamus, synchronized primarily by light exposure
- Strongly influences the [[Dopamine Baseline]] — inverted sleep schedules (sleeping late, waking late) disrupt dopamine function even if total sleep hours are sufficient
- Morning sunlight exposure is one of the most effective ways to calibrate the circadian clock
- "Just sleeping enough" is not sufficient — *when* you sleep matters as much as *how long*

## Practical Implications

- Rise with the sun; get sunlight exposure in the first 30-60 minutes after waking
- Wind down activity as evening approaches
- Avoid bright screens and high-stimulation content before bed
- Consistency matters more than perfection — a regular schedule is key
