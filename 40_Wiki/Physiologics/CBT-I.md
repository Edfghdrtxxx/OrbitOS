# CBT-I

## Definition

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I) is the gold-standard, first-line treatment for chronic insomnia. It is a structured, multi-component behavioral and cognitive intervention that targets the perpetuating factors of insomnia rather than masking symptoms with medication. It outperforms sleeping pills in long-term outcomes and has no side effects.

## Key Points

- Five core components: (1) [[Stimulus Control Therapy]], (2) sleep restriction, (3) [[Cognitive Offloading]] and cognitive restructuring, (4) sleep hygiene education, (5) relaxation training
- Typically delivered over 6-8 sessions, but self-guided programs (apps, workbooks) also show strong efficacy
- Works by breaking the **three P's of insomnia**: Predisposing factors (trait anxiety), Precipitating factors (life events like a move), and Perpetuating factors (bad sleep habits formed in response)
- Effect sizes are comparable to medication in the short term and **superior at long-term follow-up** — benefits persist after treatment ends, unlike medication
- Recommended by the American College of Physicians as first-line treatment *before* any pharmacological intervention

## Examples

- A graduate student with 3 weeks of poor sleep after moving to a new dormitory: CBT-I would address the [[First-Night Effect]] (precipitating), [[Pre-sleep Hyperarousal]] from thesis stress (perpetuating), and build robust sleep habits that outlast the adjustment period
- Free guided programs: Insomnia Coach (VA app), Sleepio, and structured workbooks like "Say Good Night to Insomnia" by Gregg Jacobs
