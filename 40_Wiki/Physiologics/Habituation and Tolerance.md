# Habituation and Tolerance

## Definition

The process by which the brain adapts to repeated stimuli, reducing its response over time (习惯化/耐受). In the context of dopamine, this means increasingly intense stimulation is needed to produce the same effect, driving compulsive behaviors and eroding the [[Dopamine Baseline]].

## Key Points

- The brain's natural adaptation mechanism — not inherently bad, but becomes destructive with high-intensity stimulation sources
- **Vicious cycle**: high stimulation → tolerance → need for stronger stimulation → further baseline erosion → even stronger stimulation needed
- Common modern triggers: short-form video scrolling, high-fat/high-sugar binge eating, social media notifications, addictive substances
- This is the mechanism behind why "everything feels boring" — the baseline has been lowered by chronic overstimulation

## Examples

- Scrolling short videos: initially entertaining, but over weeks/months you need to scroll longer to get the same satisfaction
- Food: portion sizes gradually increase; plain food becomes "tasteless" as the palate adapts to hyper-palatable foods
- Substances: nicotine doesn't add happiness — it lowers the baseline so that *not smoking* feels worse than normal

## Countermeasures

- Deliberately choose low-stimulation activities (plain food, walks, reading)
- Periodic "dopamine fasts" — reduce high-stimulation inputs to let the baseline recover
- Replace consumption habits with creation habits (exercise, meaningful work)
