# Cortisol

## Definition

Cortisol is a steroid hormone produced by the adrenal glands, often called the "stress hormone." It is released in response to stress and low blood glucose, and follows the [[Circadian Rhythm]] — peaking in early morning and declining at night.

## Key Points

- Regulated by the [[HPA Axis]] (hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis): the brain signals the adrenal glands to release cortisol when a threat or stressor is detected
- Chronically elevated cortisol (from sustained stress) impairs sleep, weakens immunity, disrupts memory consolidation, and can lower the [[Dopamine Baseline]]
- Cortisol and [[Dopamine Baseline]] have an inverse relationship — high chronic cortisol suppresses dopamine, contributing to low motivation and [[Learned Helplessness]]
- Acute cortisol spikes are healthy and adaptive (e.g., waking up, exercise); the problem is chronic elevation without recovery

## Examples

- Morning cortisol spike helps you wake up and feel alert — this is part of the normal [[Circadian Rhythm]]
- Chronic exam stress keeps cortisol elevated, leading to insomnia, poor focus, and [[Habituation and Tolerance]] to the stress signal
