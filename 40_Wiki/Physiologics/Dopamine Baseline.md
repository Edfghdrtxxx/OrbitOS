# Dopamine Baseline

## Definition

The resting level of dopamine in the brain when no significant external stimulation is present (多巴胺基线). A moderate-to-high baseline supports motivation and positive mood; a low baseline leads to boredom, apathy, and the feeling of "not wanting to do anything."

## Key Points

- Too low: lack of motivation, chronic boredom, disengagement from daily life
- Too high: can lead to mania — the optimal state is **moderate-to-high**
- Modern habits that erode it: doomscrolling short videos, binge-eating high-fat/high-sugar foods, disrupted sleep, addictive substances
- The erosion mechanism is [[Habituation and Tolerance]] — the brain adapts to repeated high-intensity stimulation, requiring ever-stronger hits to feel the same pleasure

## How to Maintain / Restore

1. **Diet**: Choose [[Low-Glycemic Index Foods]], practice portion control, avoid hyper-palatable foods
2. **Sleep**: Align with [[Circadian Rhythm]] — rise with the sun, wind down at sunset
3. **Exercise**: Regular physical activity elevates baseline (the benefit comes *after* the workout, not during)
4. **Mindset**: Focus on small everyday pleasures rather than obsessing over grand distant goals
5. **Values alignment**: Ensure your work environment matches your personal values
6. **Avoid addictive substances**: Alcohol and nicotine artificially manipulate dopamine and lower baseline long-term
7. **Social connection**: Small, timely responses to friends/family matter more than you think
