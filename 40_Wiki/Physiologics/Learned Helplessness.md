# Learned Helplessness

## Definition

A psychological state in which repeated exposure to uncontrollable negative events causes an individual to stop attempting to change their situation, even when change becomes possible (习得性无助). It is associated with severe motivational decline and damage to the dopamine system.

## Key Points

- First demonstrated in animal experiments: mice shocked in an inescapable cage eventually stop trying to escape even when the barrier is removed
- The core mechanism: when effort repeatedly produces no results, the brain learns to stop trying
- Directly damages the [[Dopamine Baseline]] — the person loses intrinsic motivation not just for the specific task, but broadly across life
- Can manifest as chronic procrastination, career stagnation, or emotional withdrawal

## Examples

- A student who consistently fails exams despite studying may eventually stop studying altogether
- An employee whose suggestions are always ignored may become disengaged and passive
- The classic mouse experiment: electric shock + glass barrier → mouse stops escaping even after barrier is removed
