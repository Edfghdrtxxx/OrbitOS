# Pre-sleep Hyperarousal

## Definition

Pre-sleep hyperarousal is a state of elevated cognitive and physiological activation at bedtime that prevents the natural transition into sleep. The brain remains in "problem-solving mode" — the sympathetic nervous system stays engaged, cortisol levels remain elevated, and racing thoughts block the onset of drowsiness.

## Key Points

- Has two components: **cognitive** (racing thoughts, planning, worry) and **somatic** (elevated heart rate, muscle tension, shallow breathing)
- Most common in intellectually active individuals managing high-stakes, multi-track life situations (e.g., graduate students, career changers)
- The person may not *feel* anxious — the HPA axis can run hot without subjective distress
- Measurable markers: elevated resting heart rate at bedtime (above personal baseline), reduced HRV, longer sleep onset latency
- Key countermeasures: [[Cognitive Offloading]], [[Stimulus Control Therapy]], [[Progressive Muscle Relaxation]], and hard cutoff times for activating tasks

## Examples

- Lying in bed mentally drafting an email to a professor while heart rate sits at 63 bpm (above a personal baseline of 53-62 bpm)
- Falling asleep fine on low-stakes days but struggling on nights before deadlines — the arousal is task-linked, not environment-linked
