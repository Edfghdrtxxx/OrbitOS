## Overview

The **Feynman-Recall-Spaced (FRS) Study Method** is a three-phase AI-assisted study workflow that combines three evidence-based learning techniques into a single structured session. It is designed to move knowledge from shallow familiarity to deep, durable understanding.

Learning begins with intake, but it becomes far more efficient when the learner **expresses** ideas and iterates on feedback. FRS makes expression the central loop.

## The Three Phases

### Phase 1 — Feynman Check (费曼检验)

Based on the [[Feynman Technique]]: explain a concept in your own words as if teaching someone else. The AI (or study partner) then:

- Points out **errors**, **omissions**, and **imprecise areas** in your explanation
- Asks **3 follow-up questions** to probe understanding depth

**Why it works:** Forces you to confront gaps between "I've seen this" and "I truly understand this."

### Phase 2 — Active Recall Test (主动回忆测试)

Generate **10 test questions** based on the studied content, stratified by difficulty:

| Tier                 | Count | Purpose                            |
| -------------------- | ----- | ---------------------------------- |
| Basic (基础)           | 3     | Core definitions and facts         |
| Applied (应用)         | 4     | Use concepts in context            |
| Comprehensive (综合分析) | 3     | Cross-topic synthesis and analysis |

After answering, each question is graded with error explanations provided.

**Why it works:** Retrieval practice is one of the strongest predictors of long-term retention (Roediger & Butler, 2011).

### Phase 3 — Spaced Repetition Plan (间隔复习计划)

Based on test results, the AI:

- **Marks weak knowledge points** identified from incorrect or partial answers
- Generates a **7-day review schedule**
- Keeps each daily review session under **5 minutes**

**Why it works:** Spaced repetition exploits the spacing effect, reviewing material at increasing intervals to maximize retention per unit of time.

## When to Use

- Preparing for exams (e.g., physics entrance exam, TOEFL)
- Learning new technical concepts (e.g., nuclear physics, ML architectures)
- Any topic where deep understanding matters more than surface familiarity
