# Self-fulfilling prophecy

## Definition

A self-fulfilling prophecy is a belief or expectation that influences behavior in ways that make the belief come true. It is a feedback loop where perception shapes action and action shapes outcomes.

## Key Points

- Expectations can change how people treat others, altering performance or choices.
- The effect often works through subtle cues, opportunities, or reduced effort.
- It can reinforce negative patterns similar to [[Learned Helplessness]].
- The loop can be broken by changing expectations, feedback, or incentives.

## Examples

- A teacher expects a student to excel and gives more support, improving results.
- A rumor of a bank failure triggers withdrawals that cause the failure.
