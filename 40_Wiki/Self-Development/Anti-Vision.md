# Anti-Vision

## Definition

An Anti-Vision is a detailed, emotionally vivid description of the worst-case future that results from inaction or continued autopilot behavior. Popularized by Dan Koe, it serves as a negative motivational anchor — the life you are trying to avoid — paired with a positive Vision of the life you are building.

## Key Points

- Functions through loss aversion: humans are more motivated to avoid pain than to pursue gain, making the Anti-Vision a stronger daily driver than the Vision alone
- Must be specific and sensory — a concrete "Tuesday in that life" — not abstract ("I'll be unhappy")
- Reviewed when motivation drops; it re-engages emotional stakes that logical planning cannot
- Complements the Vision; together they form the motivational poles of the [[Game_Framework]]
- Related to [[self-fulfilling prophecy]] — without deliberate course correction, the Anti-Vision tends to manifest by default

## Examples

- "You become the person who always talked about Japan but never went. The vault was organized. The scorecard was filled out. But the emails were never sent." (from [[Game_Framework]])
- A generic anti-vision like "I'll be stuck" is less effective than one describing the specific daily routine, emotions, and regrets of the unwanted future.
