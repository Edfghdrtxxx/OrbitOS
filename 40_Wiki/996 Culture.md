# 996 Culture

## Definition

996 Culture (996工作制) refers to the Chinese tech and corporate work schedule of 9am to 9pm, 6 days a week — 72 hours per week. It became widely criticized after gaining public attention in 2019, and was ruled illegal by China's Supreme Court in 2021, though enforcement remains inconsistent.

## Key Points

- Originated in Chinese tech companies (Alibaba, Huawei, ByteDance) and has spread to academia and research institutions
- Violates China's Labour Law, which caps standard working hours at 44 hours/week, but cultural pressure often overrides legal protections
- Directly opposed to sustainable-pace research careers; a key constraint in the [[Game_Framework]]
- The rejection of 996 is a primary motivator for seeking a research career in Japan, where lab culture tends toward more structured boundaries

## Examples

- A supervisor who expects lab presence from morning to late evening, including weekends, is practicing 996-adjacent culture even in an academic setting.
- Choosing a PhD lab partly based on its working-hour norms is a deliberate filter against 996 encroachment.
