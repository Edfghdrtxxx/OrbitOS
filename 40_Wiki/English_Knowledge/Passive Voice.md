# Passive Voice

## Definition

A sentence construction where the **object** of an action becomes the **subject** of the sentence, formed with **be + [[Past Participle]]**. The doer (agent) is either omitted or introduced with "by". Essential in academic and scientific writing where the process matters more than who performed it.

## Key Points

- **Form:** subject + be (conjugated) + past participle — "The data **were analyzed** using ROOT."
- **Why use it in academic writing:** Shifts focus from the researcher to the process/results — "The experiment **was conducted** at RIKEN" sounds more objective than "We conducted the experiment"
- **Tense variations:**
  - Present: "The sample **is prepared** daily."
  - Past: "The results **were published** in 2025."
  - Present perfect: "The detector **has been calibrated**."
  - Future: "The thesis **will be submitted** by June."
  - Modal: "Errors **can be reduced** by averaging."
- **When NOT to use it:** Overuse makes writing dull and evasive. Use active voice for clear attribution — "We propose a new method" is better than "A new method is proposed"
- **Common error:** Missing "be" — ~~"The data analyzed"~~ → "The data **were** analyzed"

## Examples

- "The TPC **was designed** for particle tracking." (agent omitted — who designed it isn't the point)
- "Candidates **are selected** by the admissions committee." (agent included with "by")
- "This phenomenon **has been observed** in multiple experiments." ([[Present Perfect]] + passive — common in literature reviews)
