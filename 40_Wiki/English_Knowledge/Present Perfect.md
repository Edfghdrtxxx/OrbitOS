# Present Perfect

## Definition

A verb tense formed with **have/has + past participle** that connects a past action or state to the present moment. It expresses that something happened at an unspecified time before now, or that a past event has current relevance.

## Key Points

- **Form:** subject + have/has + past participle (e.g., "I **have visited** Tokyo three times")
- **Unspecified past time:** Use when *when* it happened doesn't matter — "She has read that paper" (at some point before now)
- **Life experience:** "I have never been to Japan" — emphasizes experience up to the present
- **Result in the present:** "He has lost his keys" — he still doesn't have them now
- **Contrast with [[Simple Past]]:** Simple past pins the action to a specific time ("I visited Tokyo *in 2024*"); present perfect leaves time open or stresses the result

## Examples

- "I **have finished** my thesis draft." (result: it's done now)
- "**Have** you ever **studied** Japanese?" (life experience)
- "She **has lived** in Lanzhou since 2020." (started in the past, continues now — overlaps with [[Present Perfect Continuous]])
