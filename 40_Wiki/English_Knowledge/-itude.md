# -itude

## Definition

A Latin derivational suffix (*-itūdō*, stem *-itūdin-*) that attaches to Latin adjective stems to form abstract nouns expressing a state, quality, or condition. Entered English via Old French *-itude*.

**Formula:** Latin adjective stem + *-itūdō* → abstract noun meaning "the state/quality of being [adjective]"

## Key Points

- **Derivational, not a root:** -itude is a suffix — it does not carry independent meaning but transforms an adjective into a noun naming its abstract quality. Example: *grātus* (pleasing) → *grātitūdō* → [[Gratitude]] (state of being thankful).
- **Key examples:**

| Latin stem | Meaning | Latin noun | English word | Gloss |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| *grāt-* | pleasing | *grātitūdō* | [[Gratitude]] | state of being thankful |
| *magn-* | great | *magnitūdō* | magnitude | state of being great |
| *sōl-* | alone | *sōlitūdō* | solitude | state of being alone |
| *apt-* | fit | *aptitūdō* | aptitude | state of being fit/suited |
| *cert-* | sure | *certitūdō* | certitude | state of being certain |
| *fort-* | strong | *fortitūdō* | fortitude | state of being strong/brave |
| *mult-* | many | *multitūdō* | multitude | state of being many |
| *lāt-* | wide | *lātitūdō* | latitude | state of being wide |
| *long-* | long | *longitūdō* | longitude | state of being long |
| *alt-* | high | *altitūdō* | altitude | state of being high |

- **Abstract → concrete drift:** Latitude, longitude, and altitude began as abstract qualities ("wideness," "longness," "highness") before becoming concrete geographic and spatial terms — a common pattern of [[Semantic Drift]].
- **Cousin suffixes:** *-itude* (*-itūdō*), *-tion* (*-tiōnem*), *-ness* (Germanic), *-ity* (*-itātem*). When both *-itude* and *-ity* exist for the same adjective, *-itude* tends to feel more literary or formal (e.g., *certainty* vs *certitude*).
- **Register:** -itude words cluster in formal, literary, and academic registers (see [[Academic_Word_List_AWL]]).

## Examples

- "She bore it with quiet **fortitude**." (abstract quality — strength of character)
- "The **magnitude** of the earthquake was 7.2." (abstract quality turned measurable quantity)
