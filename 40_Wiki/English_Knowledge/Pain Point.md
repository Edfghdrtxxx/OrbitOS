# Pain Point

## Definition

A **pain point** is a specific problem, frustration, or unmet need that causes difficulty — most often used in business, UX, and product contexts. The [[Collocation]] is always *pain **point***, never "pain spot."

## Key Points

- Widely used in product management, marketing, and UX design to describe user frustrations
- Always a noun phrase: "identify pain points," "address a pain point"
- Common collocations: *identify / address / solve / alleviate* pain points
- The word **point** pairs with *pain*; swapping to "spot" breaks the [[Collocation]]
- Often used in plural: "What are your customers' pain points?"

## Examples

- "Long checkout times are a major **pain point** for online shoppers."
- "The team mapped out all the **pain points** in the onboarding flow."
