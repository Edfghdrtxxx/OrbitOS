# Address vs Solve

## Definition

**Address** means to deal with, engage with, or direct attention toward a problem or issue — without implying resolution. **Solve** means to find a definitive answer or resolution to a problem. The key distinction is that *address* focuses on the **process** of handling something, while *solve* focuses on reaching a **conclusion**.

## Key Points

- **Address** is broader: you can address a concern, a question, or even a person — it implies acknowledgment and effort, not necessarily success
- **Solve** implies finality: the problem has a clear answer or fix (e.g., solve an equation, solve a bug)
- In academic writing, "address" is preferred when discussing open-ended challenges or research gaps (e.g., "This paper addresses the issue of…"), since research rarely *solves* an entire problem
- "Solve" collocates with concrete, bounded problems: puzzles, equations, technical errors
- "Address" collocates with abstract or ongoing issues: concerns, needs, challenges, topics

## Examples

- "We need to **address** the scalability concerns before deployment." (= deal with, not necessarily fix completely)
- "The algorithm **solves** the optimization problem in polynomial time." (= finds the definitive answer)
- Academic: "Previous studies have **addressed** this phenomenon but have not fully **solved** the underlying mechanism." — both words in one sentence, showing the contrast clearly
