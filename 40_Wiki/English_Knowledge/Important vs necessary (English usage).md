# Important vs necessary (English usage)

## Definition

In English prioritization, **important** ranks options by value or impact, while **necessary** states a requirement. This distinction prevents awkward phrasing like "most necessary" when discussing improvement priorities.

## Key Points

- **Important** answers "Which matters most?" and is used for ranking.
- **Necessary** answers "What must happen?" and is typically not graded with "most."
- Use priority frames like "most important" or "should improve first" instead of "most necessary."
- Track confusing pairs in [[Daily Accumulation]] to stabilize [[Word Choice]].

## Examples

- "Clarity is the most important thing to improve in this draft."
- "Fixing the bug is necessary before release."
