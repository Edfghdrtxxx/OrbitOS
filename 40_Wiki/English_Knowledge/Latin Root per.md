# Latin Root *per-*

## Definition

The Latin root **per-** means "through," "thoroughly," or "completely." It appears as a prefix in many English words, intensifying the base meaning or conveying the idea of passage, completion, or thoroughness.

## Key Points

- **per- = through / throughout**: *perceive* (per + capere "to take") — to take in thoroughly, to become aware of through the senses
- **per- = thoroughly / completely**: *perfect* (per + facere "to do/make") — thoroughly done, complete in every way
- **per- = forward / onward**: *persist* (per + sistere "to stand") — to stand through, to continue firmly despite difficulty
- Combined with [[Latin Root form]], **per + form** gives *perform* — to carry through a form or shape, i.e., to carry out an action completely
- Many *per-* words appear on the [[Academic_Word_List_AWL]], making them essential for TOEFL and academic writing

| **permanent** | per + manere (remain) | lasting through time, enduring |
| **permit** | per + mittere (send) | to let through, to allow |
| **persist** | per + sistere (stand) | to continue firmly, stand through |
| **perspective** | per + specere (look) | a way of looking through; viewpoint |
| **persuade** | per + suadere (urge) | to urge thoroughly, to convince |
| **pervade** | per + vadere (go) | to go through entirely, to spread throughout |
| **perform** | per + form (shape) | to carry through completely; see [[Latin Root form]] |
| **pertinent** | per + tenere (hold) | holding through to the point; relevant |
