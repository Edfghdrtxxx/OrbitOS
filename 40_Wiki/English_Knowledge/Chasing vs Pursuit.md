# Chasing vs Pursuit

## Definition

"Chasing" and "pursuit" both describe going after something, but differ in **register** and **connotation**: "chasing" is informal and implies urgency or desperation, while "pursuit" is formal and suggests deliberate, dignified effort.

## Key Points

- **Register:** "chasing" is conversational/everyday; "pursuit" is literary/academic/formal
- **Connotation:** "chasing" can feel frantic or naive (*chasing money* = restless); "pursuit" feels noble and purposeful (*the pursuit of knowledge* = committed)
- **Plural form:** "pursuits" is commonly used to mean "activities/interests" (*intellectual pursuits*); "chasings" is virtually never used
- Both work for concrete actions (*chasing a thief* / *in pursuit of a suspect*) and abstract goals (*chasing dreams* / *pursuit of happiness*), but the tone shifts significantly

## Examples

- *He's been chasing fame his whole life.* (informal, slightly negative — implies restlessness)
- *She dedicated herself to the pursuit of excellence.* (formal, positive — implies commitment)
- *His many pursuits include painting and hiking.* (meaning: hobbies/activities — no "chasing" equivalent)
