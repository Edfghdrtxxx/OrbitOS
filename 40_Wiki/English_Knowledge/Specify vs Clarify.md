# Specify vs Clarify

## Schematics

*No suitable open-license schematics found on Wikimedia Commons for this topic.*

## Definition

**Specify** means to state something explicitly and in precise detail — naming exact items, values, or conditions. **Clarify** means to make something that is already stated but unclear easier to understand — removing ambiguity or confusion. The core distinction: specify adds *new detail*; clarify makes *existing detail* more transparent.

## Key Points

- **Specify** answers "what exactly?" — it introduces precise, previously unstated information (e.g., "Please specify the deadline" = tell me the exact date)
- **Clarify** answers "what do you mean?" — it restates or explains something already mentioned to resolve confusion (e.g., "Could you clarify what you mean by 'soon'?" = help me understand your existing statement)
- In academic writing, **specify** typically appears when defining parameters, constraints, or requirements; **clarify** appears when resolving reviewer questions or [[Ambiguity]]
- A common error is using "clarify" when you actually need new information (specify), or "specify" when you want someone to re-explain (clarify)
- Both belong to the [[Academic_Word_List_AWL]] and appear frequently in formal and technical contexts

## Examples

- ✅ "The proposal does not **specify** which detector will be used." → Missing detail needs to be stated.
- ✅ "Could you **clarify** why you chose Method A over Method B?" → The choice was made, but the reasoning is unclear.
- ❌ "Can you **clarify** the meeting time?" → If no time was ever given, use **specify** instead.
- ❌ "Please **specify** your earlier point." → If the point was already made but confusing, use **clarify** instead.
