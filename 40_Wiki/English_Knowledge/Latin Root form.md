# Latin Root *form-*

## Definition

The Latin root **form-** (from *forma*) means "shape" or "structure." It generates a large family of English words related to giving shape, changing shape, or the nature of shape itself.

## Key Points

- **form = shape / structure**: *format* (forma + -atus) — the shape or arrangement of something
- **Prefixes change the direction of shaping**: *transform* (trans + forma) — to change shape across; *deform* (de + forma) — to undo the shape; *reform* (re + forma) — to shape again
- **-ation suffix creates process nouns**: *formation*, *information*, *transformation*, *deformation* — the act or result of shaping
- Combined with [[Latin Root per]], **per + form** gives *perform* — to carry a shape through to completion
- Many *form-* words are high-frequency in academic and TOEFL contexts; see [[Academic_Word_List_AWL]]

## Examples

| Word | Etymology | Meaning |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **conform** | con- (together) + form | to shape oneself together with; to comply |
| **deform** | de- (away) + form | to distort or ruin the shape of |
| **formal** | form + -alis | relating to the established shape/structure |
| **format** | form + -atus | the way something is shaped or arranged |
| **formula** | form + -ula (diminutive) | a little form; a fixed pattern or rule |
| **inform** | in- (into) + form | to put form into the mind; to give knowledge |
| **perform** | per- (through) + form | to carry through a form; to execute; see [[Latin Root per]] |
| **reform** | re- (again) + form | to shape again; to improve by change |
| **transform** | trans- (across) + form | to change shape entirely |
| **uniform** | uni- (one) + form | having one form; consistent throughout |
