# UTokyo CNS

## Definition

The Center for Nuclear Study (CNS) is a research center within the University of Tokyo's Graduate School of Science, focused on nuclear physics, hadron physics, and accelerator science. It operates experimental programs at [[RIKEN Nishina Center for Accelerator-Based Science (RNC)|RIKEN]] and is a primary target for PhD enrollment via the [[Kenkyusei]] pathway.

## Key Points

- Research areas include nuclear structure, nuclear reactions, and detector development — aligning with TPC and [[Particle Identification]] expertise
- Located at the Hongo campus (administration) with experimental facilities at RIKEN Wako
- PhD students can receive funding through the RIKEN JRA (Junior Research Associate) program (~200k JPY/month)
- Supervisor outreach and [[Naidaku]] are prerequisites before formal application
- Entrance exams cover physics and mathematics ([[Fundamental_Knowledge]] preparation)

## Examples

- A prospective student contacts a CNS professor specializing in direct reactions, obtains [[Naidaku]], applies as [[Kenkyusei]] for October, then transitions to PhD enrollment.
