## Phase 1: Foundation (Feb 17 – Apr 15, 2026) — 8 weeks

**Goal:** Build core skills, close vocabulary gaps, establish daily habits.

### Daily Routine (1-1.5 hours)

| Day | Focus | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Vocabulary + Reading | 60-90 min |
| Tue | Listening (intensive) | 60-90 min |
| Wed | Vocabulary + Writing | 60-90 min |
| Thu | Listening (extensive) | 60-90 min |
| Fri | Speaking practice | 60-90 min |
| Sat | Full section practice test (rotate weekly) | 60-90 min |
| Sun | Review mistakes + vocabulary review | 60-90 min |

### Vocabulary (ongoing throughout all phases)

- [ ] Get a TOEFL word list app or Anki deck (recommended: "TOEFL 5000" or Magoosh TOEFL vocabulary)
- [ ] Learn **15-20 new words per day** using spaced repetition
- [ ] Focus on academic word list (AWL) — 570 word families that appear frequently in TOEFL
- [ ] Keep a personal "mistake words" list — words you encounter in practice and don't know
- [ ] Target words from assessment gaps: ubiquitous, exacerbate, substantiate, proliferate, mitigate, juxtapose, corroborate, pragmatic, ambivalent, unprecedented

### Reading

- [ ] Read 1 academic article per day (15-20 min) — sources: Scientific American, Nature News, The Economist
- [ ] Practice skimming and scanning techniques
- [ ] Learn to identify: main idea, supporting detail, inference, vocabulary in context, rhetorical purpose
- [ ] Do 2 TOEFL Reading practice passages per week (timed: 20 min per passage)

### Listening (priority focus)

- [ ] **Daily:** Listen to 1 English podcast or lecture (20-30 min) — sources below
- [ ] Practice **note-taking while listening** — this is the key TOEFL Listening skill
- [ ] Start with slower content, gradually increase speed
- [ ] Transcribe 5 minutes of audio per day (dictation exercise)

**Recommended listening sources:**
- TED-Ed (short, clear academic lectures)
- 6 Minute English (BBC) — good for warming up
- Scientific American 60-Second Science
- Crash Course (YouTube) — covers science topics relevant to your background
- NPR Short Wave — science podcast, natural conversation speed

### Speaking

- [ ] Practice **describing familiar topics** for 45 seconds (TOEFL Independent Speaking format)
- [ ] Record yourself and listen back — identify pronunciation issues
- [ ] Learn the TOEFL Speaking response templates (introduction → detail 1 → detail 2 → conclusion)
- [ ] Use shadowing technique: play audio, pause, repeat exactly what was said

### Writing

- [ ] Review basic essay structure: introduction, body paragraphs, conclusion
- [ ] Practice writing topic sentences and supporting details
- [ ] Write 1 short paragraph (150 words) per practice day on an academic topic
- [ ] Focus on reducing grammar errors identified in assessment: subject-verb agreement, conditionals

### Grammar Review

- [ ] Master the **third conditional** (If + past perfect, would have + past participle)
- [ ] Review subject-verb agreement with collective nouns (team, group, committee)
- [ ] Practice parallel structure in writing
- [ ] Review common preposition collocations (attributed to, result in, lead to, contribute to)

### Phase 1 Checkpoint

- [ ] Take a **full TOEFL practice test** (free: ETS TOEFL Practice Online, or use official practice materials)
- [ ] Record baseline scores for each section
- [ ] Identify top 2 weakest areas for Phase 2 focus
- [ ] **Target score at this checkpoint: 80-85**

## Phase 2: Skill Building (Apr 16 – Jun 30, 2026) — 11 weeks

**Goal:** Develop test-specific strategies, intensive Listening improvement, first mock test under real conditions.

### Listening Intensive (40% of study time)

- [ ] Do **1 full TOEFL Listening section** (6 passages) per week, timed
- [ ] After each practice: replay missed questions, transcribe the relevant 30-second segment
- [ ] Practice **lecture note-taking format:** main topic → subtopics → examples → conclusion
- [ ] Listen to content at **1.25x speed** for training; TOEFL will feel slow after this
- [ ] Start listening to academic lectures: MIT OpenCourseWare, Yale Open Courses (physics lectures are ideal — familiar content + English practice)

### Reading Strategy

- [ ] Practice the "read the questions first" technique for detail questions
- [ ] Master **inference questions** — these are worth the most points and are often missed
- [ ] Do 3 TOEFL Reading passages per week (timed)
- [ ] Focus on **insert text** and **summary** question types (these are harder and worth more)

### Speaking Development

- [ ] Practice all 4 TOEFL Speaking task types:
  - Task 1: Independent — give opinion on a topic (45 sec prep, 60 sec speak)
  - Task 2: Integrated — read + listen + speak (campus life)
  - Task 3: Integrated — read + listen + speak (academic)
  - Task 4: Integrated — listen + speak (academic lecture summary)
- [ ] Record and review 3 responses per week
- [ ] Focus on: clear organization, fluency over perfection, transition words
- [ ] If possible, find a speaking partner or use AI conversation tools for practice

### Writing Development

- [ ] Practice both TOEFL Writing tasks:
  - Integrated Writing: read passage + listen to lecture + write (20 min, 150-225 words)
  - Academic Discussion: contribute to an online discussion (10 min, ~100 words)
- [ ] Write 2 full TOEFL Writing responses per week
- [ ] Build a **template bank** for common writing patterns:
  - Comparison/contrast
  - Cause/effect
  - Agree/disagree with reasons
- [ ] Focus on: clear thesis, specific examples, logical transitions, varied sentence structure

### Phase 2 Checkpoint

- [ ] Take a **full timed TOEFL mock test** (simulate real conditions: 2 hours, no breaks, quiet room)
- [ ] Analyze section-by-section scores
- [ ] **Target score at this checkpoint: 88-93**
- [ ] Adjust Phase 3 focus based on results

## Phase 3: Test Preparation (Jul 1 – Sep 15, 2026) — 11 weeks

**Goal:** Peak performance. Full test simulations, targeted weakness elimination, test-day readiness.

### Listening Final Push

- [ ] If Listening is still below 25: dedicate 50% of daily time to Listening
- [ ] Practice with **faster and more complex** academic lectures
- [ ] Focus on: understanding speaker attitude, catching detail shifts ("however," "actually," "but in fact")
- [ ] Master multi-part questions (select 2 or more correct answers)

### Speaking Refinement

- [ ] Polish pronunciation of commonly mispronounced academic words
- [ ] Practice speaking responses with a **timer visible** — pacing is critical
- [ ] Prepare flexible templates that work across many topics
- [ ] Record final versions of 10 practice responses — review for fluency

### Writing Polish

- [ ] Practice writing under strict time limits (20 min integrated, 10 min discussion)
- [ ] Build a collection of **versatile examples** you can adapt to many prompts
- [ ] Focus on: word count targets, clear paragraph breaks, varied vocabulary

### Test Registration & Logistics

- [ ] Register for TOEFL iBT at [ets.org/toefl](https://www.ets.org/toefl) — choose Sep or early Oct 2026 date
- [ ] Identify nearest test center and book early (seats fill up)
- [ ] Check passport validity (TOEFL requires passport as ID in China)
- [ ] Plan logistics: travel to test center, arrive early, bring only passport + confirmation

## Phase 4: Final Week & Test Day

### 7 Days Before

- [ ] Stop learning new material — focus on review only
- [ ] Do 1 light practice session per day (30 min max)
- [ ] Review vocabulary flashcards
- [ ] Review your "mistake journal" — focus on patterns, not individual questions
- [ ] Get sleep schedule aligned (test is usually morning)

### 2 Days Before

- [ ] Stop all practice
- [ ] Prepare test-day items: passport, confirmation email, water bottle, snack for break
- [ ] Light review of speaking/writing templates only
- [ ] Relax — rest > cramming at this point

### Test Day

- [ ] Wake up early, eat a good breakfast
- [ ] Arrive 30 minutes early
- [ ] During the test: **don't panic on hard questions** — TOEFL is adaptive and some questions are designed to be challenging
- [ ] Use all available time — never submit early
- [ ] After the test: scores arrive in 4-6 days for Reading/Listening, 6-10 days for Speaking/Writing

## Retake Strategy

If you score below 100:

| Score | Action |
|---|---|
| 95-99 | Consider accepting — still competitive. Retake only if a specific section dragged you down |
| 85-94 | Retake in Dec 2026 — focus intensively on weakest section for 2 months |
| Below 85 | Analyze what went wrong, adjust strategy, retake in Dec 2026 or Jan 2027 |

> [!note] You can retake TOEFL after a **3-day waiting period**. But a focused 2-month gap between attempts is more effective.
