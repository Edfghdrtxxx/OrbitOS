# Questions and Verifiable Sources
> [!warning] These findings are from **English-language** UTokyo pages fetched 2026-03-10. Japanese-language guidelines (募集要項) may differ. Professor-communicated information may override what's on the website. Treat as flags to verify, not corrections.

## 1. GRE Physics — Confirmed for Special Selection; Likely for GSGC

**Vault claim:** GRE Physics is compulsory for GSGC.

**Finding:** GRE Physics is **explicitly required** for the Physics department under **Special Selection for International Applicants** (source: [Special Selection (EN)](https://www.s.u-tokyo.ac.jp/en/admission/graduate.html)):

| Department | GRE Subject | GRE General |
|---|---|---|
| **Physics** | **Physics — Required** | Not required |
| **Astronomy** | **Physics — Required** | Not required |
| Earth & Planetary Science | Per faculty member | Not required |
| Chemistry | Not required | Required |
| Biological Sciences | Optional | Required |

GSGC pages don't list per-department GRE requirements, but the FAQ and Guidelines PDF both reference GRE, suggesting it's relevant for GSGC too. General doctoral admission (一般入試) does not mention GRE.

> [!success] Bottom Line
> GRE Physics is needed regardless of pathway. The vault claim is **correct in substance**.
> **GRE General is NOT required** for the Physics department — only Chemistry and Biological Sciences require it.

## 2. GSGC vs Special Selection — Key Distinction

From the [GSGC FAQ](https://www.s.u-tokyo.ac.jp/GSGC/faq/) (verbatim):
> "GSGC students are expected to attend both the Master's course and the Doctoral course whereas students in the Special Selection for International Applicants course do not have to attend both."

This confirms they are separate programs. For someone with an existing master's, Special Selection allows direct D1 entry; GSGC may require M1 re-enrollment. See [[Japan_Itinerary]] for current pathway decision and [[UTokyo_RIKEN]] for the execution plan.
