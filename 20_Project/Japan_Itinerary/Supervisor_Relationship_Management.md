# Supervisor Contact Preparation

> [!info] Purpose
> This note prepares for the **first contact with a prospective UTokyo supervisor** (target: late Feb / early Mar 2026). It consolidates every question, verification item, and decision gate that this contact can resolve. The outcome directly determines the study plan in [[Fundamental_Learning_Progress]] and the overall execution path in [[UTokyo_RIKEN]].

> [!danger] Decision Gate
> The single most important question to resolve: **Which admission route — Regular Physics exam (Route A) or GSGC (Route B) — should you prepare for?** If GSGC is confirmed and the written exam is waived, Phases 5–7 of your 18-month study plan can be significantly reduced and freed hours shift to GRE Physics prep. This one answer reshapes everything downstream.

## Step 0 — IMP Advisor Briefing

> [!tip] In Japanese academia, a warm introduction from a known alumnus carries significantly more weight than a cold email. Your IMP advisor — with a full UTokyo pedigree (BSc + MSc + PhD) — is your single strongest outreach asset.

Your IMP advisor (current supervisor at the Institute of Modern Physics, CAS) completed a Bachelor's, Master's, and PhD at UTokyo and knows people at CNS — specific names unknown to you yet. However, your IMP advisor is only partially aware of your UTokyo/RIKEN immigration plan. A warm introduction is very likely if asked. **Complete this section before any outreach to UTokyo professors.**

- [ ] Have a direct conversation with your IMP advisor about the full UTokyo CNS / RIKEN JRA plan (currently only partially aware) (see [[UTokyo_RIKEN]] for the full plan details)
- [ ] Ask which CNS professors your IMP advisor knows personally — this may reorder the priority list in [[UTokyo_RIKEN_Professor_Outreach]]
- [ ] Ask for a warm introduction email (far more effective than cold outreach in Japanese academia)
- [ ] Ask for insider knowledge: admission route advice (Route A vs GSGC), lab culture, housing tips
- [ ] Gauge whether your IMP advisor can speak to any verification items (V2, V4, V6, V8) from firsthand UTokyo experience

### Conversation Memorandum

A structured agenda and question checklist for the face-to-face conversation with your IMP advisor. Review [[UTokyo_RIKEN]] beforehand to refresh on the full plan. Tick off each question as you get an answer.

> [!tip] Tone guidance
> Approach this as a mentorship conversation, not a transactional request. Express gratitude for the advisor's guidance. Be transparent about the immigration goal — withholding it may seem evasive if it comes out later.

**1. Opening context** — Explain that your post-graduation goal is to immigrate from China permanently via a research career. Frame this as seeking guidance, not announcing a decision.

**2. The plan in brief** — Walk through the two-phase roadmap so the advisor has the full picture:
   - Phase 1: Kenkyusei (Research Student) at UTokyo CNS, Oct 2027 – Sep 2028, self-funded.
   - Phase 2: PhD enrollment at UTokyo, Oct 2028+, funded via RIKEN JRA (~200k JPY/month).
   - Long-term: permanent residency through an academic/research career in Japan.

**3. The Kenkyusei question** — The single highest-value question in this conversation. The answer may eliminate an entire year and the self-funded gap.
- [ ] Is it possible to apply directly for PhD enrollment at UTokyo from China, skipping the Kenkyusei buffer year entirely?
- [ ] Did the advisor's own path to UTokyo involve Kenkyusei? If so, was it mandatory for international students or optional?
- [ ] If Kenkyusei is skippable, does the advisor recommend skipping it — or is the buffer year still valuable for building lab rapport and exam prep?

**4. Admission route** — Determines your entire 18-month study plan.
- [ ] Which doctoral admission route does the advisor recommend: Regular Physics exam (Route A, 一般入試) or GSGC?
- [ ] If GSGC, is the written exam typically waived by the supervisor, or should you prepare for it regardless?

**5. Connections & introduction** — The primary reason this conversation comes first.
- [ ] Which CNS professors does the advisor know personally? (Names may reorder the priority list in [[UTokyo_RIKEN_Professor_Outreach]].)
- [ ] Is the advisor willing to send a warm introduction email on your behalf?
- [ ] Does the advisor recommend contacting any professor not currently on your list?

**6. Insider knowledge** — Practical questions the advisor can answer from firsthand UTokyo experience.
- [ ] Can a Kenkyusei sit for the doctoral entrance exam during enrollment? `[V6]`
- [ ] What is the maximum Kenkyusei enrollment duration — and can you extend and retake if you fail the exam? `[V8]`
- [ ] Is RIKEN JRA funding (~200k JPY/month, Shakai Hoken) still accurate for CNS-affiliated PhD students? `[V2]`
- [ ] Are international PhD students eligible for UTokyo's tuition exemption? How is income defined — does JRA count? `[V4]`
- [ ] Any tips on housing near the Wako campus, or does UTokyo offer dormitory access for Kenkyusei?
- [ ] What is the lab culture like — language used in meetings, work-life balance expectations?

> [!note] Terminology used in this note
> - **IMP advisor** = your current supervisor at IMP, CAS (the UTokyo alumnus)
> - **UTokyo supervisor** / **prospective supervisor** = the target professor at UTokyo CNS
> - **Route A** = UTokyo Physics Department Regular doctoral entrance exam route (一般入試), i.e., the full written exam path

## Pre-Contact Checklist

> [!tip] Complete ALL items below before sending the first email. A well-prepared first impression is critical — Japanese professors value thoroughness.

### Research Preparation
- [ ] Read 2–3 recent publications from the target professor's lab (last 2–3 years)
- [ ] Identify specific experiments or results you can reference in your email
- [ ] Note the beam facilities and detectors the lab currently uses (RIBF, SHARAQ, SAMURAI, etc.)
- [ ] Check if the professor has co-authored with any RIKEN researchers (strengthens the CNS–RIKEN connection angle)
- [ ] Review the lab's current student/postdoc roster on the CNS website to understand group size

### Your Materials
- [ ] CV updated with: education (IMP/CAS/UCAS), research experience (AFTPC framework, TPC, direct reactions), ML skills (ResNet, ViT, DANN, MCD), any publications or presentations
- [ ] Research-fit statement drafted (1 paragraph): how your TPC + direct reactions + ML-PID work at IMP connects to the professor's research at CNS/RIKEN
- [ ] Master's thesis abstract ready (brief, emphasizing methods transferable to their lab)
- [ ] TOEFL score or expected score noted (for Route A or GSGC requirements)
- [ ] **Dependency:** Complete [[#Step 0 — IMP Advisor Briefing]] above — warm introduction from your IMP advisor is the preferred outreach path

### Logistical Prep
- [ ] Confirm the professor's email format: `{username}@cns.s.u-tokyo.ac.jp` (see [[UTokyo_RIKEN_Professor_Outreach]] for usernames)
- [ ] Decide which professor to contact first (recommended: **Kentaro Yako** — strongest direct-reaction alignment)
- [ ] Prepare to send during Japan business hours (JST morning, ideally Tue–Thu)

## Email Draft Skeleton

> [!tip] Adapt the template for each professor. Two variants are provided: (1) **Warm-introduction follow-up** (preferred — use when your IMP advisor sends an intro first), and (2) **Cold-email fallback** (for professors your IMP advisor doesn't know). The cold-email example below targets **Prof. Kentaro Yako** (Priority 1).

> [!warning] Before sending, have someone review the email for tone and grammar. Japanese academic email conventions: be respectful, concise, and demonstrate you've done your homework. Do NOT attach files unless invited — mention willingness to share.

### Warm-Introduction Variant (Preferred Path)

> [!tip] If your IMP advisor sends an introduction email first, use this follow-up template instead. The warm intro has already established credibility — your email can be shorter and more direct.

```
Subject: Follow-up — Kenkyusei → PhD Inquiry (introduced by Prof. [IMP Advisor Name])

Dear Professor [Name],

My name is [Your Name], a master's student at the Institute of Modern 
Physics (IMP), Chinese Academy of Sciences. Professor [IMP Advisor Name] 
kindly introduced me and mentioned your group's research at CNS. I am 
writing to follow up on that introduction.

I am interested in joining your group as a Kenkyusei (Research Student) 
starting October 2027, with the goal of pursuing a PhD at the University 
of Tokyo. My current research at IMP focuses on TPC-based direct reactions 
and ML-driven particle identification (ResNet/ViT), which I believe 
connects well with your work at RIBF.

I would be very grateful for your guidance on two points:

1. Which doctoral admission route would you recommend for an international 
   student entering through Kenkyusei — the Regular Physics exam or GSGC?

2. Would there be opportunities for a PhD student in your group to apply 
   for RIKEN JRA funding?

I am happy to share my CV and research summary at your convenience. 
Thank you very much for your time.

Sincerely,
[Your Full Name]
[Your Email]
Institute of Modern Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences
```

### Cold-Email Fallback

> [!warning] Use this template for professors your IMP advisor doesn't know personally. The cold version needs to work harder to establish credibility — do NOT shorten it. The example targets **Prof. Kentaro Yako** (Priority 1).

```
Subject: Inquiry about Kenkyusei → PhD Opportunity at CNS (Nuclear Physics, IMP/CAS)

Dear Professor Yako,

My name is [Your Name], and I am a master's student in Particle and 
Nuclear Physics at the Institute of Modern Physics (IMP), Chinese 
Academy of Sciences, with an expected graduation in June 2027. I am 
writing to express my strong interest in joining your research group 
at the Center for Nuclear Study (CNS) as a Kenkyusei (Research Student) 
starting October 2027, with the goal of pursuing a PhD at the University 
of Tokyo.

My current research at IMP focuses on Time Projection Chambers (TPC) 
and direct reactions, where I developed AFTPC — an automated framework 
for TPC charged-particle track classification using deep learning 
(ResNet-18 and Vision Transformers). This work combines nuclear physics 
instrumentation with machine learning for particle identification, 
which I believe aligns well with your group's research on exotic nuclear 
reactions using RI beams at RIBF.

I have two questions I would be grateful for your guidance on:

1. For an international student entering through the Kenkyusei route, 
   would you recommend the Regular Physics doctoral exam or the GSGC 
   pathway? This would help me plan my preparation effectively.

2. Would there be opportunities for a PhD student in your group to 
   apply for RIKEN JRA funding?

I would be happy to share my CV and a summary of my research if you 
are interested. Thank you very much for your time and consideration.

Sincerely,
[Your Full Name]
[Your Email]
Institute of Modern Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences
```

> [!note] Customization points for other professors
> - **Taku Gunji:** Emphasize TPC upgrade (GEM) and ML-based tracking — the AFTPC method match is excellent even though physics focus differs (QGP vs direct reactions).
> - **Nobuaki Imai:** Emphasize detector development experience and the OEDO connection to instrumentation.
> - **Hidetoshi Yamaguchi:** Frame nuclear reactions broadly; mention that ML-PID methods are facility-agnostic.
> - **Yasuhiro Sakemi:** Only if top candidates don't respond. Frame as interest in precision experiments + data analysis techniques.

## Post-Contact Actions

### On Positive Response (within 1 week of receiving reply)

- [ ] Reply promptly (within 24–48 hours) thanking the professor
- [ ] Send CV and research summary if requested
- [ ] Update [[UTokyo_RIKEN_Professor_Outreach]] — change outreach status to "Replied — positive"
- [ ] Update [[UTokyo_RIKEN#Verification Tracker]] with any confirmed items (V2, V4, V5, V6, V7, V8)
- [ ] Apply the [[#Decision Gate Impact Matrix]] — update [[Fundamental_Learning_Progress]] based on Route A vs GSGC answer
- [ ] Update [[General Checklist]] — check off confirmed items
- [ ] If Naidaku (Letter of Acceptance) is offered → begin Kenkyusei application document prep
- [ ] Schedule follow-up questions (from the Medium-priority list) for the next email exchange
- [ ] If the warm-introduction path was not used for this professor, ask your IMP advisor if a follow-up endorsement email would be appropriate
- [ ] Keep your IMP advisor in the loop on all correspondence — your IMP advisor may offer to vouch for you in a follow-up if needed

### On No Response (after 2 weeks)

- [ ] Send a polite follow-up email (shorter, referencing original message, reiterating interest)
- [ ] Wait another 2 weeks after follow-up
- [ ] If still no response → move to next professor on the ranked list in [[UTokyo_RIKEN_Professor_Outreach]]
- [ ] Consider whether a different email approach is needed (e.g., shorter, different subject line)

### On Negative Response or Poor Fit

- [ ] Thank the professor graciously — do not burn bridges
- [ ] Ask if they can recommend a colleague at CNS who might be a better fit
- [ ] Move to next professor on the ranked list
- [ ] If all 5 UTokyo CNS professors exhausted without success:
  - [ ] Reassess research-fit statement — is the framing too narrow?
  - [ ] Activate [[Osaka_University]] backup pathway
  - [ ] Consider expanding search to other UTokyo departments (e.g., ICEPP for detector/ML work)

### Ongoing (regardless of outcome)

- [ ] Log every email sent/received with dates in [[UTokyo_RIKEN_Professor_Outreach]]
- [ ] Keep a decision journal: what you learned, what surprised you, what to adjust
- [ ] Continue physics study plan — do NOT pause studying while waiting for responses
