## My Core Thoughts (Decision Gates)

### Gate 1: What excites me?
The "blueprint and mystery" of astrophysics appeals to me more than pure nuclear structure. Imai's SAKURA program connects directly to where heavy elements come from. Even though the daily work is nuclear reactions at RIBF (not astrophysics modeling), the astrophysical motivation matters to me.

### Gate 2: Can I graduate?
UTokyo PhD likely requires PRL-level publications. A clear physics-measurement pipeline (reaction → cross-section → paper) is safer than a path heavier on instrumentation development. Imai's program has this pipeline with approved experiments already queued.

### Gate 3: Do I need to continue TPC/ML work?
No. I'm open to pivoting — my AFTPC + ML/PID experience was training, not a career commitment. I want to apply ML skills as analysis tools in whatever physics context I choose, not lock myself into TPC hardware.

### Gate 4: Does advisor rank matter?
Associate Professor is fine. An advisor with fewer students and active career-building incentive may give more mentoring attention. And since both groups are at UTokyo CNS on the same campus, Aoi's network remains accessible through joint experiments regardless.

### Gate 5: Peer support?
Having Chinese seniors who navigated the same path (immigration, JRA, UTokyo bureaucracy) is practically invaluable. Imai's group has this; Aoi's does not visibly.

## Critical Constraints

- **IMP advisor directive:** Contact ONE professor only. The Imai meeting is the commitment point, not a comparison step.
- **Cross-group collaboration:** Choosing Imai does NOT cut off access to Aoi or CAT-M. Both groups share OEDO-SHARAQ infrastructure and run joint experiments. Standard practice in Japanese nuclear physics labs.
