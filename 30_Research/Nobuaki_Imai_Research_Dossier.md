## 2. The DONUTS Group

**Website:** https://donuts.cns.s.u-tokyo.ac.jp/

The DONUTS group is Imai's research group at CNS. The exact acronym expansion was not found on any public page, but based on the research program (direct reactions, nuclear structure, transfer and scattering), it likely relates to **Direct reactions On NUclei using Transfer and Scattering** or similar. The group also has a Google Sites mirror at https://sites.google.com/cns.s.u-tokyo.ac.jp/cnsdonuts/.

### Research Program

The group studies nuclei as quantum many-body systems of protons and neutrons, from ground states near absolute zero to excited states in chaotic regimes. Two major pillars:

#### Pillar 1: Self-Organization in Finite Quantum Many-Body Systems
- **Deformation coexistence:** Quantum phase transitions where nuclear ground states shift from spherical to deformed configurations (Zr, Mg isotopes)
- **Novel collective motions:** Pairing correlations in neutron-rich nuclei; connections to neutron star physics; two-proton decay
- **Extreme rotation states:** Hyperdeformed nuclei under extreme angular momentum; toroidal nuclear configurations

#### Pillar 2: Nuclear Reaction Dynamics & Astrophysics
- **Island of stability:** Superheavy nuclei (Z > 100), production mechanisms, predicted long-lived species
- **Heavy element origins (r-process):** Neutron capture cross-sections in neutron-rich nuclei for r-process nucleosynthesis in neutron star mergers
- **Nuclear transmutation:** Data for converting long-lived radioactive waste (LLFP) into stable isotopes

### Key Sub-Projects
- **SAKURA** (Study of Astrophysical Key reactions in the Universe with the low-energy RI beam Apparatus) — uses OEDO-SHARAQ for r-process and vp-process nucleosynthesis
- **OEDO-SHARAQ** international collaboration
- **GRAPE** detector array (position-sensitive germanium detectors)
- **Tsinghua-UTokyo joint research program**

## 3. The OEDO Beamline & OEDO-SHARAQ System

### What is OEDO?

**OEDO = Optimized Energy Degrading Optics for RI beams**

OEDO is an upgrade of the High-resolution beamline at RIKEN's RI Beam Factory (RIBF), designed to produce slowed-down, well-focused radioactive-isotope (RI) beams at 10–50 MeV/nucleon.

**Prof. Imai is the scientific coordinator of OEDO.** Researchers must contact him before preparing experimental proposals.

### How It Works

The OEDO system uses three key components:
1. **Two superconducting quadrupole triplet magnets** — for beam focusing
2. **One radio-frequency (RF) deflector** — exploits longitudinal phase-space correlations to compress horizontal beam spots
3. **Angle-tunable wedge energy degrader** — slows the beam to the desired energy

The system takes fast RI beams produced by BigRIPS (at ~200 MeV/u) and decelerates them to 10–50 MeV/u while maintaining beam quality. This enables low-energy nuclear reaction studies (transfer reactions, fusion reactions) with exotic nuclei that cannot be produced as targets.

### Performance
- Construction completed: March 2017; first beam: June 2017
- Beam species demonstrated: 107Pd, 93Zr, 77,79Se at 20–30 MeV/u
- Transmission: ~18% to secondary target (S0) under optimal conditions
- Momentum acceptance at F1: +/-2%
- Spot size at F3: <10 mm diameter

### OEDO-SHARAQ Combined System
The OEDO beamline feeds into the SHARAQ high-resolution magnetic spectrometer (resolving power dP/P ~ 1/15000, constructed 2009). Together, OEDO-SHARAQ is "the world's first beamline characterized by energy-degrading of RI beams with a followed magnetic spectrometer for fragment analysis."

This enables two modes of operation:
1. **Low-energy RI reaction measurements** (transfer, fusion, surrogate reactions)
2. **Intermediate-energy high-resolution spectroscopy** (mass measurements, giant resonances)

## 5. The 2024 PTEP Paper on Double Gamow-Teller Giant Resonance

> M. Sasano, K. Yako, S. Ota, ... N. Imai, ... T. Uesaka, "Candidate for the Double Gamow-Teller Giant Resonance in 48Ca Studied by the (12C, 12Be(0+2)) Reaction at 250 MeV/Nucleon," *Prog. Theor. Exp. Phys.* 2024(12), 123D03 (2024).
> - DOI: 10.1093/ptep/ptae174
> - [Oxford Academic](https://academic.oup.com/ptep/article/2024/12/123D03/7927568)

### Experimental Setup
- **Reaction:** 12C + 48Ca -> 12Be(0+2) + 48Ti at 250 MeV/nucleon
- **Facility:** RIKEN RI Beam Factory, using 12C primary beam
- **Key technique:** Double charge-exchange reaction; events with double isospin- and spin-flip selected by measuring decay gamma-rays from 12Be(0+2)
- **Resolution:** Energy resolution 1.6 MeV; angular resolution 0.17 degrees
- **Spectrometer:** SHARAQ used for high-resolution particle identification

### Key Findings
- First application of the (12C, 12Be(0+2)) double charge-exchange reaction to search for the double Gamow-Teller giant resonance (DGGTR)
- Observed a **forward-peaking component** in double-differential cross sections
- Integrated 0-degree cross section: **1.33 +/- 0.12 microbarn/sr** in the excitation energy region below 34 MeV in 48Ti
- DWBA + multipole decomposition analysis indicates **~40% of the forward-peaking component originates from double Gamow-Teller transitions**
- This is a "candidate" observation — the DGGTR has never been conclusively identified before
- **Significance:** Connects to neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element calculations

### Imai's Role
N. Imai is listed as a co-author. The experiment used the SHARAQ spectrometer which is part of the OEDO-SHARAQ system that Imai coordinates. The lead authors are from the Yako group (K. Yako is also at CNS) and RIKEN.

## 6. The 2023 PTEP Paper on Dual Gain GEM

> C. Iwamoto, S. Ota, R. Kojima, ... N. Imai, ... E. Takada, "Performance of prototype Dual Gain Multilayer Thick GEM with high-intensity heavy-ion beam injections in low-pressure hydrogen gas," *Prog. Theor. Exp. Phys.* 2023(8), 083H01 (2023).
> - DOI: 10.1093/ptep/ptad093
> - [Oxford Academic](https://academic.oup.com/ptep/article/2023/8/083H01/7223477)
> - [arXiv: 2305.07680](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.07680)

### What Was Developed
A prototype **Dual Gain Multilayer Thick Gas Electron Multiplier (DG-M-THGEM)** with 10 cm x 10 cm active area, featuring an alternating structure of electrodes and insulators with separately controlled gain regions.

### Key Results
- **Beam region:** Effective gas gain < 100 with 3% charge resolution (handles intense primary beam without saturation)
- **Recoil region:** Effective gas gain of 2,000 (high sensitivity for detecting low-energy recoil particles)
- **High-intensity stability:** Gain shift suppressed within 3% at beam intensities up to 2.5 x 10^6 particles per second
- **Test conditions:** Low-pressure hydrogen gas (40 kPa), 132Xe beam irradiation

### Purpose
Designed for a **large-volume active-target time projection chamber (AT-TPC)** that can operate under high-intensity heavy-ion beam injections. The dual-gain concept solves the fundamental problem that beam particles and low-energy recoil particles require very different detector gains.

### Imai's Contribution
Imai is one of 22 co-authors. This is not ALICE TPC-related per se; rather, it builds on GEM/THGEM technology that CNS has developed, which has been adopted by experiments including ALICE at CERN. CNS's detector R&D page notes their "GEM-based TPC: Thick gas-electron-multiplier technology adopted globally, including CERN's ALICE collaboration."

## 8. Summary of Research Program — What Imai's Group Actually Does

Prof. Imai's research program centers on **using decelerated radioactive-isotope beams at RIKEN RIBF to study nuclear structure and nuclear astrophysics**. The unique capability is the OEDO-SHARAQ system he coordinates, which is the world's only beamline that decelerates RI beams and feeds them into a high-resolution spectrometer.

### Three Main Research Directions

1. **Nuclear astrophysics (r-process):** Measuring neutron capture cross-sections of neutron-rich nuclei (79Se, 130Sn) via surrogate (d,p) reactions to understand heavy element formation in neutron star mergers. This is the **SAKURA project**.

2. **Nuclear waste transmutation:** Using the same surrogate reaction technique to determine neutron capture and spallation cross-sections of long-lived fission products (79Se, 93Zr, 107Pd) for designing nuclear waste treatment facilities.

3. **Nuclear structure far from stability:** Studying deformation, shell evolution, and collective motion in exotic nuclei through direct reactions, fusion reactions, and spectroscopy with RI beams.

### Why This Matters for a PhD Student
- The OEDO-SHARAQ system is a **world-unique facility** — no other place can do exactly this
- Research spans both fundamental physics (r-process, nuclear structure) and applied physics (nuclear waste)
- Students gain hands-on experience with cutting-edge detector development
- International collaboration network (RIKEN, RCNP Osaka, KEK, Tsinghua, TRIUMF, GSI)
- RIKEN JRA positions available for funding (current student J.T. Li has JRA status)
- Recent PhD completion: J.T. Li defended thesis on SHARAQ19 (January 2026)
